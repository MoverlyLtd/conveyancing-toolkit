## 3.1.5 What other documents are acceptable for verifying identity?

If you are dealing face to face, you need to provide one item from list A (photographic) OR one item from list B and one item from list C. If the documentation is sent by post, then two items will be required, one of which must be from list A or B.
LIST A - Evidence of Identity (Government-issued document with photograph) incorporating
full name, either residential address or date of birth, such as:-
ï?? Valid signed passport
ï?? Valid photocard Driving Licence (full or provisional)
ï?? National Identity card (non-UK nationals)
ï?? Firearms Certificate or shotgun licence
ï?? Identity card issued for voters in Northern Ireland
LIST B Evidence of Identity (Government-issued document without photograph) incorporating
full name, either residential address or date of birth, such as:-
ï?? Valid old style full driving licence
ï?? Recent evidence of entitlement to a state or local authority-funded benefit, e.g. i) Pink card BR464; ii) Original notification letter from the Benefits Agency confirming the right to benefits
ï?? Inland Revenue Tax Notification (including Coding Notices/Self Assessment Statement of Account)
LIST C Evidence of address and other acceptable identity documents:
ï?? Current council tax demand letter or statement
ï?? Recent evidence of Recent Utility Bill or Certification from a supplier of utilities confirming arrangements to pay for services on pre-payment terms. We cannot accept mobile phone or internet bills
ï?? Pink card BR464 (if not used for List B), Original notification letter from the Benefits Agency confirming the right to benefits (if not used for List B)
ï?? Current Bank or Building Society passbook or recent statement containing current address
ï?? Most recent original mortgage statement from a recognised lender.
ï?? Local Council rent card or tenancy agreement
ï?? Recent Credit Card statement (Visa/Mastercard/American Express)
ï?? Recent instrument of a court appointment

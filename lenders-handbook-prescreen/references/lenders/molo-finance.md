## 5.4.6 Does the lender accept search insurance and, if yes, what are the lender's specific requirements?

For Buy to Let mortgages, the lender can accept Search Insurance and Lender’s Title Insurance (Lender's Title Insurance must be arranged through Lime Risk Agency Limited (Known as Lime Risk).
Search insurance is acceptable for remortgages of single self-contained units, or where the borrower is transferring ownership from a personal name into a company, provided at least one of the original individuals remains involved in the transaction. This insurance is only acceptable in respect of local authority searches and must fully protect our interests, including title, without limitation.
In such cases, the conveyancer must be satisfied that there are no restrictions or limitations on the title, it must be marketable and must provide an unqualified Certificate of Title. Where a local search cannot be obtained, delayed search insurance may be used if the conveyancer confirms that their advice and certification of title remain unaffected. On a case-by-case basis, we may accept appropriate no search indemnity and/or search delay or expiry insurance, subject to the same conditions.
Search insurance is not accepted for:
- Houses in multiple occupation (HMOs).
- Multi-unit buildings.
- Remortgages of properties currently owned mortgage-free.
- Semi-Commercial properties.
Lender’s Title Insurance (Lender's Title Insurance must be arranged through Lime Risk Agency Limited (Known as Lime Risk), subject to the insurer’s and lender's criteria and terms being met. This covers purchases, remortgage transactions, including new build properties.
What property searches are included within Lime Risk's coverage?
· A search in Form LLC1 of the Register of Local Land Charges with a full set of enquiries of the appropriate local authority in Form Con 29 or any official forms in substitution thereof
· An enquiry to the relevant sewerage or water undertaker in respect of sewerage or water matters contained in Form Con 29DW or any official form in substitution thereof
· An enquiry of the Coal Authority in respect coal mining matters contained in Form Con 29M or any official form in substitution thereof
· Any matter in a commons registration, Environment Agency, Cheshire brine, tin, limestone or other mining search
· A chancel repairs search
· An environmental search
. A highways search
What is excluded from Lime Risk's Lender's Title Insurance coverage?:
· Any matters or defects in title which are known prior to the loan origination date
· Standalone flood searches
How can you utilise Lime Risk's Lender's Title Insurance cover?
· Adhere to the underwriting criteria
· Submit a monthly declaration [Return] to Lime Risk Agency by the 14th day post month end of all originations for the previous month.
· Lime Risk Agency will provide an invoice to the completing solicitor following submission of the monthly declaration, which needs to be paid within [7] days of receipt.
· Advise Lime Risk Agency if there are no cases to declare at the end of the month so it can be entered as a nil return for that particular month.
To be eligible for Lenders Title Insurance, you must:
· Adhere to the underwriting criteria contained within the policy
· The loan amount must be equal to or less than £750,000, including any capitalised fees
· No property searches have been undertaken (as mentioned above)
· A full title interrogation has not been conducted
· The property must be in England and or Wales
When is Lender's Title Insurance not eligible?
· Mortgage lending, including any capitalised fees, exceeds £750,000
· The application falls under an HMO, MUFB or has any element of Commercial lending.
· If any of the searches detailed above have been conducted, this loan is not suitable for title insurance.
. When the policy is not taken out through Lime Risk.
Can I refer cases to Lime Risk that do not meet the above to see if my client is eligible (excluding the value limit and property restrictions)?
Yes, all referrals should be sent to referrals@limeriskagency.com or Matt.chappell@limeriskagency.com (A response will be given within 48 hours)

## 5.14.17b Does the lender have any specific instructions about building safety?

You do not need to comply with this section if the building is not a “relevant building” under the Building Safety Act 2022 (“the Act”).
We will lend where the EWS1 form gives the property a rating of A1, A2 or B1. We will not lend where the EWS1 form gives the property any other rating or where a PAS 9980 assessment highlights the need for remedial work, unless the block is on the Government's approved list for remediation works, in which case you must report this to us immediately for consideration.
If you are made aware during the conveyancing process that the valuer’s assumptions regarding any remediation works under the Building Safety Act 2022 are incorrect or the security property has been identified as being in need of remediation and there is no relevant information on this point within the valuation, you must report this to us immediately for consideration. You must do so at the earliest opportunity.
If the mortgage property is in a block of flats, you must obtain a copy of the most recent Fire Risk Assessment (“FRA”). We will not lend where the block is required to have an FRA but does not or where the FRA is not in date. You must also obtain confirmation that any recommended works have been completed or whether there are any outstanding recommended works. If there are any outstanding recommended works this must be reported to us along with the detail and timing of those works. Please do not send us a copy of the FRA.
You must also report to us if any works are likely to have a significant impact upon the service charge or may result or have resulted in a notice under s20 Landlord and Tenant Act 1985.
You must tell us as soon as you become aware if the current owner of the property is a non - qualifying leaseholder.
You must not complete the mortgage until you have received our further written instructions. We recommend that you report such matters before exchange of contracts because we may have to withdraw or change the mortgage offer.
Where an EWS1 has expired, this must be reported immediately for consideration.

## 5.14.1 What minimum unexpired lease term does the lender accept?

The lease must have a minimum of 70 years remaining at the time of completion, and 55 years remaining at the end of the mortgage term.
Completion is defined as the commencement of the mortgage term.
Where the mortgage protection clause is absent, the acting solicitors must seek to have the lease amended so that it provides adequate protection for the Lender and secures the Lender’s interest in the property.
If the necessary amendment cannot be reasonably or practically obtained and the lease does not include equivalent protections for the lender, the solicitor must notify the Lender without delay. Such notification must clearly set out: – the protections that are missing;
– the steps taken to remedy or negotiate amendments;
– what mitigating measures have been put in place; and
– Are there any Service charge arrears or pending litigation regarding the management company/specific property; and
– how, in the solicitors’ view, the Lender’s interest remains protected.
The matter will then be reviewed by the Lender, and no completion should take place until the Lender has confirmed how it wishes to proceed."

Molo / ColCap UK will allow ad-hoc warranty providers to be considered on a case by case basis, as long as:
- The warranty (Insurer) must cover the building structure and all communal areas (for flats) and provide adequate coverage to fully complete or partially rebuild the property in the event of a building defect or structural failure. The warranty must have a minimum term of 10 years, with a maximum claim amount equal to or greater than the total cost of rebuilding the property. It must meet the minimum rating of A.M.Best(B), Standard & Poor’s(A-), Moody’s(A3) or Fitch Ratings(A-) and be based in the UK. The building should have been inspected by a qualified professional, and the warranty must be transferable to any subsequent owner or buyer within its validity period.
Retrospective warranties can be considered on a case by case basis when it meets at least one of the scenarios below:
- The existing provider has gone into liquidation.
- The new provider is using the same Insurer, and they confirmed they visited the site during the build.
- The new provider was changed during the build, and the new Insurer is comfortable with the overall building standards.
In addition, it must also meet the minimum requirements as noted above.

## 5.8.1 Does the lender accept security which comprises a building converted into not more than four flats where the borrower occupies one of those flats and the borrower or another flat owner also owns the freehold of the building and the other flats are subject to long leases?

Fleet only provides mortgages on a buy to let basis and so does not lend in situations where the borrower or a relative* is to occupy the mortgage property.
When lending against leasehold, Fleet will also require a charge over the freehold where the entire freehold is ultimately owned or controlled by:
• the borrower or their relative*;
• a company or other entity over which the borrower or their relative* can exercise control; or
• a combination of the above,
* where the borrower is a company or partnership, this includes a director, shareholder, guarantor or partner of the borrower or anyone related to such person.
Please refer to the mortgage offer and conditions for further details regarding these requirements.

## 5.8.5 Does the lender accept security which comprises one of two leasehold flats in a building where the borrower also owns the freehold reversion of the other flat and the other leaseholder owns the freehold reversion in the borrower's flat?  If so, are there any specific requirements?

Yes, in the following circumstances:
a) where the repairing obligation is divided between the two units split at first floor joist level;
b) there is a remaining term on the lease of at least 85 years subject to a nominal (peppercorn) ground rent;
c) the freehold must also be charged to the lender;
d) the freehold of neither flat can be transferred without the lease also being transferred to the same owner; and
e) access including maintenance and legal arrangements relating to the property and yard are correctly established.

## 5.14.17b Does the lender have any specific instructions about building safety?

Fire Risk Assessment:
If the mortgage property is in a block of flats, you must obtain confirmation that a current Fire Risk Assessment (FRA) is in place. We will not lend where an FRA is required but not available or out of date. You must also confirm whether the FRA identifies any recommended works assessed as presenting a ‘substantial’ or ‘intolerable’ risk, or a high likelihood of extreme harm, and if so you must report these to us together with details and timescales, including where such works may significantly impact service charges or give rise to a section 20 notice; please do not send us the FRA itself.
EWS1 Forms:
Where the building was completed on or after 1 January 2020, an EWS1 form is not required. For buildings completed before this date, where the valuation report indicates that an EWS1 form is required, this should be passed to Fleet for review.
If, during conveyancing, you become aware that the valuer’s assumptions regarding Building Safety Act 2022 remediation liabilities are incorrect, or that the property has been identified as requiring remediation, you must report this to us immediately as we will not proceed.

## 5.14.9 If different from 1.11, contact point for matters connected with the lease:

See 1.11a.
1. Where ground rent provisions cause (or, during the term of the mortgage, are likely to cause) the lease to be treated as an Assured Tenancy under the Housing Act 1988, this is no longer applicable following legislative changes introduced by the Renters’ Rights Act 2025. From 27 December 2025, any lease with a fixed term of more than 21 years cannot become an assured tenancy, regardless of ground rent amount. Accordingly, you do not need to report ground rent levels for this purpose.
2. You are still required to report any onerous ground rent provisions to us. Onerous provisions would include, but are not limited to:
a) Provisions which allow for ground rents to double after fixed periods of less than 25 years;
b) Ground rents calculated by reference to the capital value of the mortgage property from time to time (whereupon we will expect to be given full details of the method of calculating a new capital value, who bears the costs associated with the process and how any disputes are ultimately resolved);
c) Provisions allowing for fixed increases by reference to an inflation-linked index, but which are clearly above inflation expectations.
We expect any leases granted after 22 June 2022 to be regulated leases under the Leasehold Reform (Ground Rent) Act 2022 and for any lease extensions granted after that date to also comply with that Act.

RENT CHARGES
We expect our conveyancers to make enquiries to discover whether or not the property is subject to rent charges.
Where the property is subject to a rent charge, an indemnity policy will be acceptable where the charge relates to an historic rent charge where there are no services being provided and the rent has not been collected for 12 years or more or where the rent has been collected within the last 12 years but the owner of the rent charge cannot, after reasonable enquiries, be located.
If an estate rent charge is payable on a property, this will be acceptable on the basis one of the following can be satisfied: ;
a) The terms of the estate rent charge specifically exclude the provisions of section 121 of the Law of Property Act 1925; or
b) The terms of the estate rent charge include a mortgagee protection clause which states that at least 28 days’ notice must be given to any mortgagee prior to any action for non-payment or other enforcement action being taken (including the exercise of the remedies contained in section 121 of the Law of Property Act 1925) by the owner of the estate rent charge; or
c) The owner of the estate rent charge is a management company owned by the residents, who are the shareholders of a private freehold development.
If condition b) (above) is satisfied, you should also consider whether a suitable indemnity policy should be taken out to further protect our interests.

## 6.6.3 Does the lender require counterpart/certified copy tenancy agreement to be sent to you?

No, we do not require any tenancy agreements to be sent to us.
However, you must ensure that any tenancy agreement meets the criteria set out below:
Acceptable Tenancies:
• Assured Shorthold Tenancies (ASTs), or in Wales, Standard Occupation Contracts (SOCs) with a term not exceeding 36 months and total rental income below £100k per annum.
• Non-assured tenancy agreements:
1. Corporate Tenancies of wholly residential property, including to Local Authorities/Housing Associations/Registered Social Housing Providers/Charities (but excluding tenancies to organisations which provide care or other services to occupants on the premises)
2. Shared Households/Student Lets (provided the property is not on a student campus) - tenancies may be in individual names or a group of names but the number of tenancies must be consistent with the size of the property
Meeting the following criteria:
o Maximum 5 year term
o Tenant must not have option to renew or be granted security of tenure
o Use of the property must be restricted to residential purposes only with business use prohibited.
o Prohibition on assignment/subletting, except for Corporate Tenancies where the tenant is granting an AST/SOC/licence to the end user and is not remaining in occupation or control.
For any tenancy agreement over 12 months, the landlord must have an unconditional right to terminate (at least after the first 12 months) or the right to terminate upon the appointment of a receiver.
Properties must not be used as holiday lets (including Airbnb or similar) or short-term lets. Fleet does not accept protected or assured tenancies, tenancies granted to diplomats/diplomatic missions or agricultural tenancies.
Where a tenancy agreement contains an unconditional right for the property to be converted into an HMO, you must ensure the loan is documented under an HMO product.
The agreement must contain no other provisions which adversely affect the lender’s security or its right to enforce its remedies under the mortgage deed executed by the borrower.

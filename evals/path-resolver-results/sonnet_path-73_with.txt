Yes, for items the seller intends to remove that would normally stay with the property, use:

**`/propertyPack/fixturesAndFittings/itemsToRemove`**

This covers fixtures and fittings the seller plans to take that a buyer might reasonably expect to remain, such as:

- Built-in appliances (fitted dishwashers, ovens, hobs)
- Light fittings and chandeliers
- Fitted wardrobes or shelving
- Garden features (plants, decorative items)
- Security systems
- Curtain poles and blinds
- Bathroom fixtures (towel rails, mirrors)

The PDTF schema distinguishes between:
- **`/propertyPack/fixturesAndFittings`** — general included/excluded items
- **`/propertyPack/fixturesAndFittings/itemsToRemove`** — specifically items the seller intends to remove that might be considered fixtures

This helps avoid completion day disputes by clearly identifying what the seller is taking that buyers might otherwise assume would stay.

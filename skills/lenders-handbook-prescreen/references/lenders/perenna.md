## 4.5b Where should the certificate of title be sent?

All Certificates of Title must be submitted in electronic form using the Lender Exchange Portal. Certificates of Title submitted via any other method or in any other form will not be accepted.
You should not submit your Certificate of Title without first confirming with your customer that all pre-completion queries have been resolved.
We require your Certificate of Title to be submitted with no less than 5 full working days’ notice. For example, if you submit a Certificate of Title on a Monday, the earliest completion day would be the following Monday
PLEASE NOTE: Where less than 5 full working days is required for completion an exception process must be followed. Contact must be made to the Mortgage team by either telephone: 0330 341 2013 or Email: conveyancing@perenna.co.uk advising the nature of the exception required and rationale.

## 5.7.1a Does the lender lend on flying freeholds?

Yes, providing the element of flying freehold does not exceed 20% and subject to confirmation that adequate rights of support and mutually enforceable repairing covenants exist.

## 5.7.1b Does the lender lend on freehold flats?

Not normally acceptable. However, the following circumstances are acceptable: -
o There is a leasehold interest in the flat and the reference to freehold relates to a share of the freehold interest in the block;
o The other flat(s) in the block are all leasehold; or
o There are reciprocating lease/freehold arrangements (such as Tyneside Flat situation).
For any other cases or where clarification is required, please contact our Mortgage team by either telephone: 0330 341 2013 or Email: conveyancing@perenna.co.uk

## 5.7.1c If the lender is prepared to accept a title falling within 5.7 and the property is a freehold flat or flying freehold, to which contact point must this be reported?

There is no need to report to the lender providing the conditions listed within 5.7 can be met.
For any other cases or where clarification is required, please contact our Mortgage team by either telephone: 0330 341 2013 or Email: conveyancing@perenna.co.uk

This applies equally to new mortgages and remortgages. Applicants for remortgages who have an existing charge securing a gifted deposit must redeem the charge upon completion of their remortgage.
A back-to-back purchase occurs where a Perenna customer is purchasing from a
third party who has purchased the property within the last 6 months at a
discounted/lower purchase price.
A back-to-back remortgage occurs when an applicant purchases or exchanges
contracts at a given price, and then attempts to undertake a re-mortgage with a
lender at an enhanced current value, within 6 months of the original transaction.
Perenna will only lend on back-to-back transactions by exception. Typical
exceptions for a back-to-back transaction would be scenarios such as probate or
purchase/sale by a developer.

## 5.20.3 Does the lender have additional requirements relating to leases of roof space for solar PV panels, and if so, what are they?

We will consider properties with solar panels where the applicant(s) are funding the
purchase of panels themselves or via additional borrowing, providing they are doing so without creating any long-term lease arrangement with the panel provider.
Solar panels will be acceptable in the following circumstances:
o Where the solar panels are bought outright.
o We will not lend on a property where the panel provider is supplying and fitting panels free of charge, is taking income from the grid tariff scheme, and is creating a long-term lease against the roof and roof air space.
However, where an adequate break clause (of 6 months or less) within the lease is present Perenna will consider the acceptability if there is no impact to resale ability.

## 6.4.4 Does the lender require me to report incentives?

Yes, unless it is:
o A builder or vendor cash/financial incentive in relation to the purchase of a new build property and the value of cash incentives is no greater than 5% of the lower of the purchase price or valuation stated in the offer and the loan amount (excluding fees added to the mortgage) is no greater than 90% of the lower of the purchase price or valuation stated in the offer; or
o A builder or vendor non-cash/non-financial incentive for fixtures, fittings, or white goods in relation to the purchase of a new build property.
Cash/financial incentives include but are not limited to cashback deals, stamp duty / land and buildings tax contributions, payment of fees to professionals (e.g., solicitors, surveyors), rental payment guarantees, gifted deposits.
Non-cash/non-financial incentive for fixtures, fittings or white goods include but are not limited to carpets, curtains, kitchen upgrades, bathroom upgrades, electrical upgrades, turf, landscaping.

## 10.2a Will the mortgage advance be paid electronically or by cheque?

The mortgage advance will usually be paid by Perenna 1 working day before the expected completion date on the Certificate of Title. The funds will be disbursed electronically using FPS (Faster Payment Service). It is expected that funds will reach the recipient bank account immediately but in rare cases this could take 2 – 24 hours.
Where less than 5 working days', notice has been provided funds may, by exception, be sent on the day of completion due to insufficient processing time. The solicitor will be kept informed of this by the Bank.

## 10.2b What is the minimum number of days notice lenders require?

Operational processes require 5 full working days' notice from solicitors wishing to complete on the loan however in exceptional circumstances we will facilitate quicker completions to avoid customer detriment or where Perenna is at fault for any delays.
PLEASE NOTE: Where less than 5 full working days is required for completion an exception process must be followed. Contact must be made to the Mortgage team by either telephone: 0330 341 2013 or Email: conveyancing@perenna.co.uk advising the nature of the exception required and rationale.

## 10.3 What are the standard deductions made from the mortgage advance?

Funds transfer fee (FPS (Faster Payment Service)) and other fees as per the Mortgage Offer.

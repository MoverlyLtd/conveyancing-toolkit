## 5.4.6 Does the lender accept search insurance and, if yes, what are the lender's specific requirements?

Search insurance is only acceptable for a remortgage, with one exception that this acceptable on a "re bridge". We will accept search indemnity insurance at the solicitor’s own risk provided this covers the full loan amount, that the solicitors are satisfied the policy adequately protects us as mortgagee and that the insurance policy covers our successors and assigns in title without limitation. The solicitor must provide an unqualified Certificate of Title.

## 5.14.9 If different from 1.11, contact point for matters connected with the lease:

As per 1.7
a. Any ground rent at the start of the mortgage term should not exceed annually £250 outside of London or a £1,000 inside London. If this is the case you must immediately contact the Society. We will advise you if our mortgage offer remains valid.
b. Ground rent and other event fees must be reasonable at all times during the lease term. For example, it is acceptable for ground rent escalation to be linked to RPI (Retail Price Index) or a similar index and where this is the case we do not need to be advised. However, unreasonable multipliers of ground rent will not be permitted, for example, doubling every 5, 10 or 15 years. These must be referred to the Society and we will advise you if our mortgage offer remains valid. If you are unsure as to whether the terms of a lease are unreasonable, please refer the details to the Society immediately.

## 5.20.3 Does the lender have additional requirements relating to leases of roof space for solar PV panels, and if so, what are they?

If the property is subject to a registered lease of roof space for solar pv panels we require you to confirm that the lease contains a valid mortgagee break right which is capable of exercising by us in the event of a repossession.
Where the property is leasehold confirmation must be obtained from the landlord that they have provided their consent to the installation of the equipment.
Confirmation from the borrower that they have advised their buildings insurance company of the installation of the equipment.

## 6.7.1 What new home warranty schemes are acceptable to the lender?

Properties built within the previous 10 years provided they have the benefit of an NHBC Certificate, Premier Guarantee, Building Life Plan, LABC New Home Warranty or an Architect’s or Chartered Surveyor’s Certificate. If such certificates are not available, loans of up to 33.33% only may be made subject to receipt of a satisfactory building survey report and other criteria being met. Warranties from providers not on the approved list can be accepted subject to the agreement of the Head of Mortgages or Credit Committee.

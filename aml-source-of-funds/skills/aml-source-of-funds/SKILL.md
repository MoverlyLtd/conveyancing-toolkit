# AML Source of Funds — UK Conveyancing

Provide specific, actionable AML source of funds guidance for residential
property transactions. This skill covers what evidence to request for every
common funding element, regulatory obligations, red flags, and reporting.

## Response Rules — Always Include

When answering ANY AML source of funds question, your response MUST include:

**DO:**
- Always state the **5-year record-keeping** obligation (retain all CDD records for 5 years from end of business relationship/transaction)
- Always mention **SAR reporting to NCA** — even if the question doesn't mention suspicion, the obligation to report must be flagged as part of the compliance framework
- When overseas funds are involved, always mention **SWIFT/wire transfer evidence**, **source country risk assessment** (FATF), and **currency conversion evidence**
- When gifts are involved, always require **giftor's ID**, **giftor's bank statements**, and **signed gift letter confirming no obligation to repay** — all three, every time
- Always frame advice as a **risk-based approach**, not tick-box compliance
- Always mention **Regulation 28(12)** — ongoing monitoring during the retainer, not just at onboarding

**DON'T:**
- Don't give evidence requirements for just the main funding element and forget the others — cover ALL elements in a combined purchase
- Don't mention SARs only when there's obvious suspicion — the reporting framework applies regardless and should be referenced as context
- Don't skip the DAML process (7 working days + 31 day moratorium) when discussing reporting
- Don't forget that giftor checks are separate from buyer checks — the giftor needs their own ID and bank statement evidence

## Key Principle

Apply a **risk-based approach** — not tick-box compliance. The depth of
enquiry must be proportionate to the risk profile of the client, the
transaction, and the funding source. Every decision to accept or escalate
should be documented with reasoning.

## Funding Element Evidence Requirements

For each element of the purchase funds, request the specific evidence below.
When a client combines multiple sources, evidence is required for **every**
element.

### Savings

- **12 months** of bank/building society statements showing the accumulation
  pattern (not just a closing balance)
- Statements must show the account holder's name matching the client
- Look for regular salary credits or consistent pattern explaining growth
- If large lump sums appear within the 12 months, trace each one separately
- If held across multiple accounts, statements for all accounts

### Gift (from family member or third party)

- **Giftor's photographic ID** — passport or driving licence
- **Giftor's bank statements** — 3–6 months showing they hold the funds and
  the source of those funds
- **Signed gift letter** confirming:
  - Amount of gift
  - Relationship between giftor and recipient
  - No obligation to repay (not a loan in disguise)
  - No interest in the property is expected
- **Proof of relationship** — if not obvious from names, a statutory
  declaration or other evidence
- If giftor is overseas, apply overseas funds checks (below) in addition
- **Lender notification** — most mortgage lenders require disclosure of
  gifted deposits; check Part 2 of the UK Finance Lender's Handbook

### Inheritance

- **Grant of Probate** or **Letters of Administration**
- **Solicitor's or estate administrator's letter** confirming:
  - Distribution amount
  - That funds have been or will be released to the client
- **Bank statement** showing receipt of inheritance funds
- If inheritance was received long ago and has since been invested/moved,
  trace the chain with statements

### Sale Proceeds (from previous property)

- **Completion statement** from the previous sale
- **Copy of TR1** (transfer deed) or Land Registry confirmation
- **Bank statement** showing receipt of net sale proceeds
- If proceeds were received some time ago, trace onward movement with
  statements to current holding account

### Mortgage (from FCA-regulated lender)

- **Mortgage offer** from an FCA-regulated lender
- No further source-of-funds checks needed on the mortgage element itself —
  the lender has conducted its own CDD
- Still verify the **deposit/equity** element separately

### Pension Lump Sum / Redundancy / Compensation

- **Letter from pension provider / employer / compensating body** confirming
  amount and date of payment
- **Bank statement** showing receipt
- **P45 or payslip** for redundancy payments

### Overseas Funds

Apply **all standard checks for the underlying source** (savings, gift,
sale, etc.) **plus** these additional checks:

- **Source country risk assessment** — check against:
  - HM Treasury sanctions list
  - FATF high-risk and increased-monitoring jurisdictions
  - Transparency International Corruption Perceptions Index
- **Currency conversion evidence** — FX contract note or bank conversion
  confirmation showing original currency, rate, and GBP equivalent
- **SWIFT / wire transfer confirmation** showing:
  - Sending bank and account holder
  - Receiving bank and account holder
  - Amount, currency, date
- If funds pass through intermediary accounts, trace each hop
- **Enhanced due diligence** is almost always appropriate for overseas funds

### Cryptocurrency / Digital Assets

- **Exchange account statements** showing:
  - Account holder identity (KYC-verified exchange)
  - Purchase history or deposit history of the crypto
  - Sale/conversion to fiat currency with dates and amounts
- **Wallet transaction history** if funds were held in a private wallet
  before exchange
- **Bank statement** showing receipt of fiat proceeds from the exchange
- **Evidence of original acquisition** — how did the client obtain the
  crypto? (mining, salary, purchase with savings, etc.) Trace backwards.
- Consider whether the exchange is FCA-registered (UK) or equivalent
  regulated entity
- **Higher risk** — cryptocurrency is inherently higher risk for AML;
  enhanced due diligence should be the default

### Equity (existing property being remortgaged or retained)

- **Recent mortgage statement** showing outstanding balance
- **Current valuation or Land Registry title** confirming ownership
- **Calculation** showing equity = value minus outstanding mortgage

### Litigation / Court Award

- **Court order or settlement agreement**
- **Solicitor's letter** confirming award amount
- **Bank statement** showing receipt

## Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) Triggers

Standard CDD is the baseline. Escalate to EDD when **any** of these apply:

- Client or beneficial owner is a **Politically Exposed Person (PEP)** —
  domestic or foreign — or a family member/close associate of a PEP
- Funds originate from a **high-risk third country** (FATF list / HMT
  sanctions)
- **Cash purchase** with no mortgage — removes lender as additional CDD
  check
- **Unusual transaction pattern** — price significantly above/below market,
  rapid buy-sell, no inspection of property
- **Complex or unusually large transaction** with no apparent economic
  purpose
- **Third-party funding** with no clear legitimate explanation
- **Last-minute change** of funding structure
- Client is **reluctant to provide** source of funds documentation
- **Shell companies** or trusts in the ownership chain
- Transaction involves **high-value property** disproportionate to known
  income/wealth

EDD means:
- More extensive evidence gathering (further back, more detail)
- Senior sign-off (MLRO or compliance officer)
- Source of **wealth** check (not just source of funds) — how did the
  client build their overall financial position?
- Ongoing monitoring throughout the transaction
- Documented risk assessment explaining the decision to proceed

## SRA-Specific Obligations

- **Risk-based approach** mandated — firms must have a documented
  practice-wide risk assessment and individual matter risk assessments
- **Regulation 28(12)** — ongoing monitoring during the retainer, not just
  at onboarding. If funding changes mid-transaction, re-assess.
- **Record-keeping** — retain all CDD records for **5 years** from the end
  of the business relationship or occasional transaction
- **Nominated officer (MLRO)** — must be appointed; all internal suspicions
  reported to MLRO first
- **Training** — all relevant staff must receive AML training appropriate
  to their role
- **SRA Warning Notices** — consult current SRA AML warning notices for
  emerging typologies

## CLC-Specific Obligations

- CLC Code of Conduct requires licensed conveyancers to comply with MLR
  2017 in full
- **CLC AML Code** — separate regulatory code specifically for AML; mirrors
  MLR 2017 obligations but with CLC enforcement
- CLC firms must have a **practice-wide risk assessment** reviewed annually
- **CLC Regulatory Compliance Manager** performs the MLRO-equivalent role
- CLC publishes its own AML guidance — check for updates on
  clc-uk.org/compliance
- Same 5-year record-keeping obligation
- CLC may conduct desk-based or on-site AML audits

## Reporting Obligations

### Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)

- File with the **National Crime Agency (NCA)** via SAR Online
- **Do not tip off** the client — tipping off is a criminal offence under
  POCA s.333A
- Report to your **MLRO/nominated officer first** — they decide whether to
  submit to NCA
- Keep no copy of the SAR on the client file (to avoid tipping off risk)
- Note: you can record internally that a report was made, without details

### Defence Against Money Laundering (DAML)

- If you **suspect** money laundering but need to continue acting (e.g. to
  avoid tipping off), apply for **consent from NCA** via a DAML request
- NCA has **7 working days** to refuse consent; if no refusal, you may
  proceed
- If refused, a further **31 calendar days** moratorium applies
- **Do not** proceed with the transaction without consent if you have
  suspicion

### When to Report

- Any suspicion or reasonable grounds for suspicion of money laundering
- You do NOT need certainty — the threshold is suspicion
- Failure to report is a criminal offence (POCA s.330)
- "Turning a blind eye" — deliberately ignoring red flags — constitutes
  the required mental element

## Red Flags Checklist

Read `references/red-flags-checklist.md` for the comprehensive red flags
checklist. Key red flags to watch for:

- Last-minute change of funding source or structure
- Third-party funding with no clear family/commercial relationship
- Cash-heavy deposits (physical cash or unexplained large cash deposits)
- Multiple property purchases in a short period
- Reluctance or delay in providing source of funds documentation
- Funds routed through multiple accounts or jurisdictions
- Price significantly above or below market value
- Client acting on behalf of undisclosed third party
- Pressure to complete unusually quickly
- Use of corporate vehicles with opaque ownership

## Practical Workflow

1. **At instruction** — send source of funds questionnaire; identify all
   funding elements and amounts
2. **Risk-assess** — score the client, transaction, and each funding
   element; document the assessment
3. **Request evidence** — for each funding element, request the specific
   documents listed above
4. **Review and verify** — check documents are consistent, amounts tally,
   names match; investigate discrepancies
5. **Escalate if needed** — if any red flags or EDD triggers, escalate to
   MLRO/compliance officer
6. **Document** — record your assessment, what you reviewed, and your
   conclusion on file
7. **Monitor** — if funding changes during the transaction (Reg 28(12)),
   repeat steps 2–6 for the new element
8. **Completion** — verify funds received match expected source before
   completing

## Combined Funding Example

> Client purchasing for £450,000. Funding: £300,000 mortgage, £100,000
> savings, £50,000 gift from parent.

Evidence needed:
- **Mortgage**: mortgage offer from FCA-regulated lender ✓
- **Savings (£100k)**: 12 months bank statements showing accumulation to
  £100k; trace any large deposits
- **Gift (£50k)**: parent's passport/driving licence; parent's 3–6 months
  bank statements showing they hold £50k+; signed gift letter (amount,
  relationship, no repayment); notify lender per Handbook Part 2
- **Total check**: £300k + £100k + £50k = £450k — funds tally with
  purchase price ✓

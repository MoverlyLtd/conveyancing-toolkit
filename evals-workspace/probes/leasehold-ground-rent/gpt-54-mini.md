Yes — it is an issue, and the answer depends on **what date the lease was granted** and **what advice is needed for the transaction**.

## 1) On your facts: a 2015 lease is not caught by the 2022 Act

The **Leasehold Reform (Ground Rent) Act 2022** only applies to **“regulated leases”** granted on or after **30 June 2022**.  
A lease granted in **2015** is therefore **outside the Act** and its ground rent terms remain enforceable unless varied.

So, a ground rent of **£250 per annum, doubling every 25 years** in a 2015 lease is **not illegal under the 2022 Act**, because the Act is **not retrospective**.

## 2) Why it still matters commercially

Even though the Act does not apply, a **doubling ground rent clause** is often a problem for:

- **mortgageability**
- **resale value**
- **investor / buyer appetite**

Many lenders treat doubling ground rent provisions as onerous, especially if the rent escalates to a level that becomes high relative to value or income. A lease that starts at **£250 pa** and doubles every **25 years** becomes:

- year 1–25: **£250**
- years 26–50: **£500**
- years 51–75: **£1,000**
- years 76–100: **£2,000**

That can become a significant issue over time, particularly if the lease term is long enough for the later increases to arise.

In practice, you should check the lender’s handbook instructions and any specific lender policy. Some lenders are concerned about:
- **doubling clauses**
- **review patterns**
- ground rent exceeding certain thresholds as a percentage of value or at future dates

## 3) What changed under the Leasehold Reform (Ground Rent) Act 2022?

For **regulated leases** granted on or after **30 June 2022**:

- the ground rent must be **peppercorn**
- effectively, the rent is reduced to **nil**
- rent provisions requiring payment of money, or anything else of monetary value, are prohibited if they amount to ground rent

### Key statutory points
The main operative provision is **section 1** of the Act, which provides that a **rent is not payable under a regulated lease** except a permitted rent.

A **regulated lease** is broadly a long residential lease granted for a term certain of more than 21 years, with some exceptions. The relevant starting point is **sections 2 and 3**.

The Act applies to leases **granted on or after 30 June 2022**. Leases granted before that date are generally outside scope.

## 4) Important exceptions and practical carve-outs

The Act does **not** apply to all leases. There are exceptions, including certain:
- business leases
- statutory lease extensions
- community housing leases
- home finance plan leases
- some very low-value / special category leases

But for a typical residential flat lease granted in 2015, the Act is irrelevant because the lease is simply **pre-commencement**.

## 5) What you should do on this purchase

For a 2015 lease with doubling ground rent, I would suggest:

1. **Check the lease term remaining**
   - If the lease is getting short, that may be a bigger issue than the ground rent itself.

2. **Check the lender’s requirements**
   - Some lenders will not accept doubling rent clauses beyond certain thresholds.
   - If a lender is involved, compare the clause against that lender’s handbook policy.

3. **Consider a deed of variation**
   - If the seller can obtain a variation to reduce the rent to a peppercorn or a fixed low figure, that may improve mortgageability and resale.

4. **Raise the issue early with the client**
   - Explain that the 2022 Act does not rescue pre-30 June 2022 leases.
   - Advise that future purchasers may face the same concern.

5. **Check whether the rent is reviewed on the “double every 25 years” basis from grant**
   - If so, it may be less problematic than shorter doubling cycles, but it can still be regarded as onerous.

## 6) Bottom line

- **Is it an issue?**  
  **Yes, potentially for mortgageability and resale**, even though it is not unlawful under the 2022 Act.

- **What changed with the 2022 Act?**  
  For **new regulated leases granted on or after 30 June 2022**, ground rent is generally reduced to **peppercorn** and **no ground rent is payable** under the lease, subject to limited exceptions.

- **Does it affect a 2015 lease?**  
  **No**, not directly. The existing ground rent clause remains valid.

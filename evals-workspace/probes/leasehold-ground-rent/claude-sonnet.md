## The Problem with Doubling Ground Rent

Starting at £250 in 2015, this ground rent will:
- Double to £500 in 2040
- Reach £1,000 in 2065
- Hit £2,000 in 2090
- Escalate to £4,000 in 2115

This exponential growth will likely make the property unmarketeable and unmortgageable in future years, as lenders are increasingly refusing to lend on properties with onerous ground rent terms.

## Leasehold Reform (Ground Rent) Act 2022

The Act, which came into force on 30 June 2022, applies **prospectively only** to:
- New residential long leases (over 21 years) granted on or after 30 June 2022
- It caps ground rent at one peppercorn per year (effectively £0)

**Crucially, this Act does NOT apply to your client's situation** because:
1. The lease was granted in 2015 (pre-Act)
2. This is an existing lease, not a new grant
3. The Act has no retrospective effect

## Practical Advice

1. **Mortgage implications**: Check whether your client's intended lender will accept this ground rent clause. Many now have policies against lending on properties with doubling ground rents.

2. **Future saleability**: Warn your client that this could severely impact future sale prospects.

3. **Consider alternatives**:
   - Negotiate with the seller for a reduction in purchase price
   - Investigate whether the freeholder might accept a ground rent buyout
   - Consider whether lease extension might be viable (though this won't eliminate the ground rent issue entirely)

4. **Full disclosure**: Ensure your client fully understands the long-term financial implications before proceeding.

The 2022 Act provides no relief for existing leases with problematic ground rent terms.

## Legal Implications

**Building Regulations Non-Compliance:**
- The loft conversion likely required Building Regulations approval under the Building Act 1984
- Without a completion certificate, there's no evidence the work complies with Building Regulations
- This constitutes a breach of Building Regulations, which is a criminal offence

**Property Law Issues:**
- Potential breach of title guarantee if the seller cannot demonstrate lawful alterations
- Risk of enforcement action by the local authority
- Insurance complications - many insurers exclude cover for non-compliant work
- Resale difficulties - future buyers will face the same concerns

## Practical Consequences

1. **Local Authority Powers:**
   - Can serve an enforcement notice requiring remedial work
   - Can prosecute for Building Regulations breaches
   - May require partial demolition if work cannot be made compliant

2. **Financial Risks:**
   - Cost of obtaining retrospective approval
   - Potential remedial works if non-compliant
   - Insurance claims may be invalid
   - Reduced property value

## Available Remedies

### 1. Regularisation Certificate
**Building Act 1984, Section 35:**
- Apply to local authority for a regularisation certificate
- Costs typically £300-600 plus any required remedial works
- Authority will inspect and may require modifications
- Takes 6-12 weeks typically

### 2. Structural Engineer's Report
- Commission detailed structural assessment
- Costs £500-1,500
- Provides professional opinion on compliance
- Not legally equivalent to Building Regulations approval but offers some protection

### 3. Specialist Building Regulations Insurance
- Indemnity insurance for non-compliant work
- Costs £200-500 for typical loft conversion
- Covers enforcement action and remedial costs
- Policy typically for 6+ years
- Reduces over time as enforcement becomes statute-barred

### 4. Contractual Protection
- **Price reduction** reflecting the risk and cost of remedies
- **Retention** of purchase monies until regularisation obtained
- **Warranty from seller** to cover enforcement costs
- **Right to rescind** if regularisation refused

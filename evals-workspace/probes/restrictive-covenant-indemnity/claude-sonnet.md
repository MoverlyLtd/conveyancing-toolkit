## Legal Options for Your Client

### 1. **Risk of Enforcement Assessment**
Given the covenant dates from 1965 (59 years old) and has never been enforced, enforcement risk may be relatively low, but consider:
- **Who has benefit of covenant**: Original developer, neighbouring properties, or management company
- **Whether beneficiaries still exist** and have continuing interest in enforcement
- **Pattern of development** in the area since 1965

### 2. **Potential Defences if Challenged**

**Abandonment/Acquiescence**: If similar home office use has occurred openly in the area without objection, this could indicate abandonment of the covenant's benefit.

**Section 84 Law of Property Act 1925 Application**: Could apply to Upper Tribunal for covenant modification/discharge on grounds that:
- Original purpose has ceased to be practical benefit (s.84(1)(a))
- Proposed use won't injure persons entitled to benefit (s.84(1)(aa))
- Covenant impedes reasonable user and provides no practical benefit (s.84(1)(c))

**Costs**: £2,500-£5,000+ plus potential compensation to beneficiaries.

### 3. **Indemnity Insurance**

**Typical Premium**: £200-£600 for standard home office use, depending on:
- Property value
- Specific covenant wording
- Level of cover required (usually 2-3x property value)
- Insurer's risk assessment

**Cover**: Usually includes legal costs and damages if covenant is enforced. **Excess**: Typically £1,000-£2,500.

**Limitations**: Won't cover if breach is discovered during policy application process or if enforcement action already commenced.

### 4. **Practical Recommendation**

For discrete home office use (no signage, visitors, or noise), **indemnity insurance is often the most cost-effective solution**. The 1965 date and lack of enforcement history should result in competitive premiums.

**Action**: Obtain insurance quotations before advising on formal legal routes, which are significantly more expensive.

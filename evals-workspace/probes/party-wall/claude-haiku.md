In this scenario, your client will need to serve a Party Wall Notice on their neighbour as the planned single-storey rear extension will be within 3 metres of the neighbour's building.

The relevant legislation is the Party Wall etc. Act 1996. Under Section 6 of the Act, a building owner must serve a Party Wall Notice on any adjoining owner where the works involve:

1. Building on the line of junction between the two properties (i.e. the party wall).
2. Excavating within 3 metres of a neighbour's building and going deeper than their foundations.
3. Carrying out work to an existing party wall.

As your client's extension will be within 3 metres of the neighbour's building, they will need to serve a notice under Section 6(1) of the Act.

The process is as follows:

1. Your client must serve a written Party Wall Notice at least 2 months before the planned start date of the works.
2. The notice must include details of the proposed works, drawings, and the proposed start date.
3. The notice must be served on all adjoining owners that will be affected by the works.

Once the notice has been served, the adjoining owner has 14 days to respond. They can:
- Consent to the works
- Dissent and appoint their own surveyor
- Take no action, in which case your client can appoint a surveyor to act on behalf of both parties.

If the adjoining owner dissents, a Party Wall Award will need to be agreed, setting out the details of the works and any required protection measures. This process can take 1-3 months.

In summary, your client will need to serve a Party Wall Notice at least 2 months before the planned start date, and be prepared for the possibility of a Party Wall Award being required, which could add 1-3 months to the timeline.

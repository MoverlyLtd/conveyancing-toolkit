Your client will likely need to serve a **Party Wall Notice** under the Party Wall etc. Act 1996, specifically a **Notice of Adjacent Excavation** under **Section 6**.

## When Section 6 Notice is Required

A Section 6 notice is needed when:
- Excavating within **3 metres** of a neighbour's building, AND
- The excavation will go deeper than the neighbour's foundations

For a typical single-storey rear extension, foundation depth is usually 450mm-1200mm, which often exceeds existing house foundations built to older standards.

## Process and Timeline

**1. Serve the Notice (Section 6)**
- Must be served **at least 1 month** before work commences
- Serve on all affected adjoining owners
- Must include detailed plans showing excavation depth and proximity

**2. Neighbour's Response Options**
They have **14 days** to respond:
- **Consent**: Work can proceed after 1-month notice period
- **Dissent or no response**: A Party Wall Surveyor must be appointed

**3. If Dispute/No Consent**
- **Surveyor appointment**: Each party appoints a surveyor, or they can agree on one "Agreed Surveyor"
- **Award preparation**: Surveyor(s) prepare a Party Wall Award (typically 2-4 weeks)
- **Work can commence**: Once Award is served

## Total Timeline
- **Minimum**: 1 month (if neighbour consents)
- **If disputed**: 2-3 months typically

## Practical Advice
- Serve notice early in the planning process
- Consider appointing surveyors proactively if relations are strained
- Budget £1,500-£3,000+ for surveyor fees if disputed
- Ensure your client's architect confirms exact foundation depths before serving notice

The extension itself doesn't trigger party wall requirements unless it's actually on the boundary - it's the excavation proximity that matters here.

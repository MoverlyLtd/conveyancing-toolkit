Under current SRA AML expectations, you should evidence **both source of funds (SoF)** for each contribution and, where relevant, **source of wealth (SoW)** for the donors as well. For a £750,000 purchase funded by:

- **£200,000 savings**
- **£100,000 gifted by parents**
- **£450,000 mortgage**

you should take the following.

---

## 1) £200,000 from the client’s own savings

### What to obtain
You should obtain a **clear audit trail** showing how the savings accumulated and that they belong to the client.

### Typical evidence
- **6 months’ bank statements** (often longer if needed) showing the build-up of the funds
- Statements from any accounts the money was transferred through
- Evidence of where the savings came from, depending on the origin:
  - salary/payslips and P60s if earned income
  - sale completion statement if proceeds of an earlier property sale
  - dividend vouchers / tax returns if investment income
  - inheritance paperwork if inherited
  - business accounts if from company profits
- If the funds were recently moved between accounts, the **full chain of transfers** needs showing

### Practical point
If the £200,000 has been held for a long period, you do **not** necessarily need years of statements, but you do need enough to be satisfied the funds are genuine and that there is a plausible trail from origin to current account. If it is a large balance built over time, ask for evidence of the main source(s) of accumulation.

---

## 2) £100,000 gift from parents

This is the area where firms commonly under-document.

### What to obtain
You need:
1. **Gifted deposit letter / deed of gift** signed by the parents
2. Evidence that the money has come from the parents’ account to the client’s account or to the conveyancer/client account
3. **Source of wealth/source of funds evidence for the parents** to support the gift

### Gift letter should confirm
- full names and addresses of the parents
- relationship to the client
- exact amount: **£100,000**
- that the money is an **unconditional gift**
- that it is **not repayable**
- that the donors will have **no beneficial interest** in the property
- that it is not secured against the property
- ideally whether the donor expects any occupation rights or future equity

### Parent donor evidence
You should normally obtain:
- **6 months’ bank statements** for the parents’ account from which the gift is made
- Evidence of the parents’ source of wealth if the balance is not obviously from income/savings
- If the gift is funded from a house sale, inheritance, pension lump sum, business sale, etc., get the relevant supporting documents

### Practical point
For a **£100,000 parental gift**, many firms also request proof of ID for the donors, even if the parents are not your clients, because of AML and lender expectations.

---

## 3) £450,000 mortgage

A mortgage advance is not usually treated as “client source of funds” in the same way as cash savings, but you still need to satisfy yourself as to the legitimacy of the funding and any AML implications.

### What to obtain
- **Mortgage offer**
- Confirmation from the lender/broker of the amount: **£450,000**
- If applicable, evidence of deposit/own contribution against lender conditions
- Check the lender and product are genuine and that the borrower is named correctly

### Practical point
If any part of the mortgage is funded through:
- cash-backed unsecured borrowing,
- family loans,
- director’s loans,
- second charges,
- bridge finance,

then you need to treat that element separately and obtain SoF evidence for that borrowing source too.

---

# What the SRA expects in practice

The key point from the SRA Standards and Regulations is that you must **not facilitate transactions unless you have effective systems and controls to identify and verify source of funds and source of wealth where required**.

The SRA’s AML guidance is risk-based, but for a transaction like this:

- **£200,000 savings** = verify with bank statements and underlying provenance
- **£100,000 gift** = verify donor identity, donor bank statements, and donor source of wealth/source of funds
- **£450,000 mortgage** = verify mortgage offer and lender details

## Suggested document pack for this exact case

### For the client
- Photo ID and proof of address
- 6 months’ statements for the account(s) holding the £200,000
- Explanation of how savings were accumulated, with supporting documents

### For the parents
- ID and address evidence
- Signed gift letter/deed
- 6 months’ bank statements
- Evidence of source of the £100,000 if not obvious from their savings/income

### For the mortgage
- Copy mortgage offer for **£450,000**
- Broker/lender confirmation if needed
- Evidence of repayment basis if lender requests it

---

## If you want a conservative “best practice” AML approach
For a purchase of this size, many firms would ask for:
- **6 months bank statements for all relevant accounts**
- **Specific evidence for each large credit**
- **A written SoF explanation from the client**
- **Gifted deposit letter**
- **Donor SoF/SoW evidence**
- **Mortgage offer**

That is usually enough to satisfy a risk-based file review, provided the trail is coherent.

---

## Relevant regulatory touchpoints
You should be thinking in terms of:
- **SRA Standards and Regulations**
- **SRA Code of Conduct for Solicitors, RELs and RFLs**
- **SRA AML guidance on source of funds and source of wealth**
- A risk-based approach under the **Money Laundering Regulations 2017**

There is no single “one-size-fits-all” prescribed document list in the SRA rules, but for this fact pattern the above is the practical minimum most compliant firms would expect.

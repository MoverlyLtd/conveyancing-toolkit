Under the SRA Code of Conduct (Outcome 7.3) and Money Laundering Regulations 2017, you need the following specific evidence for each funding source:

## £200,000 Savings
**Required evidence:**
- **Bank statements** showing the accumulation of funds over 3-6 months minimum
- **Payslips/employment confirmation** to verify income source that created the savings
- If self-employed: **SA302 forms** or **certified accounts** for 2-3 years
- **Investment statements** if funds are from investment sales/dividends

## £100,000 Gift from Parents
**Required evidence:**
- **Signed gift letter** confirming it's a gift with no expectation of repayment
- **Parents' bank statements** (3-6 months) showing they held these funds
- **Parents' source of wealth evidence** - payslips, pension statements, or sale proceeds if from property
- **Transfer documentation** showing movement from parents' account to client's account
- **Parents' ID verification** (if you haven't acted for them before)

## £450,000 Mortgage
**Required evidence:**
- **Mortgage offer letter** from the lender
- **Lender's confirmation** that their own AML checks are complete
- **Evidence of deposit paid to lender** (usually from the above sources)

## Additional Requirements
- **Client ID verification** (passport/driving licence + utility bill)
- **Beneficial ownership verification** if client is not the ultimate beneficial owner
- **PEP screening** results
- **Sanctions checking** documentation

**SRA Warning Notice (December 2023)** emphasizes you must be satisfied about the **legitimacy of all funds** and cannot rely solely on lender AML checks for the mortgage element's deposit requirements.

Retain all evidence for **5 years** post-completion as required under MLR 2017, regulation 40.

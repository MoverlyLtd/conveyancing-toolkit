For your client's purchase of the £300,000 buy-to-let property, the SDLT calculation is as follows:

**Standard SDLT on £300,000:**
- £0 - £250,000: 0% = £0
- £250,001 - £300,000: 5% = £2,500
- **Standard SDLT total: £2,500**

**Additional Dwelling Surcharge:**
Since your client already owns their main residence (worth £180,000) and is purchasing an additional dwelling, they must pay the 3% surcharge on the entire purchase price under Schedule 4ZA of the Finance Act 2003.

- 3% × £300,000 = **£9,000**

**Total SDLT payable: £2,500 + £9,000 = £11,500**

**Key points:**
- The surcharge applies because this is a second property
- The surcharge is calculated on the full purchase price, not in bands
- No relief applies as the existing property will be retained as the main residence
- The additional dwelling surcharge rate has been 3% since April 2016

Your client should budget £11,500 for SDLT, payable within 14 days of completion.

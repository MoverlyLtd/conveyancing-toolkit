Because your client is buying a **second property** and will still own and occupy their current home, the **3% additional dwelling surcharge** applies under **FA 2003 Sch 4ZA**.

## 1) SDLT on the £300,000 buy-to-let
For an **additional dwelling**, the residential SDLT rates are:

- 0% on first £125,000
- 2% on the portion £125,001 to £250,000
- 5% on the portion £250,001 to £925,000

So on **£300,000**:

- First £125,000 @ 3% = **£3,750**  
- Next £125,000 @ 5% = **£6,250**  
- Remaining £50,000 @ 8% = **£4,000**

### Total SDLT = **£14,000**

## 2) Breakdown
This can also be seen as:

- Standard residential SDLT on £300,000:
  - 0% on £125,000 = £0
  - 2% on £125,000 = £2,500
  - 5% on £50,000 = £2,500  
  - Standard SDLT total = **£5,000**

- Additional dwelling surcharge:
  - 3% of £300,000 = **£9,000**

### Combined total = **£14,000**

## 3) Practical points
- The value of the existing home (**£180,000**) is irrelevant to the SDLT on the purchase price, except that it confirms they will own another dwelling at completion.
- If they are replacing their only/main residence and meet the relief conditions in **FA 2003 Sch 4ZA para 3**, the surcharge may not apply; but on your facts, this is a straight **second property to rent out**, so the surcharge applies.
- SDLT is due within **14 days of completion** and reported via an SDLT return.

Based on the current Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) rates for England and Northern Ireland (effective from 23 September 2022), here is the specific breakdown for your client’s purchase.

### 1. Total SDLT Payable
For a purchase price of **£300,000**, where the buyer will own more than one property at completion and is not replacing their main residence, the total SDLT due is **£11,500**.

---

### 2. Detailed Calculation Breakdown
Because your client is purchasing an "additional residential property" and is not selling their current main residence, the **Higher Rates for Additional Dwellings (HRAD)** apply. This adds a flat **3% surcharge** on top of the standard residential rates.

The calculation is as follows:

| Portion of Value | Standard Rate | HRAD Rate | Calculation | Tax Due |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **£0 – £250,000** | 0% | 3% | £250,000 × 3% | **£7,500** |
| **£250,001 – £300,000** | 5% | 8% | £50,000 × 8% | **£4,000** |
| **Total** | | | | **£11,500** |

---

### 3. Statutory References
*   **The Charging Provision:** The surcharge is governed by **Section 56 and Schedule 4ZA of the Finance Act 2003**.
*   **Condition A (Section 3 of Schedule 4ZA):** This condition is met because the chargeable consideration is £40,000 or more and, at the end of the day of the transaction, the purchaser owns an interest in another dwelling (the £180,000 property) which has a market value of more than £40,000 and is not subject to a lease of more than 21 years.
*   **Replacement of Main Residence Rule:** The client does not qualify for the exception under **Paragraph 3(5) of Schedule 4ZA** because they are not disposing of their previous main residence; they are retaining it to live in while buying an investment property.

### 4. Practical Advice and Considerations

#### Non-UK Resident Surcharge
The figure of **£11,500** assumes your client is a UK resident for tax purposes. If the client is a "non-UK resident" (as defined by the 183-day rule), a further **2% surcharge** applies under **Schedule 9A FA 2003**. This would add an extra £6,000, bringing the total to £17,500.

#### Filing Deadlines
You must ensure the **SDLT1 return** is filed and the tax is paid to HMRC within **14 days** of the "effective date" (usually the date of completion). Failure to do so results in an automatic £100 penalty plus interest.

#### Future Sale of the First Property
If your client later decides to move into the £300,000 property as their main residence and sells the £180,000 property within **3 years**, they may be eligible for a refund of the 3% surcharge element (£9,000 in this case). However, since the prompt states the second property is "to rent out," this is unlikely to apply unless their intentions change and they occupy the second property as their primary home.

#### First-Time Buyer Relief
Your client is **not** eligible for First-Time Buyer Relief (Finance Act 2003, Schedule 6ZA) as they already own a residential property. Ownership of property anywhere in the world disqualifies a buyer from this relief.

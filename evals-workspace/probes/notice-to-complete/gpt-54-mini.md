Under the **Standard Conditions of Sale (5th Edition)**, if the buyer does not complete on the contractual completion date, the seller may serve a **notice to complete** **immediately after completion has failed** — so **from Saturday 5 April 2025 onward** (assuming the contract was not otherwise varied).

## Key contract provisions
- **Condition 6.8.1**: if either party is ready, able and willing to complete but the other side does not complete on the completion date, the innocent party may serve a notice to complete.
- **Condition 6.8.2**: the notice must give **10 working days** after service for completion.
- **Condition 6.8.3**: the completion period does **not include** the day of service, and only **working days** count.

## Your dates

- Buyer fails to complete on **Friday 4 April 2025**

### Earliest service
The seller can serve the notice **on Friday 4 April 2025 itself**, once it is clear completion has not taken place, or practically **from Saturday 5 April 2025**.

## When does the 10-working-day notice expire?
If served on **Friday 4 April 2025**, the 10 working days run from the next working day and expire at the end of:

1. Mon 7 Apr
2. Tue 8 Apr
3. Wed 9 Apr
4. Thu 10 Apr
5. Fri 11 Apr
6. Mon 14 Apr
7. Tue 15 Apr
8. Wed 16 Apr
9. Thu 17 Apr
10. Fri 18 Apr 2025

So the notice would expire at **end of business on Friday 18 April 2025**.

## Practical point
If the notice is served **after** 4 April, the expiry moves accordingly, but the period is still **10 working days**. Also, if served by post, you need to allow for the contract’s service provisions when calculating the service date.

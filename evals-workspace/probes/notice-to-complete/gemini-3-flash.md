Under the **Standard Conditions of Sale (5th Edition — 2011 Revision)**, the timing for serving a notice to complete and its subsequent expiry is governed by conditions 1.1, 6.1, and 6.8.

### 1. When can the seller serve the Notice to Complete?

According to **SCS 6.8.1**, the seller may serve a notice to complete "at any time on or after the completion date," provided the seller is "ready, able, and willing to complete."

However, this is subject to the **2:00 pm rule** found in **SCS 6.1.1**. Completion is required to take place no later than 2:00 pm on the contractual completion date. If completion does not occur by this time, the buyer is technically in breach.

*   **Specific Date:** The seller can serve the notice immediately after **2:00 pm on Friday 4 April 2025**.
*   **Condition:** The seller’s solicitor must ensure the seller is "ready, able, and willing." This means the transfer deed is signed, all undertakings are ready, and any mortgages to be discharged are accounted for.

### 2. When does the Notice to Complete expire?

Under **SCS 6.8.2**, the parties must complete within **ten working days** of the notice being given. In calculating this period, the day on which the notice is served is excluded.

**Definition of a "Working Day" (SCS 1.1.1(m)):**
A working day is any day from Monday to Friday, excluding bank holidays and the period between 24 and 31 December.

**The April 2025 Calendar & Easter Holidays:**
In 2025, Easter falls as follows:
*   **Good Friday:** 18 April 2025 (Bank Holiday)
*   **Easter Monday:** 21 April 2025 (Bank Holiday)

**Calculation (assuming service on Friday 4 April 2025 after 2:00 pm but before 4:00 pm via email/DX):**
*   **Day 0:** Friday 4 April (Day of service – excluded)
*   **Day 1:** Monday 7 April
*   **Day 2:** Tuesday 8 April
*   **Day 3:** Wednesday 9 April
*   **Day 4:** Thursday 10 April
*   **Day 5:** Friday 11 April
*   **Day 6:** Monday 14 April
*   **Day 7:** Tuesday 15 April
*   **Day 8:** Wednesday 16 April
*   **Day 9:** Thursday 17 April
*   *[Friday 18 April – Good Friday – Excluded]*
*   *[Monday 21 April – Easter Monday – Excluded]*
*   **Day 10:** **Tuesday 22 April 2025**

**Expiry:** The notice will expire at **23:59 on Tuesday 22 April 2025**. The seller would be entitled to rescind the contract and forfeit the deposit from the first moment of Wednesday 23 April 2025.

### 3. Practical Advice and Technical Nuances

*   **Methods of Service (SCS 1.3):**
    *   If you serve by **Email** or **Personal Delivery** before 4:00 pm on Friday 4 April, it is deemed served that day.
    *   If you serve by **DX or First Class Post**, it is deemed served on the **second working day** after posting (**SCS 1.3.7(b)**). If posted on Friday 4 April, it would not be "served" until Tuesday 8 April, which would push the expiry date back significantly (to Wednesday 23 April or later). **Always serve by email/PDF to ensure the fastest turnaround.**
*   **"Ready, Able, and Willing":** If the seller serves a notice but is not actually ready to move out or hasn't signed the transfer deed, the notice is invalid. The buyer could potentially sue the seller for breach of contract if the seller tries to rescind based on an invalid notice.
*   **Interest:** Under **SCS 7.3**, the seller is entitled to contract interest (usually 4% above base rate as specified in the contract) for every day of delay from 4 April until completion.
*   **Deposit:** Once the notice expires on 22 April, the seller can rescind the contract under **SCS 7.5**. They can forfeit (keep) the 10% deposit and any accrued interest, and resell the property. If a 10% deposit was not paid on exchange (e.g., only 5% was paid), the seller is entitled to claim the remaining balance to bring it up to 10% (**SCS 7.5.2**).

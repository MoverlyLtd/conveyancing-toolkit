Under the **Standard Conditions of Sale (5th Edition)** (“SCS 5”), if the buyer does not complete on the contractual completion date, completion is not automatically “time of the essence”. The seller must **serve a Notice to Complete** to make time of the essence and start the contractual “grace” period.

## 1) When can the seller serve the Notice to Complete?

### Rule / reference
- **SCS 5 – Condition 6.8.1** (Notice to complete): a party may serve notice **when the other party is in default** of an obligation to complete.

### Apply to your dates
- Contractual completion date: **Friday 4 April 2025**
- Buyer fails to complete on that date: the buyer is in default **as soon as completion does not take place on 4 April** (in practice, once the contractual completion arrangements have failed that day).

So the seller can serve a Notice to Complete **on Friday 4 April 2025**, **after** it is clear completion has failed (often served late afternoon), or on the next working day. There is no need to wait.

**Practical conveyancing point:** a notice served *before* the buyer is actually in default can be invalid. In practice, firms often serve the notice **once it is clear funds/undertakings won’t arrive by the completion deadline** that day.

## 2) What is the notice period and when does it expire?

### Rule / reference
- **SCS 5 – Condition 6.8.2**: the notice must specify a completion date **not earlier than the end of the “notice period”**.
- **SCS 5 – Condition 6.8.3**: the **notice period is 10 working days** after service of the notice (with the detailed service/timing mechanics governed by the SCS service condition).

### Counting the 10 working days (assuming service on 4 April 2025)
If the seller serves a valid Notice to Complete on **Friday 4 April 2025**, the **10 working days** (excluding weekends and bank holidays in England & Wales) run as follows:

1. Mon **7 Apr** (Day 1)  
2. Tue **8 Apr** (Day 2)  
3. Wed **9 Apr** (Day 3)  
4. Thu **10 Apr** (Day 4)  
5. Fri **11 Apr** (Day 5)  
6. Mon **14 Apr** (Day 6)  
7. Tue **15 Apr** (Day 7)  
8. Wed **16 Apr** (Day 8)  
9. Thu **17 Apr** (Day 9)  
- Fri **18 Apr** = **Good Friday** (not a working day)  
- Mon **21 Apr** = **Easter Monday** (not a working day)  
10. Tue **22 Apr** (Day 10)

### Expiry / deadline
- The notice period therefore ends at the close of business on **Tuesday 22 April 2025**.
- The Notice to Complete effectively **expires at the end of Tuesday 22 April 2025** (i.e., the buyer must complete **by that date**, in accordance with the notice).

## 3) Practical advice / common pitfalls

1. **Service method/timing matters.** If the notice is served late (e.g., after the SCS deemed service cut-off) it may be deemed served the **next working day**, pushing the expiry back. Make sure it is served in accordance with the contract’s service provisions (the SCS contains a service condition dealing with deemed service).
2. **Seller must be “ready, willing and able” to complete.** A seller who cannot complete (e.g., missing discharge, cannot give vacant possession) may be unable to rely on the notice/forfeit deposit.
3. **Interest during the notice period.** Under SCS, the defaulting party (here the buyer) typically pays **contractual interest on the balance of the price** from the completion date until actual completion/termination (see **SCS 5 Condition 7**).

Under the Standard Conditions of Sale (5th Edition), here are the key timings:

## When the seller can serve a notice to complete:

**Immediately from 4 April 2025** - The seller can serve a notice to complete as soon as the buyer fails to complete on the contractual completion date. There is no waiting period required under SC 6.8.

## Notice to complete period:

The notice to complete gives the buyer **10 working days** to complete from the date the notice is served (SC 6.8.1).

**Working days exclude:**
- Saturdays and Sundays  
- Bank holidays
- Christmas Day and Good Friday (SC 1.1.1(k))

## Practical example:

If the seller serves the notice to complete on **Monday 7 April 2025**, the 10 working days would run as follows:

- Start: Monday 7 April (Day 1)
- End: **Friday 18 April 2025** (Day 10)

The buyer must complete by **4:00 PM on Friday 18 April 2025** (assuming standard completion time).

## Key points:

- The seller has no obligation to serve a notice - they could pursue other remedies immediately
- Once served, if the buyer fails to complete within the 10 working day period, the seller can rescind the contract and claim damages
- The notice period cannot be extended except by mutual agreement

**Note:** Always check if any special conditions modify these standard timings and consider Easter holidays when calculating the exact expiry date.

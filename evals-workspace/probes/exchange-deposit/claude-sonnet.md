Your client faces a shortfall between the standard 10% exchange deposit (£40,000) and their available funds (£20,000). Here are the viable options:

## 1. Negotiate Reduced Exchange Deposit
- Request the seller accept a 5% deposit (£20,000) on exchange
- This matches their mortgage LTV and available funds
- Include clause: "The deposit shall be £20,000 being 5% of the purchase price"
- **Risk**: Seller may refuse, preferring standard 10% for security

## 2. Deposit Guarantee Insurance
- Providers like **Legal & General** or **Qdos** offer deposit guarantee policies
- Cost: typically £150-£400 for policies covering the shortfall
- Allows exchange with reduced deposit while guaranteeing the seller up to 10%
- Policy pays seller if buyer defaults on the missing deposit amount

## 3. Family/Third Party Assistance
- Temporary loan from family for the £20,000 shortfall
- Must be repaid at completion from mortgage proceeds
- Ensure lender is notified if this affects their lending criteria

## 4. Delay Exchange
- Wait until additional funds become available
- **Risk**: Property market movement, seller may withdraw

## 5. Bridging Finance (Short-term)
- Borrow £20,000 short-term to meet 10% requirement
- Expensive option (rates typically 0.75%+ per month)
- Only viable if completion within weeks

**Recommendation**: Start with option 1 (negotiate 5% deposit) as most cost-effective. If seller refuses, consider deposit guarantee insurance as the most practical solution at relatively low cost.

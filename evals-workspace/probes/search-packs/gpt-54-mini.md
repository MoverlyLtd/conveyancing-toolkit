For a **typical residential purchase in England**, a buyer’s conveyancer will usually order the following **standard searches**, with a few **recommended/conditional searches** depending on the property.

## 1) Standard searches

### A. Local Authority Search
Usually split into:
- **LLC1** (Local Land Charges Register)
- **CON29R** (standard enquiries)

**What it shows**
- Planning permissions and breaches
- Building regulation approvals
- Road adoption matters
- Conservation area/listing status
- Tree preservation orders
- Compulsory purchase proposals
- Enforcement notices

**Typical cost**
- **£100–£250** in most areas, but can be **£300+** in some London authorities.

---

### B. Drainage and Water Search
Usually a **CON29DW** or equivalent.

**What it shows**
- Whether the property is connected to mains foul water and surface water drainage
- Location of public sewers and water mains
- Responsibility for sewer/drainage connections
- Water supplier details

**Typical cost**
- **£40–£80**

---

### C. Environmental Search
This is the standard search for:
- Contaminated land
- Landfill/industrial use
- Flood risk indicators
- Ground stability/subsidence indicators
- Radon screening in some reports
- Nearby environmental issues

**Typical cost**
- **£50–£90**

---

### D. Land Registry Title/Plan
Usually obtained as part of the transaction anyway, but not always called a “search” in the same way as the others.

**Typical cost**
- **£3** for official copy of title register
- **£3** for title plan  
So **£6 total** for title documents.

---

## 2) Searches often recommended, depending on the property

### E. Flood Search / Specialist Flood Report
Sometimes included in an environmental search, but often ordered separately where risk is suspected.

**Recommended if**
- Near river/coast
- Low-lying area
- Previous flood history
- Mortgage lender asks for more detail

**Typical cost**
- **£20–£40** if standalone
- More if a full specialist report is needed

---

### F. Chancel Repair Liability Search
Checks whether the property may be liable to contribute to repairs of the local parish church.

**Recommended if**
- Older rural or village property
- Particularly where no indemnity policy is already in place

**Typical cost**
- **£20–£35**

If the result is positive or uncertain, a **chancel indemnity policy** is often used instead.

---

### G. Coal Mining Search
Relevant if the property is in a coalfield area.

**Recommended if property is in**
- Parts of the North East, North West, Yorkshire, Midlands, South Wales, etc.

**Typical cost**
- **£25–£50**

---

### H. Mining Searches other than coal
May include:
- **Tin mining**
- **Brine extraction**
- **Limestone or gypsum**
- **Metal mining**
- **Borehole/mineral extraction risk**

**Recommended if**
- Relevant local history or lender guidance

**Typical cost**
- **£25–£60+** depending on the search type

---

### I. Commons Registration / Village Green Search
Checks whether land may be registered as common land or a village green, which can affect development and use.

**Recommended if**
- Rural/edge-of-village property
- Large plots, paddocks, or land with development potential

**Typical cost**
- **£20–£40**

---

### J. Planning Search / Planning Report
This is not a replacement for the local authority search, but sometimes used for extra detail or quicker intelligence.

**Recommended if**
- The property is unusual
- There are known development concerns nearby
- Client wants more planning background

**Typical cost**
- **£30–£100**

---

### K. Highway Search / Road Adoption Report
Sometimes covered by CON29R, but separate specialist checks may be used to confirm:
- Road adoption status
- Access rights
- Future road proposals

**Recommended if**
- Private road
- Unclear access
- New estate roads

**Typical cost**
- **£20–£60**

---

### L. HS2 / Infrastructure / Transport Scheme Search
Checks for major infrastructure proposals affecting the property.

**Recommended if**
- Near proposed rail/road schemes
- Property is in an affected corridor

**Typical cost**
- Usually **£20–£40**, if ordered separately

---

### M. Energy / Infrastructure / Development risk searches
These may cover:
- Wind farms
- Solar farms
- Power lines
- Pipelines
- Telecoms infrastructure
- Major development nearby

**Recommended if**
- Rural property
- Land with open views
- Buyer is concerned about future amenity

**Typical cost**
- **£20–£60**

---

## 3) Typical total cost

### Standard search pack total
For a normal residential purchase, the usual core pack is:

- Local Authority: **£100–£250**
- Drainage & Water: **£40–£80**
- Environmental: **£50–£90**
- Land Registry title docs: **£6**

**Typical total: £196–£426**

A very common real-world total is around:
- **£250–£350**

---

### With common optional searches
If you add a few typical extras:
- Chancel: **£20–£35**
- Coal/mining: **£25–£50**
- Flood specialist: **£20–£40**

Then a fuller package is often:
- **£300–£500** in total

---

## 4) What is actually “standard”?
In practice, for an ordinary freehold house or flat in England, most conveyancers will usually order:

1. **Local Authority**
2. **Drainage & Water**
3. **Environmental**

Those are the core searches expected by most mortgage lenders.

---

## 5) Practical advice
- **Do not rely on the seller’s answers alone**; searches are the buyer’s independent protection.
- **Always check the lender’s requirements**: some mortgage lenders insist on specific searches.
- If the property is:
  - **rural** → consider **chancel, commons, mining**
  - **near water** → add **specialist flood**
  - **older/converted** → local authority and environmental are especially important
  - **on a private road** → consider **highway/access search**
- Search results can take **a few days to several weeks**, depending on the local authority.

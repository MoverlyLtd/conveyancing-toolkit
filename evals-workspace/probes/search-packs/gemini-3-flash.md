In English conveyancing, searches are enquiries sent to various public authorities and providers to uncover risks that are not visible during a physical inspection or in the title deeds.

The standard "Big Three" searches are required by almost all mortgage lenders under the **UK Finance Mortgage Lenders’ Handbook (Section 5.4)**.

---

### 1. The "Standard Three" Searches
These are ordered for almost every residential transaction.

#### A. Local Authority Search (Official or Personal)
*   **Comprises:** Two parts: **LLC1** (Official Certificate of Search of the Local Land Charges Register) and **CON29** (Enquiries of Local Authority).
*   **Details:** Covers planning permissions, building regulation certificates, conservation areas, tree preservation orders, and proposed road schemes.
*   **Relevant Law:** Local Land Charges Act 1975.
*   **Cost:** Varies significantly by local authority, typically **£150 – £350**.

#### B. Drainage and Water Search (CON29DW)
*   **Details:** Confirms if the property is connected to a public sewer and mains water, whether there are public pipes within the property boundary (which could restrict extensions), and the location of the nearest water main.
*   **Cost:** Set by the regional water company (e.g., Thames Water, Severn Trent), typically **£60 – £100**.

#### C. Environmental Search
*   **Details:** Identifies risks of contaminated land (under **Part 2A of the Environmental Protection Act 1990**), flood risk, ground stability, and radon gas.
*   **Cost:** Typically **£50 – £80**.

---

### 2. Location-Specific Recommended Searches
Depending on the property's geographical location, these may be mandatory for lenders or strongly recommended.

#### D. Coal Mining Search (CON29M)
*   **Requirement:** Mandatory if the property is in a "Coal Mining Reporting Area."
*   **Details:** Checks for past, present, and future underground/adit mining, shafts, and subsidence claims.
*   **Cost:** Roughly **£50 – £60**.

#### E. Chancel Repair Liability Search
*   **Details:** Checks if the property has a residual medieval liability to pay for repairs to the local parish church chancel.
*   **Practical Advice:** Many conveyancers now skip the search and simply buy an **Indemnity Insurance Policy**, as the search is often inconclusive.
*   **Cost:** Search is **£15 – £25**; Indemnity insurance is often **£20 – £30** (one-off payment).

#### F. Other Mining/Ground Stability Searches
*   **Tin Mining:** (Cornwall/Devon).
*   **Brine/Salt Mining:** (Cheshire).
*   **Limestone:** (West Midlands).
*   **Cost:** Roughly **£60 – £100** per search.

#### G. Planning Search (Detailed)
*   **Details:** While the CON29 covers the property itself, a detailed Planning Search looks at planning applications on *neighboring* land (e.g., if a field behind the house is slated for 500 new homes).
*   **Cost:** Roughly **£30 – £50**.

---

### 3. Pre-Completion Searches
These are conducted after contracts are exchanged but before completion to ensure the status of the legal title hasn't changed.

#### H. Land Registry Priority Search (OS1)
*   **Details:** "Freezes" the register for 30 days, preventing any new interests being registered ahead of the buyer.
*   **Relevant Law:** Land Registration Act 2002.
*   **Cost:** **£3** per title.

#### I. Bankruptcy Search (K16)
*   **Details:** Mandatory if the buyer is using a mortgage. It ensures the buyer is not an undischarged bankrupt.
*   **Cost:** **£2** per name.

### Practical Advice for Conveyancers & Buyers

1.  **Order Bundles:** Most search providers (e.g., SearchFlow, InfoTrack, Landmark) offer bundles. A "Standard Pack" (Local, Water, Environmental) is usually cheaper than ordering individually.
2.  **Personal vs. Official Local Searches:** An "Official" search is signed by a council officer; a "Personal" search is conducted by an external agent. Most lenders accept both, but some (a small minority) insist on "Official." Always check the **UK Finance Handbook (CML)** Part 2 for the specific lender's requirements.
3.  **The "6-Month Rule":** Most lenders will not accept searches that are more than 6 months old at the date of completion. If a chain collapses and the purchase is delayed, the buyer will likely have to pay for fresh searches.
4.  **No-Search Indemnity:** In "cash" purchases where speed is vital, a buyer can choose to forgo searches and take out **No-Search Indemnity Insurance**. This is rarely an option if a mortgage is involved, as lenders require the actual due diligence.

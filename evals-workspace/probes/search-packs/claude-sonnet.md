Here are the standard and recommended searches for a residential purchase in England:

## Standard/Essential Searches

**1. Local Authority Search (LLC1 + CON29)**
- Cost: £250-400
- Reveals planning permissions, building regulations, highways, environmental issues, and local authority matters

**2. Water and Drainage Search (CON29DW)**
- Cost: £35-50
- Shows water supply, sewerage connections, and drainage arrangements

**3. Environmental Search**
- Cost: £40-80
- Identifies contaminated land, flooding, ground stability, and other environmental risks

## Recommended Searches

**4. Chancel Repair Search**
- Cost: £15-25
- Checks liability for ancient church repair obligations

**5. Land Registry Official Copy Entries (Title Register)**
- Cost: £3 per title
- Essential for checking ownership, charges, and title restrictions

**6. Land Registry Title Plan**
- Cost: £3
- Shows property boundaries and extent of ownership

## Area-Specific/Risk-Based Searches

**7. Coal Mining Search**
- Cost: £35-50
- Required in former coalfield areas (covers ~25% of England)

**8. Tin/Clay Mining Search**
- Cost: £40-60
- Necessary in Cornwall, Devon, and other mineral extraction areas

**9. Salt Mining Search**
- Cost: £30-50
- Required in Cheshire, Staffordshire, and parts of the Midlands

**10. Limestone/Chalk Mining Search**
- Cost: £35-55
- For properties in limestone areas (parts of Midlands, Yorkshire, etc.)

## Additional Searches (Property-Specific)

**11. Railway Search**
- Cost: £40-60
- For properties within 250m of railway lines

**12. HS2 Search**
- Cost: £20-35
- For properties potentially affected by HS2 route

**13. Flood Search (if not included in environmental)**
- Cost: £20-40
- Detailed flood risk assessment

**14. Rights of Way Search**
- Cost: £30-50
- Identifies public footpaths, bridleways, etc.

## Total Estimated Costs

- **Minimum (standard searches only)**: £350-550
- **Typical residential purchase**: £500-800
- **Complex/high-risk properties**: £700-1,200

## Timing
Most searches take 5-10 working days, though Local Authority searches can take up to 20 working days in some areas.

## Professional Advice
The exact combination depends on:
- Property location and geology
- Lender requirements
- Risk assessment
- Client instructions

Your conveyancer should advise which searches are essential versus recommended based on the specific property and circumstances.

Below is a practical “menu” of searches a buyer’s conveyancer typically orders on a **residential purchase in England**, split into **standard (almost always)** and **recommended/conditional (depends on the property/location)**, with **typical costs** and what each search covers.

Costs vary a lot by local authority and provider; figures below are **typical 2025 ranges** for a straightforward purchase, **per property**, excluding your conveyancer’s fees/VAT unless stated.

---

## 1) Standard searches (commonly treated as the core package)

### A. Local Authority Search (LLC1 + CON29)  
**What it covers**
- **LLC1** (Local Land Charges Register): financial charges, tree preservation orders (TPOs), conservation areas, listed building status, improvement/renovation grants, smoke control, etc.  
- **CON29**: planning permissions/refusals, building regulation approvals, enforcement notices, highway matters (adoption/road schemes), notices, compulsory purchase, etc.  
- Often includes **CON29O** (optional enquiries) only if relevant (e.g., pipelines, noise abatement zones).

**Cost (typical)**
- **Council “official” search:** ~**£120–£250** (can be higher in some councils)
- **Personal search (via search agent):** ~**£80–£160**

**Why it matters**
- Lenders nearly always require it (or acceptable equivalent).  
- Reveals things like **unadopted roads**, enforcement risks, future road schemes, and planning issues.

---

### B. Drainage & Water Search (CON29DW) – usually via Thames Water / regional undertaker data  
**What it covers**
- Whether the property is connected to **mains water/sewer**
- Location of **public sewers/drains** within boundaries (build-over risk)
- Basis of charging (metered/non-metered)
- Adoption status of sewers and water mains

**Cost (typical)**
- ~**£50–£80**

---

### C. Environmental Search (contaminated land / landfill / flooding screening)  
**What it covers**
- Risk screening for **contaminated land** (Part IIA Environmental Protection Act 1990 regime flags)
- Landfill and waste sites, industrial uses
- Basic **flood risk** indicators (often surface water, river, groundwater)
- Radon potential (often as a flag)

**Cost (typical)**
- ~**£45–£75**

---

### D. Chancel Repair Liability Search (or “ChancelCheck”)  
**What it covers**
- Screens whether the property may fall within a parish where **historic chancel repair liability** could exist.

**Cost (typical)**
- ~**£20–£35**

**Practical note**
- Many firms now treat this as “belt-and-braces” and/or move straight to **chancel indemnity insurance** if indicated.

---

## 2) Frequently recommended / often added (depending on risk profile)

### E. Flood Risk Search (specialist, beyond the environmental screening)  
**What it covers**
- More detailed flood modelling: **river, coastal, surface water, groundwater**, historic events, flood defences, insurability indicators

**Cost (typical)**
- ~**£20–£45** (basic flood report)  
- Up to ~**£60–£100** for more detailed reports in higher-risk areas

**When to consider**
- Near rivers, coast, low-lying areas; if the environmental search flags flood risk; if insurer quotes look high.

---

### F. Ground Stability / Mining / Subsidence searches (location-dependent)
These are very area-specific:

1) **Coal Authority Mining Search (CON29M)**  
- **Covers:** past/present/future coal mining activity, shafts/adits, subsidence claims  
- **Cost:** ~**£45–£70**  
- **Common in:** many Midlands/North areas.

2) Other mining: **tin, limestone, gypsum, brine, chalk, metal mines** (various providers)  
- **Cost:** ~**£35–£120** depending on region and report type  
- **Common in:** Cornwall (tin), Cheshire (brine), parts of Yorkshire (gypsum), etc.

3) **Groundsure/land stability** type reports  
- **Cost:** ~**£30–£60**  
- **Useful where:** general subsidence/land instability is suspected.

---

### G. HS2 / Crossrail / major infrastructure search (where relevant)
**What it covers**
- Whether the property is affected by safeguarded routes, compensation zones, construction impacts.

**Cost (typical)**
- ~**£20–£50**

---

### H. Planning/Development Search (wider-area planning)  
**What it covers**
- Planning applications and development proposals in the surrounding area (not just the property)  
- Helpful for spotting new estates, road widening, commercial developments nearby.

**Cost (typical)**
- ~**£25–£60**

---

### I. Commons Registration / Village Green search (occasional)
**What it covers**
- Whether the land is registered as **common land** or **town/village green**, which can severely restrict use.

**Cost (typical)**
- ~**£20–£60**

---

### J. Highways search / Roads adoption (where the local search is unclear)
**What it covers**
- Confirms whether roads/paths adjoining are **maintainable at public expense** (adopted highways), public rights of way.

**Cost (typical)**
- ~**£80–£150** (specialist highways report)  
- Note: some highway info comes via the local search, but not always conclusively.

---

### K. Utilities / Infrastructure searches (as-needed)
**Examples**
- **Public sewer build-over check** (sometimes already in CON29DW; sometimes a separate enquiry)  
- **Pipeline/easements**, electricity transmission, telecoms masts proximity

**Cost (typical)**
- ~**£20–£60** each (varies)

---

## 3) Title-related searches (often ordered, but not “property searches” in the same sense)

### L. HM Land Registry searches
- **Official Copies of Register/Title Plan** (if needed): typically **£3 per document** (online)  
- **OS1 (priority search)**: **£3**  
- **Bankruptcy search (K16)** (per buyer name): **£2** each

These are small individually but are standard conveyancing disbursements.

---

## 4) New-build / leasehold-specific additions (common “recommended extras”)

### M. New-build: planning/building control documentation checks (not a “search”, but crucial)
Your solicitor will review:
- planning permissions/conditions, building control sign-off, CIL/Section 106 implications, roads/sewers adoption agreements (s38/s104)

Sometimes this leads to additional targeted searches (highways/drainage).

---

### N. Leasehold: managing agent / landlord information pack (not a search, but a major cost)
Not strictly a “search”, but a common disbursement:
- **LPE1 pack / landlord/agent replies**: often **£200–£500+** (can be more)

---

## Rough total cost (typical)

### Core “standard” search bundle total (Local + Drainage & Water + Environmental + Chancel)
- **Typical range:** **~£200 to £420**
  - Example mid-range: Local **£180** + Drainage **£60** + Environmental **£60** + Chancel **£25** = **£325**

### With common recommended add-ons (Flood + Coal/mining where relevant)
- Add Flood: **+£25–£45**
- Add Coal/mining: **+£45–£70**
- **Typical total with these:** **~£270 to £535**

### With several targeted extras (planning/neighbourhood + highways + other mining)
- Can reach **~£400 to £700+**, mainly depending on whether you need a specialist **highways** report and multiple mining/ground reports.

---

## Practical advice (what buyers should ask their conveyancer)
1) **Are you doing an official or personal local search, and is it lender-acceptable?**  
2) **Will you order a separate flood report if the environmental search flags risk?** (Often sensible.)  
3) **Do you check mining risk by postcode automatically, or only if the lender asks?**  
4) **If a search is “recommended”, what triggers it for this property?** (Get a clear reason.)  
5) **Confirm disbursements upfront**: ask for a written list with each search cost itemised.

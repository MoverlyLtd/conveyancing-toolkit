For a property purchase of **£350,000** where the application is submitted via the Land Registry’s online portal, the registration fee is **£150.00**.

Detailed breakdown and legal references are provided below.

### 1. The Specific Figure
As you are buying a property (a "Transfer of Whole" for monetary consideration), your application falls under **Scale 1** of the Land Registry’s fee structure.

*   **Purchase Price:** £350,000
*   **Submission Method:** Online Portal (Electronic)
*   **Fee:** **£150.00**

If you were to submit this application by post (paper application), the fee would be significantly higher at **£330.00**. Using the portal provides a discount of over 50%.

### 2. Legal References
The fees are governed by the **Land Registration Fee Order 2021**, which came into effect on 31 January 2022. 

*   **Authority:** The fee is prescribed under **Schedule 1** of the Order.
*   **Scale:** This is a **Scale 1** application (applicable to transfers of registered land for monetary consideration).
*   **Paragraph 2:** This section of the Order outlines the reduced fees for "applications delivered by electronic means."

### 3. Practical Advice and Context

#### Ensure it is a "Transfer of Whole"
The £150 fee assumes you are buying the *entirety* of a registered title. If you are buying a "Transfer of Part" (for example, a plot of land being split from a larger estate or a brand-new build house that hasn't been registered as its own title yet), the fee remains the same (£150), but the processing times and complexity of the application are higher.

#### Difference from SDLT
Do not confuse this registration fee with **Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT)**. For a £350,000 property (assuming you are not a first-time buyer and this is your only property), the SDLT would be £5,000. The Land Registry fee is a separate administrative charge paid to HM Land Registry to update the Title Register.

#### Electronic Document Registration Service (EDRS)
As you mentioned using the "online portal," your solicitor will likely use the **EDRS** or the newer **Digital Registration Service (DRS)**.
*   The portal will automatically calculate the fee based on the price entered.
*   The fee is usually taken by the Land Registry via **Variable Direct Debit** from your solicitor's account on the day the application is submitted.

#### AP1 Form
When your solicitor completes the **Form AP1** (the application to change the register), they will enter the "Value" as £350,000 and the "Fee paid" as £150 in Panel 7.

#### Verification
You can verify these figures at any time using the [official GOV.UK Fee Calculator](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/hm-land-registry-registration-services-fees) or by viewing the **Scale 1** table in the Land Registration Fee Order 2021.

For a **purchase price of £350,000**, registering the **transfer of whole** (typically an **AP1** following a **TR1**) via the **Land Registry online portal** falls under HM Land Registry’s **Scale 1 fees**.

### HM Land Registry fee (online)
- **Price band:** **£200,001 to £500,000**
- **Fee (electronically/online):** **£135**

So, for £350,000, the **Land Registry registration fee is £135** when lodged via the portal.

### The legal basis / where it’s set out
- **Land Registration Fee Order** (currently in force) – **Scale 1** (for transfers for value), with the **electronic fee** applying where the application is lodged electronically (portal/eDRS).
- HMLR’s published fee table mirrors this: **“Scale 1 – electronic applications”** for **£200,001–£500,000 = £135**.

### Practical conveyancing notes (so you don’t get caught out)
- The **£135** is the fee for the **registration application** itself (the AP1). It’s separate from:
  - **Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT)** payable to HMRC (if any), and
  - your solicitor’s fees and search fees.
- If your purchase includes a **new mortgage**, the charge is usually registered **within the same AP1 application** and **does not add a separate Land Registry fee** (it remains a Scale 1 application for the transfer for value).
- The fee can change if it’s **not a “transfer of whole”** (e.g., transfer of part/new title, complex applications), but for a straightforward purchase of a registered freehold/leasehold title, **£135** is the normal figure.

With **95 years unexpired**, you’re in the **GREEN band** — **no practical saleability or mortgageability concern** in most cases.

### What it means for you (estate agent / buyer pool)
- **Mainstream lender friendly**: 95 years is comfortably above the minimums used by the big banks/building societies.
- **Normal buyer pool**: you shouldn’t see meaningful restriction on buyers purely due to lease length.
- **Value impact**: typically **no discount** attributable to lease length at this level.

### One strategic point to be aware of
- The key “cliff edge” is **80 years** (marriage value becomes payable on extension once it drops below 80).
- At 95 years, the buyer has time, but some buyers choose to extend **later** (often when it gets closer to 85–90) to keep it future-proof.

### What *can* still cause issues (even with 95 years)
Lease length is fine, but lenders can still get twitchy about:
- **Ground rent terms** (high rent, aggressive escalation, doubling clauses)
- **Service charge / major works**
- **Cladding / Building Safety Act position** (if relevant)
- **Freeholder/managing agent disputes or arrears**

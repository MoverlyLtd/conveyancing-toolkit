For a solid, UK-focused explanation of what restrictive covenants are and how they work, start with the Law Society’s guidance:

> According to the Law Society, restrictive covenants are obligations written into property deeds that restrict how land can be used (for example, limits on building, use, or alterations). (Source: https://www.lawsociety.org.uk/topics/property/restrictive-covenants)

Then, to see what covenants actually affect a *specific freehold property*, the key places to look are:

1) **HM Land Registry title documents (Title Register and Title Plan)**
- The Title Register often sets out restrictive covenants (or refers you to documents where the covenants are contained).
- You can also see if there are referenced “filed” deeds that include the full wording.
- HM Land Registry is the authoritative source for what is registered against the title: (Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/land-registry)

2) **The original deed/transfer referred to in the register**
- Many covenants are in an older transfer deed, and the register may summarise them or refer to them rather than reproduce the whole text.

For a CQS (Conveyancing Quality Scheme) renewal audit the assessor is essentially checking two things:

1) **You still meet the CQS Core Practice Management Standards (CPMS)** (governance, supervision, risk, file management, training, etc.); and  
2) **Your conveyancing files demonstrate consistent, compliant practice** (including lender requirements, ID/AML, undertakings, reporting, SDLT/LTT/LBTT where relevant, and post-completion).

Below are the key areas typically examined and the **evidence you should have ready**.

---

## 1) Governance, structure and accountability
**What they check**
- Clear responsibility for CQS within the firm (usually a **Senior Responsible Officer (SRO)** and **CQS Compliance Officer** role in practice even if titled differently).
- Management oversight of conveyancing risks, complaints, lender relationships, and client care.
- That policies are actually implemented (not just “on the intranet”).

**Evidence to prepare**
- Current **CQS application/renewal details** and confirmation of key contacts.
- Organisation chart and conveyancing team structure.
- Minutes/notes of partner/management meetings where conveyancing risk/quality is discussed.
- Evidence of **internal audits / file reviews** and actions taken.
- Business continuity plan and IT/cyber governance (if asked).

---

## 2) Supervision and competence (including training)
**What they check**
- Adequate supervision across offices/teams, especially for juniors, high volume work, or remote working.
- Competence and ongoing training, including lender panel requirements and fraud awareness.

**Evidence to prepare**
- Written **supervision policy** (who supervises whom, ratios, escalation routes).
- Job descriptions / role profiles for conveyancing fee earners and support staff.
- Training records:  
  - AML training (initial + refresher)  
  - Conveyancing/lender requirements updates  
  - Fraud/cyber security training  
  - CQS-related training
- Evidence of competency checks (e.g., induction checklists, probation reviews).

---

## 3) File management and “end-to-end” conveyancing process
**What they check**
- Consistent use of file checklists and templates.
- Matter opening to closure is controlled, evidenced, and compliant.
- Critical dates, undertakings, and post-completion are diarised and completed.

**Evidence to prepare**
- Matter opening and progression checklists (sale, purchase, remortgage, leasehold).
- Standard templates: client care letters, terms of business, TA forms process notes, report on title, completion statements, undertakings wording.
- Evidence of diary systems and how key steps are monitored (especially post-completion).
- Closed file archiving policy, retention schedules, and retrieval process.

---

## 4) Client care, transparency and complaints handling
**What they check**
- Client engagement letters and cost information meet current regulatory expectations.
- Complaints are recorded, handled within timescales, and used to improve service.

**Evidence to prepare**
- Client care letter templates showing:
  - scope, costs, disbursements, likely timescales  
  - referral arrangements/introducer disclosures  
  - complaints information and Legal Ombudsman signposting  
  - client due diligence requirements and source of funds expectations
- Complaints policy and **complaints register** (even if nil complaints, show “nil returns” and the process).
- Examples of complaint outcomes and learning actions.

---

## 5) AML, ID and Source of Funds/Wealth (high focus)
**What they check**
- Robust compliance with MLR 2017 and practice that is *visible on file*.
- Risk-based approach: client/matter risk assessment done early and updated.
- Proper handling of **source of funds** (and where relevant source of wealth) with evidence.
- PEP/sanctions screening and record keeping.

**Evidence to prepare**
- Firm-wide AML policies, procedures and controls (including:
  - CDD/EDD, PEPs, sanctions, ongoing monitoring  
  - SoF/SoW guidance  
  - reliance, beneficial owners, corporate clients  
  - SAR/escalation process  
  - record keeping
- **Matter risk assessment template** + sample completed ones on files.
- ID verification evidence (electronic verification logs or certified docs policy).
- SoF evidence examples (bank statements, sale proceeds trail, gifted deposit checks, etc.).
- Register/log of internal AML escalations (if applicable).
- Training logs and MLRO details.

---

## 6) Fraud and cyber risk controls (increasingly scrutinised)
**What they check**
- Controls to prevent payment diversion and CEO fraud.
- Procedures for verifying bank details and communicating securely.
- How you warn clients about cybercrime.

**Evidence to prepare**
- Written **anti-fraud / cyber security** policy relevant to conveyancing.
- Client warnings in engagement documentation and at key stages.
- Procedure and file evidence for:
  - verifying client bank details (call-back procedure)  
  - verifying seller solicitor details (e.g., SRA check)  
  - change of bank detail red flags and escalation  
- Incident log (even if “none”, show the reporting mechanism).

---

## 7) Lender panel compliance and UK Finance Mortgage Lenders’ Handbook
**What they check**
- That you follow the Handbook, lender-specific Part 2 requirements, and report to lenders where required.
- Correct handling of conflicts and dual representation.
- Mortgage conditions satisfied and reported.

**Evidence to prepare**
- Procedure note/checklist showing:
  - how Part 2 is checked and recorded  
  - reporting requirements and escalation  
  - gifted deposits, incentives, discrepancies, occupancy issues, gifted equity, etc.
- Sample **reporting to lender** letters/emails from files (redacted).
- Evidence of managing lender requirements for undertakings, COT (certificate of title), redemption statements, and title defects.

---

## 8) Conflicts of interest and independence
**What they check**
- Conflict checks at opening and ongoing.
- Handling of acting for buyer/seller in same transaction (where relevant), introducers, developer work, and connected parties.

**Evidence to prepare**
- Conflicts policy and conflict checking system evidence (screen prints/logs).
- Examples of conflict risk assessments and waivers where appropriate.
- Introducer/referral agreements and disclosure wording in client care letters.

---

## 9) Financial controls, client account, and undertakings
**What they check**
- Proper control of client money (even if separate COFA oversees this, CQS will still care because conveyancing is high risk).
- How you give, monitor and discharge undertakings.
- Controls around CHAPS/TT payments and authorisation levels.

**Evidence to prepare**
- Client account controls summary:
  - payment authorisations, segregation of duties, daily reconciliations (high level)  
- **Undertakings register** (or method of recording) and sample discharged undertakings on file.
- Procedure for dealing with completion monies, apportionments, retentions.

---

## 10) Leasehold, new build, and complex work (where applicable)
**What they check**
- Enhanced due diligence on higher-risk conveyancing: leaseholds, management companies, new builds, short leases, cladding/EWS1 considerations, rent review clauses (where relevant), estate rentcharges, etc.
- Clear advice to clients and lender reporting.

**Evidence to prepare**
- Leasehold report on title template and completed examples.
- New build checklist: incentives, developer deadlines, warranty, planning, road adoption/sewers, etc.
- Evidence of lender reporting where lease terms trigger it.

---

## 11) Post-completion and registration performance
**What they check**
- SDLT/LTT/LBTT submitted on time (where relevant).
- Registration applications made promptly and requisitions handled.
- Title information documents provided to client and lender as appropriate.

**Evidence to prepare**
- Post-completion checklist and diary controls.
- KPI/management info (if you track it): average time to submit SDLT, register, requisitions rates.
- Sample files showing:
  - SDLT submission and certificate  
  - AP1/FR1, OS1/OS2, priority management  
  - registration completion and reporting to lender/client

---

## 12) File audits: what they usually want to see “on the file”
Expect the assessor to sample a spread (sales/purchases/remortgages; freehold/leasehold; lender/non-lender; different fee earners; different offices). They’ll typically look for:

**On each file**
- Clear **client instructions** and scope confirmation.
- **Client care** issued promptly; cost info and referrals disclosed.
- **Conflict check** evidenced.
- **CDD** completed and matter risk assessment done early.
- **SoF/SoW** evidence proportionate and explained.
- Key legal steps evidenced: searches, enquiries, title review, reporting.
- Lender handbook compliance and reporting trail.
- Completion statement, funds movement, undertakings, redemption.
- Post-completion actions closed out and file properly closed/archived.

**Have ready**
- A list of matters opened/closed in the period so you can pull requested files quickly.
- Redacted exemplar files you can confidently show if asked for “a good example”.

---

# Practical “audit pack” checklist (what to assemble now)
1. **CQS policies pack** (latest versions, dated, with review history): AML, supervision, complaints, conflicts, undertakings, file reviews, fraud/cyber, lender compliance, referrals/introducers, document retention.  
2. **Training matrix + records** (attendance, content, dates).  
3. **File review/audit reports** for the year + evidence actions were implemented.  
4. **Registers/logs**: complaints, undertakings, AML escalations (if kept), breaches/incidents, referrals/introducers list.  
5. **Management information** (if available): post-completion timescales, requisitions, abortive matters, lender reporting issues.  
6. **File sample set** pre-checked internally against your own checklist.

---

## Common “gotchas” to fix before the assessor arrives
- Matter risk assessments missing or completed late.  
- Source of funds evidence obtained but not *explained* (no note tying documents to funds trail).  
- Lender Part 2 not evidenced (no note/screenshot/record).  
- Bank details change warnings and verification steps not documented.  
- Post-completion delays without diary notes explaining why and what was done.  
- Inconsistent use of templates/checklists across fee earners/offices.

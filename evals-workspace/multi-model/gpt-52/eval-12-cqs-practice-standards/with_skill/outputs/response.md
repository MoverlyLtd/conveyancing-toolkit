For a CQS renewal audit, the assessor is usually looking for two things: (1) you have the right governance, policies and controls in place across the conveyancing department, and (2) you can prove they’re actually used (training, file reviews, logs, audits, and examples from live/recent files).

Below are the key areas most commonly scrutinised and the evidence you should have ready to hand.

## 1) Governance & CQS ownership (who is accountable, how you monitor)
**What they check**
- You have a clearly identified CQS Senior Responsible Officer (SRO)
- The SRO’s responsibility for protocol/compliance is real and embedded
- You review CQS compliance periodically and act on issues

**Have ready (evidence pack)**
- SRO appointment/role description (named individual + responsibilities)
- Firm/org chart for conveyancing and supervision lines
- “How we comply with CQS / Conveyancing Protocol” procedure document (or equivalent)
- Records of compliance reviews (meeting minutes, action logs, internal audit reports)
- Evidence of updates made after reviews (version-controlled policies, change notes)

## 2) Supervision & file review (control over quality on actual matters)
**What they check**
- Everyone has a supervisor and escalation route
- File reviews happen at meaningful stages (not just ad hoc) and are recorded
- Issues found are corrected and used for learning

**Have ready**
- Supervision policy (who supervises whom, frequency, sampling method)
- File review checklist/template and completed examples
- A file review schedule (e.g., monthly/quarterly) and a findings/actions log
- Evidence of corrective actions (training notes, updated precedents, feedback emails)

## 3) Client care, costs and communication (front-end compliance)
**What they check**
- Clear costs and service information at the outset
- Clients are kept informed (including delays, chain issues)
- A compliant complaints procedure that’s communicated and used

**Have ready**
- Client care letter/terms templates (including cost info, disbursements, likely extras)
- Communication/progress update policy (frequency, method, recording)
- Complaints policy (and evidence it’s provided to clients and available publicly)
- Complaints register (even if “nil complaints,” keep a nil return log) and outcomes
- Evidence of “learning from complaints” (trend review notes, actions taken)

## 4) Risk management (AML, conflicts, fraud, undertakings, PII)
This is typically one of the heaviest-focus sections.

### AML / ID / source of funds / sanctions
**What they check**
- Up-to-date AML policy and matter risk assessment approach
- Documented ID&V process, SOF/SOW approach, and sanctions screening
- Triggers for enhanced due diligence are understood and used

**Have ready**
- Firm-wide AML policy + firm risk assessment (and conveyancing-specific notes if you have them)
- ID&V procedure (including remote ID approach if used)
- SOF/SOW procedure with examples of acceptable evidence
- Sanctions screening procedure (who, when, how recorded; provider details if applicable)
- Training records for relevant staff (AML + refreshers) and dates

### Conflicts
**What they check**
- Conflicts checks are done at the right time and recorded
- Management of any conflicts is documented

**Have ready**
- Conflicts policy + how checks are performed (system screenshots/process notes)
- Conflicts register/log or sample checks from files

### Fraud and fund diversion controls (incl. bank details)
**What they check**
- Controls around verifying bank details and preventing CEO/BEC scams
- Staff awareness and client warnings

**Have ready**
- Bank details verification procedure (especially pre-completion)
- Template client warnings re: phishing/bank detail changes
- Fraud policy and staff training/awareness records (phishing training etc.)
- Evidence on files that verification steps are recorded

### Professional indemnity insurance
**Have ready**
- Current PII certificate/schedule and confirmation it covers conveyancing scope

### Undertakings
**What they check**
- You track undertakings centrally and close them off

**Have ready**
- Undertakings policy and an undertakings register with status tracking

## 5) Information security & data protection (cyber, GDPR, continuity)
**What they check**
- Practical cyber controls and staff training (not just a policy)
- Secure methods for transmitting sensitive info
- Business continuity is documented and (ideally) tested

**Have ready**
- Cyber/IT security policy (access control, MFA, patching, device security)
- Cyber training records (phishing, safe handling of completion monies, BEC awareness)
- Secure email/portal process description (what you use, when you use it)
- Data protection policy, retention policy, and any DPIAs if relevant
- Business continuity plan (BCP) + evidence of a test or review
- Cyber Essentials certificate if you hold it (not mandatory, but helpful)

## 6) Training & competence (CQS and internal)
**What they check**
- Mandatory CQS training is completed and in date
- Staff have ongoing competence/CPD appropriate to their role
- New starters are inducted properly

**Have ready**
- CQS training completion certificates (fee earners and relevant support staff)
- CPD/training plan + training matrix (who has done what and when)
- Induction checklist for new conveyancing staff
- Records of regulatory update training (e.g., AML/sanctions updates, SDLT/HR changes)

## 7) Conveyancing process & version control (using current documents)
**What they check**
- You have a documented conveyancing process aligned to the Protocol (in practice)
- Use of current property forms/standard conditions and controlled precedents
- Clear exchange/completion controls and chain management approach

**Have ready**
- Process map/checklist aligned to Protocol stages (your workflow document)
- Precedent/version control procedure (how updates are made and rolled out)
- Exchange/completion checklists and authorisation process (who signs off)
- Chain management notes/procedure and examples of recording key chain info on files

## 8) File/case management, key dates, and post-completion
**What they check**
- Key dates are diarised and monitored (exchange, completion, SDLT, registration, undertakings)
- Post-completion is controlled and recorded
- File retention/storage is compliant and consistent
- Internal file audits occur

**Have ready**
- Case management system overview (how milestones/diary alerts work)
- Post-completion checklist (SDLT submission, AP1, requisitions, OS1/priority, lender discharge)
- File audit schedule + recent audit reports and actions
- File retention and destruction policy + evidence of adherence (where possible)

---

# Practical “audit-ready” pack (what to assemble now)
If you do nothing else, prepare a single folder (digital) with:
1. SRO details + governance/supervision chart  
2. Core policies: AML, sanctions, conflicts, fraud, cyber, complaints, data protection, BCP, undertakings, file retention  
3. Training matrix + CQS certificates + AML/cyber refreshers  
4. File review process + last 2–3 cycles of file review evidence and actions log  
5. Complaints register + learning review notes  
6. A small set of example files (recent and typical) showing the controls in action (ID/SOF, sanctions check, client care/costs, exchange/completion checklist, post-completion)

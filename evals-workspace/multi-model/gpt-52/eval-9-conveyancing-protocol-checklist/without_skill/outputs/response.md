Below is a practical, **Law Society Conveyancing Protocol**-aligned checklist for the point **just before exchange** (assuming a standard residential sale/purchase in England & Wales). I’ve grouped it by what you, your solicitor, and (where relevant) the seller’s solicitor should have tied down. Use this to sanity-check that the file is “exchange-ready”.

> Note: the Protocol is a best-practice framework; not every transaction follows it perfectly (e.g., new-build, auction, unregistered land, complex titles), but the checks below are the core “exchange conditions”.

---

## 1) Contract pack and “deal terms” are fully agreed
**You should ensure:**
- **Names/parties and property address/title number** are correct.
- **Price** is correct and matches your mortgage offer (if any).
- **Deposit** amount is agreed (typically 10% unless varied) and you can fund it.
- **Completion date** is:
  - fixed and agreed in writing,
  - realistic for removals/mortgage drawdown,
  - aligned with any related sale/purchase in a chain.
- **Inclusions/exclusions** (fixtures & fittings / TA10) agreed:
  - anything you expect to stay (white goods, carpets, light fittings, sheds, etc.) is explicitly included,
  - anything the seller is removing is clearly excluded.
- **Special conditions** in the contract are understood and acceptable (e.g., extras on new build, retention clauses, early access, conditional completion).

**Protocol expectation:** terms are agreed before exchange; no “to be confirmed later” items that affect value or use.

---

## 2) Title and “what you’re buying” is clear and acceptable
Ask your solicitor to confirm they are satisfied with:

### A. Title investigation
- **Title plan boundaries**: any mismatch with what you think you’re buying (fences, parking space, garden strips).
- **Rights benefiting the property**: access, parking, bin store access, rights for services.
- **Rights burdening the property**: shared access, neighbours’ rights of way, obligations to contribute to maintenance.
- **Restrictive covenants**: anything that would affect your intended use (extensions, business use, pets if leasehold, caravans, etc.).
- **Easements and service media**: confirmation the property has legal rights for water/drainage/electricity/gas/telecoms, and where pipes/drains run (especially if building works are planned).
- **Title defects**: flying freehold, lack of rights, missing deeds, adverse possession issues—plus how they’re being handled (often via indemnity insurance or deed of variation).

### B. If leasehold (or share of freehold)
Before exchange you should have:
- **Lease reviewed**: remaining term, ground rent, rent review clauses, service charge provisions, repair obligations, alteration rules, subletting/pets restrictions.
- **LPE1/management pack** received and acceptable: service charge accounts, planned major works, insurance, arrears, disputes.
- **Freeholder/managing agent consents** confirmed if needed:
  - Licence to Assign,
  - Deed of Covenant,
  - Notice of Transfer/Charge requirements and fees,
  - share certificate arrangements (share of freehold).
- **Ground rent**: confirm it’s not onerous (lender and resale risk).

**Protocol expectation:** title and lease issues are raised, answered, and resolved (or you knowingly accept the risk) before exchange.

---

## 3) Searches and enquiries are completed (or you’ve knowingly waived something)
### A. Searches
Typical pre-exchange position:
- **Local authority search** back and acceptable (planning/building regs, highways, enforcement).
- **Drainage & water** confirms mains connections and sewer location (important for extensions).
- **Environmental search** acceptable (contamination/flood/landfill risk).
- **Flood risk** considered (especially if near water or in known flood areas).
- Any **area-specific searches** where relevant (coal/mining, HS2, commons/village green, chancel, etc.).

If any searches are outstanding, you should understand:
- what the risk is,
- whether your lender will allow exchange without them,
- whether you’re relying on a **search indemnity policy** (and what it covers/doesn’t).

### B. Enquiries
Confirm:
- **All key enquiries are answered** in writing to your solicitor’s satisfaction.
- Any **inconsistencies** between TA6 (Property Info Form) and reality are resolved (e.g., neighbour disputes, boundaries, parking arrangements, works done).
- **Guarantees/certificates** are provided where expected:
  - FENSA/Certass for windows/doors,
  - Electrical (EICR / Part P),
  - Gas safety / boiler service history (not always mandatory but useful),
  - Building regulations completion certificates,
  - Planning permissions for extensions,
  - NHBC/warranty for new build,
  - damp proofing/roofing guarantees (check transferability).
- **If the seller has carried out alterations**, you have either:
  - proper consents (planning/building regs/party wall where applicable), or
  - a clear, lender-acceptable solution (sometimes indemnity—though note indemnity is often invalid if you approach the council).

**Protocol expectation:** no unanswered “material” enquiries at exchange.

---

## 4) Mortgage and finance are exchange-ready
If you’re using a mortgage, confirm:

- **Formal mortgage offer** issued and in date.
- **All offer conditions** satisfied (e.g., retention works, gifted deposit evidence, AML/source of funds, occupancy declarations).
- Your solicitor has reported to the lender as required and is able to **request funds** in time for completion.
- **Buildings insurance** is arranged from exchange (common requirement on freehold; for leasehold usually covered by block policy—confirm).
- **Deposit funds** are cleared in your solicitor’s client account (ask what deadline they need for cleared funds).

**Protocol expectation:** funding arrangements are secure before you become legally committed.

---

## 5) Seller’s ability to complete is credible (especially in a chain)
Your solicitor should have checked (or be checking) that the seller can actually give you clean title on completion:

- **Redemption figures**: seller’s solicitor has obtained up-to-date redemption statements for existing mortgages/charges and undertakes to redeem them.
- **Vacant possession**: contract states vacant possession (if that’s what you agreed) and there are no undisclosed occupiers/tenants.
- **Chain readiness**: realistic chain status—ideally everyone has signed, deposits lined up, and a completion date agreed up and down the chain.
- **If seller is buying onward**: risk of delay is understood; consider whether you need contractual protections (rare in standard deals, but worth discussing if there are known issues).

**Protocol expectation:** solicitors manage chain communication and reduce risk of last-minute failure.

---

## 6) Practical completion mechanics are set up
Confirm before exchange:

- **Signed contract** is in your solicitor’s hands (physically or validly e-signed).
- **Transfer deed (TR1)** is agreed in draft (usually signed nearer completion, but it should at least be settled).
- **Completion arrangements**: how keys are released (usually via estate agent on solicitor-to-solicitor confirmation).
- **Apportionments**: ground rent/service charge apportionments understood (leasehold).
- **Completion statement**: you’ve seen a clear estimate showing:
  - purchase price,
  - deposit paid,
  - SDLT estimate,
  - Land Registry fees,
  - legal fees/disbursements,
  - mortgage advance,
  - balance to complete.

**Protocol expectation:** no ambiguity about the steps from exchange to completion.

---

## 7) Insurance, risk, and “what happens if…”
Before exchange you should understand:

- **Risk passes at exchange** (often buyer bears risk under Standard Conditions; check your contract/lease position).
- What happens if **completion is delayed**:
  - interest payable,
  - notice to complete procedure,
  - potential loss of deposit / damages in worst cases.
- Any **indemnity policies** being put in place:
  - what they cover,
  - exclusions,
  - who pays,
  - whether your lender requires a particular wording/limit.

**Protocol expectation:** client is advised on the consequences of exchange and risk allocation.

---

## 8) Final “red flag” checks to ask your solicitor explicitly
Ask for a clear “exchange report” answer to these:

1. **Are all searches back and acceptable? If not, which are missing and what’s the risk?**  
2. **Are there any title defects, and if so how are they being resolved?**  
3. **Is the lender satisfied and are all mortgage conditions met?**  
4. **Do we have written confirmation of buildings insurance from exchange (if required)?**  
5. **Are there any unresolved disputes, notices, or neighbour issues disclosed?**  
6. **For leasehold: are there any arrears/major works/ground rent issues that could affect value or mortgageability?**  
7. **Is the completion date realistic and agreed across the chain?**  
8. **Do you recommend exchange today—yes or no—and why?**

It depends on each lender’s **minimum unexpired lease term** requirement at completion (UK Finance Lenders’ Handbook Part 1, para **5.14.1** says you must check the lender’s **Part 2**).

### Halifax
- **Likely problem at 72 years.** Halifax commonly requires a higher minimum unexpired term than 72 years, so you would usually expect this to **fail Halifax’s lease term criteria** unless a lease extension is completed (or they give a specific written exception).
- Practical conveyancing point: if the term is below the lender’s minimum, you **must report to the lender and await instructions** before exchange/completion.

### Nationwide
- **Likely problem at 72 years** for the same reason: Nationwide typically requires a minimum term above this level.
- Usually the solution is **lease extension** (often to get comfortably above the minimum at completion and/or at the end of the mortgage term).

### Barclays
- **More likely than the above to be considered**, but **still may not be acceptable** depending on Barclays’ Part 2 minimum and how their rule is framed (some lenders use “minimum now” plus “minimum at mortgage end” tests).
- If it doesn’t meet their stated minimums, it must be **referred**.

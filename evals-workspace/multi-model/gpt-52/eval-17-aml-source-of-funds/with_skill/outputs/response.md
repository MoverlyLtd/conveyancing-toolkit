Below is a practical, SRA/MLR 2017 (risk-based) source of funds pack for a £750,000 purchase funded by **£200,000 savings + £100,000 parental gift + £450,000 mortgage**. You should obtain evidence for **each element** and record on file why it is sufficient given your matter risk assessment.

---

## 1) Savings element — **£200,000**

### What to request (typical baseline)
1. **Bank/building society statements – usually 12 months**
   - For the account(s) where the savings have accrued and are currently held.
   - Must show **client name**, account number/sort code, and the **accumulation pattern** (not just a closing balance).
2. If savings are spread across accounts: **statements for all relevant accounts** and (ideally) an explanation of what is held where.

### What to do if the statements show “spikes” / large credits
- For **any large lump sums** (e.g., transfers in, investment encashments, bonuses, sale of shares, etc.) within that period, you should **evidence the underlying source** for each material credit, for example:
  - **Investment sale/ISA encashment**: broker/ISA provider statement + bank statement showing receipt.
  - **Bonus**: payslip/P60 + bank statement showing the credit.
  - **Sale of an asset**: completion invoice/receipt/contract + bank statement.
  - **Transfer from another personal account**: statements for the sending account to show where it came from.

### What you’re evidencing on file
- That the client **controls the account**, the funds are **consistent with their known income/wealth profile**, and the **£200k can be traced** to legitimate activity.

---

## 2) Gifted deposit — **£100,000 from parents**

Treat this as a separate funding element requiring **CDD on the giftors** plus **source of the gift funds**.

### A) Identity evidence (for each parent contributing)
1. **Photo ID**: passport or driving licence (plus any required verification per your firm’s process).
2. **Address evidence** (if you require it under your CDD process): e.g., utility bill/bank statement (as per your policy).

### B) Source of funds evidence (giftors)
1. **Giftors’ bank statements – typically 3–6 months**
   - Showing they **hold the £100,000** (or their share of it) and the **ability to transfer** it.
2. If the £100,000 appears as a recent incoming credit to the parents’ account(s), **trace that back** too (e.g., investment redemption, sale proceeds, pension lump sum).

### C) Gift documentation
1. **Signed gift letter** (or deed, if your firm uses one) confirming at minimum:
   - Amount gifted (**£100,000**)
   - Donor(s) names and relationship to the buyer
   - The gift is **non-refundable** / **no repayment**
   - The giftors will have **no beneficial interest** / no charge over the property
2. **Evidence of relationship** (only if not obvious): e.g., birth certificate or an alternative your firm accepts.

### D) Lender disclosure (important)
- Because there is a **mortgage**, ensure the **gift is disclosed to the lender** and that you comply with the relevant **UK Finance Lenders’ Handbook (Part 2) requirements** for that lender (many require confirmation of the gift and the donors’ details).

### What you’re evidencing on file
- The **parents are the true donors**, the money is theirs, the **origin of their funds is understood**, and the “gift” is not an undisclosed **loan** or third-party interest.

---

## 3) Mortgage element — **£450,000**

### What to obtain
1. **Mortgage offer** from an **FCA-regulated lender** (copy on file).
2. Confirmation that the mortgage advance will be coming **directly from the lender** to your client account in the usual way (part of completion mechanics rather than a separate SoF trail).

### AML point
- You do **not** generally need to “source” the mortgage advance itself beyond having the **mortgage offer** (the lender has its own CDD/underwriting). Your AML focus remains on the **deposit and any balance** coming from the client/third parties.

---

## 4) Matter-level “tie up” checks (recommended under a risk-based approach)

1. **Funds breakdown and tally**
   - Record clearly: **£200k + £100k + £450k = £750k** (plus note how SDLT/fees are being funded, and evidence those funds too if coming from the client).
2. **Bank account for completion monies**
   - Obtain evidence of the **client’s sending account details** (and in practice you’ll see this on statements) and ensure incoming funds match the expected sources.
3. **Ongoing monitoring (Reg 28(12))**
   - If funding changes (new gift, overseas transfer, crypto liquidation, last-minute account switch), re-assess and obtain new evidence.

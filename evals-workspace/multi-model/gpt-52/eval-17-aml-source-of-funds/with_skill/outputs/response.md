Below is a **risk-based** (not tick-box) source of funds pack you’d usually obtain on an SRA conveyancing file for a £750,000 purchase funded by **£200k savings + £100k parental gift + £450k mortgage**. Evidence should be gathered and assessed **for each element** and you should apply **ongoing monitoring under Regulation 28(12)** throughout the retainer (e.g., if the gift amount changes, funds come from a different account, or extra third-party funds appear, you re-assess and re-evidence).

## 1) £450,000 Mortgage (FCA-regulated lender)
**What to obtain**
- **Mortgage offer** (and any subsequent revised offer).

**AML point**
- You generally **do not “source” the mortgage advance** as funds—the lender is itself regulated and will have done its own CDD—but you must still verify the **deposit/equity elements** separately and ensure the overall funding picture is coherent.

**Practical checks**
- Ensure the offer is consistent with the transaction (borrower names, property address, loan amount).
- If the lender has conditions about the deposit (e.g., “no gifts/only family gifts”), ensure your gift evidence aligns and **the lender is informed** in line with the UK Finance Lenders’ Handbook requirements.

## 2) £200,000 From Client Savings
**What to obtain (typical baseline)**
- **12 months’ bank/building society statements** for the account(s) where the savings have built up (showing **accumulation pattern**, not just a snapshot).
- If the £200k is spread across multiple accounts: **statements for all relevant accounts**.
- If there are **large credits/lump sums** within the statement period: **trace each material lump sum** back to its own origin (e.g. bonus/pension lump sum/sale of investments/inheritance), with supporting documents for that sub-source plus the statement showing it landing.

**What you are checking**
- Account holder name matches the client.
- The build-up is consistent with the client’s profile (occupation/income/source of wealth).
- No unexplained cash deposits, circular transfers, or last-minute third-party injections.

**If savings were moved recently**
- Obtain the **chain of statements** showing the movement from prior account(s) into the “holding” account used for the conveyance.

## 3) £100,000 Gift From Parents (Gifted Deposit)
You must treat the parents as a separate source-of-funds line item and complete checks on them as giftors (separate from the buyer’s CDD).

**You should obtain all of the following (minimum):**
1) **Giftor ID (each parent contributing)**
- Passport or photocard driving licence (+ address evidence if required by your firm’s policy/risk assessment).

2) **Giftor bank statements**
- **3–6 months** statements (for each parent’s relevant account(s)) showing:
  - They hold/accumulated the funds; and
  - The **£100k** is available and (when sent) leaves their account to the buyer/solicitor.
- If the £100k itself came from a recent lump sum into the parents’ account, **trace that lump sum** (e.g., sale proceeds, investment liquidation) with documentary support.

3) **Signed gift letter**
Must expressly confirm:
- Amount: **£100,000**
- Relationship: parents of the buyer
- **No obligation to repay** (i.e., not a loan)
- **No interest in the property** (no beneficial interest / no occupation rights unless lender agrees)
- Whether it’s from one parent or both, and (ideally) the sending account(s)

**Also (often needed depending on risk/lender)**
- **Proof of relationship** if not obvious (names differ, etc.)—e.g., birth certificate or a simple corroborating document (your risk-based call).
- Confirm the lender’s position and **disclose the gift to the lender** where required (usually is).

## 4) Putting it together (file-level coherence)
- A simple **funds breakdown schedule** on file showing the £750,000 total and where each element comes from:
  - £450,000 mortgage (offer)
  - £200,000 buyer savings (12 months statements + explanations for material credits)
  - £100,000 parental gift (ID + 3–6 months statements + gift letter)
- Ensure the **expected monies in** match what you actually receive (and from whom). If completion monies arrive from an unexpected account or third party, treat that as a **Reg 28(12) trigger** and re-do SoF on the new element.

## 5) If any element is overseas (even partly)
If either the buyer’s savings or parents’ gift originates abroad, add:
- **SWIFT/wire transfer evidence** for the incoming funds (showing sending bank/account holder, receiving bank/account holder, amounts/dates).
- **Source country risk assessment** (FATF status, sanctions screening, corruption risk as part of your matter risk assessment).
- **Currency conversion evidence** (FX contract note/bank conversion confirmation showing original currency, rate, and GBP equivalent).
- In most overseas scenarios, apply **enhanced due diligence (EDD)** and document why it’s appropriate.

## 6) Reporting & record-keeping (must be on your compliance radar)
- **Record keeping:** retain CDD/SoF/SoW and supporting records for **5 years** from the end of the business relationship/transaction (per MLR 2017).
- **SAR framework:** if anything gives rise to **suspicion** (or reasonable grounds), you must escalate internally to the **MLRO/nominated officer** and, if appropriate, submit a **SAR to the NCA**. Do not tip off the client (POCA s.333A).
- **DAML:** where you have suspicion but need to continue acting, your MLRO may need to seek **DAML (consent)** from the NCA; note the **7 working day** notice period and potential **31 calendar day** moratorium if refused.

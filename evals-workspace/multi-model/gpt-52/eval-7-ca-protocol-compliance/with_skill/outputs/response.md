Under the Conveyancing Association Protocol (5th Edition), you handle this using the **Planning Permission decision tree** in **CA 4.0 Planning Permission** (and typically mirror it with the Building Regulations tree if relevant).

### 1) Treat it as “no planning permission obtained (evidence missing)”
If there are **no planning permission records** for an extension built in **2021**, you proceed on the basis that **permission may not have been obtained / cannot be evidenced** (CA 4.0).

### 2) Time since works drives the next step
Because the works were done in **2021**, they are **well under 4 years** old.

Under **CA 4.0**:
- **Works completed <12 months ago** → look for **regularisation/retrospective consent or a bespoke indemnity solution** (CA 4.0).
- **Works completed >12 months but <4 years** → **obtain planning indemnity insurance** (CA 4.0).

So for a 2021 extension *today*, it usually falls into the **“>12 months but <4 years”** bracket **only if you are still within 4 years of completion**; otherwise it moves into **“>4 years”** (where the CA tree says no further action on planning enforcement time limits, subject to the change-of-use caveat).

### 3) You must also warn the right people
The CA Protocol also expects that **where permission is not evidenced**, you:
- **advise the client** of the risk; and
- **advise the valuer/surveyor** so they can confirm the works appear structurally sound (CA 4.0).

### 4) Don’t create an insurance problem
While the Protocol doesn’t spell out “don’t contact the council” in the decision tree wording, the practical application is consistent with standard conveyancing practice: **if you’re heading toward indemnity insurance, avoid approaches to the local authority** that could prejudice cover. (This sits alongside the CA approach of keeping enquiries proportionate—see **CA 2.0 Additional Enquiries**.)

### 5) Building Regulations is a parallel check
Even though you asked about planning, an extension also usually triggers **Building Regulations**. The CA Protocol has a separate tree for this (also in **CA 4.0 Building Regulations**), and for post‑1984 works with no completion certificate it expects you to **advise client/lender and have the surveyor consider safety**, and often **indemnity** depending on age/structural nature.

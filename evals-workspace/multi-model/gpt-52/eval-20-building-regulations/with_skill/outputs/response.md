Advise on the basis that 2018 replacement windows should have been **self-certified** under a competent person scheme (most commonly **FENSA** or **CERTASS**) or else have **local authority Building Control** approval. Your practical options are:

## 1) Do not contact the local authority (yet)
**Do not** suggest the seller/buyer/agent “just checks” with Building Control. Any contact can make **building regulations indemnity insurance unavailable**, so you need to choose strategy first.

## 2) Try to obtain the missing compliance evidence (best first step)
### (a) Check competent person scheme records
Ask the seller to:
- identify the installer and provide any paperwork/invoice (often shows the scheme), and
- obtain a duplicate certificate if it was registered.

You/they can also search **competentperson.co.uk** (covers FENSA/CERTASS and others).  
If found, a scheme certificate is effectively the evidence you need—no further building control sign-off required.

### (b) If the installer is still trading
Ask them to confirm in writing:
- whether they were registered with FENSA/CERTASS (or similar) at the time, and
- whether the installation was notified, and if so, to re-issue the certificate.

## 3) If no certificate can be produced: decide between indemnity vs regularisation
### Option A — Building Regulations Indemnity Insurance (common for windows)
Usually the quickest/most proportionate solution for missing window certs, provided:
- **no one has contacted the council** about the windows, and
- the **lender accepts indemnity** (check UK Finance Lenders’ Handbook Part 2 for that lender).

Key points to explain to the buyer:
- Indemnity covers **risk of enforcement/diminution in value**, not defective windows/workmanship.
- It’s typically inexpensive and arranged quickly.
- The policy should be in the buyer’s name and benefit successors/mortgagee.

### Option B — Regularisation (retrospective approval)
If:
- the lender insists on a certificate, or
- someone has already contacted Building Control (so indemnity is off the table),  
then the seller would need to apply for **regularisation**. This is slower, costs more, and may require inspection and remedial works.

## 4) What I would recommend you do now (conveyancing steps)
1. **Enquire**: request installer details, invoice, and any scheme paperwork; ask seller to obtain duplicate certificate.  
2. **Search**: competentperson.co.uk for a record.  
3. **Check lender**: confirm whether lender will accept **building regs indemnity** for missing window certification (most do).  
4. **If no certificate** and lender accepts: proceed with **indemnity insurance** and ensure **no local authority contact**.  
5. **If lender won’t accept** or LA already contacted: require **regularisation** (or renegotiate/retention).

Below is what CQS (Conveyancing Quality Scheme) expects you to have in place when a new **fee earner** joins a **CQS-accredited practice**. CQS doesn’t prescribe a single “onboarding checklist” in the way some lenders do, but there are clear **CQS Protocol / Core Practice Management Standards (CPMS)** expectations around **competence, supervision, training, file controls and client care** that you must evidence in the event of an audit/assessment.

## 1) Allocate responsibility and record the joiner internally (CQS roles)
**CQS requires you to have named people doing specific things**, and onboarding should show how the new joiner fits your structure.

- **Senior Responsible Officer (SRO)**: remains accountable overall for CQS compliance and culture.
- **CQS Compliance Officer (CO)**: ensures the practice complies with the CQS Scheme Rules/Protocol/CPMS.
- **Supervising fee earner**: CQS expects proper supervision arrangements for conveyancing work.

**Onboarding action:** document (even in a brief note) who will supervise the new fee earner, what their authority level is (e.g., whether they can exchange/complete without sign-off), and how escalations work.

## 2) Competence, induction and training (CPMS + Protocol)
CQS expects a firm to ensure staff are competent for their role and trained in the firm’s systems and the CQS way of working.

### Minimum CQS-specific areas your induction should cover
- The **CQS Conveyancing Protocol** and your firm’s process maps/checklists aligned to it.
- **Client care** standards and communication expectations (including managing expectations and keeping clients updated).
- **Identity/AML** procedures and source-of-funds/source-of-wealth approach as applied in conveyancing.
- **Undertakings**: when they may be given, approval limits, and recording/monitoring.
- **File opening standards**: risk assessment, conflict checks, lender checks, panel requirements, retainer scope.
- **Use of standard documents** (TA forms, title review approach, reporting to client/lender, completion statements, etc.).
- **Supervision and file review**: your firm’s file review cadence and quality controls.
- **Information security & data protection** (CQS expects robust file/record controls; also dovetails with SRA and UK GDPR).

**Evidence to keep:** induction plan, attendance record, and any competency sign-off or probation review notes.

## 3) Mandatory CQS training requirement: annual update
CQS has an ongoing training requirement for conveyancing staff.

- The firm must ensure relevant staff complete the **CQS annual conveyancing update training** (delivered via the Law Society’s CQS training/online modules).
- New joiners should be **scheduled** to complete it as part of onboarding if they have not already completed the current year’s update.

**Onboarding action:** check whether they have already completed the current CQS annual update (obtain evidence if possible), and diarise completion if not.

## 4) Verify practising status and suitability (fit and proper / regulatory checks)
While not uniquely “CQS-only”, it matters to CQS because it ties directly to competence and risk management.

- Confirm **practising certificate/authorisation** (if solicitor/CLC/other) and any **conditions/restrictions**.
- Check any **disciplinary history** disclosed/known and assess supervision level accordingly.
- Ensure they understand and will follow your CQS-aligned policies (AML, undertakings, client money, conflicts).

**Evidence:** copy of practising certificate / authorisation check, onboarding declaration, policy read-and-understood acknowledgements.

## 5) Supervision arrangements must be real and demonstrable (CPMS)
CQS is very focused on whether conveyancing work is properly supervised and checked.

**Onboarding should set out:**
- Who reviews their files and how often (e.g., first X files, then monthly random reviews).
- Whether they can:
  - exchange contracts,
  - submit COTs/request funds,
  - give undertakings,
  - sign certificates of title,
  - deal with lender-specific steps
  without countersignature.
- Escalation triggers (title defects, incentives, leasehold complexity, new build, unregistered, gifted deposits, etc.).

**Evidence:** supervision plan + file review records that start from day one.

## 6) Ensure they follow your CQS file controls and checklists (Protocol alignment)
CQS expects a consistent, protocol-driven approach.

**Onboarding requirement in practice:**
- Give the joiner access to (and training on) your:
  - matter plans/workflows,
  - checklists (sale/purchase/leasehold/new build),
  - standard reporting templates,
  - lender reporting processes,
  - completion/post-completion checklists.
- Make it clear that **deviations must be justified and recorded** on file.

**Evidence:** training record + early file audits showing correct use of controls.

## 7) Update your CQS “people records” and be audit-ready
CQS assessments/audits often ask for evidence that staff doing conveyancing:
- are trained,
- are supervised,
- understand the Protocol/CPMS,
- follow the firm’s documented processes.

**Onboarding action:** add the new fee earner to:
- your training matrix/record,
- supervision log,
- list of conveyancing staff covered by your CQS policies and file review programme.

## 8) If the joiner is a conveyancing “manager” or will impact your CQS application details
CQS expects your practice information to remain accurate. If the new joiner is taking on a material role (e.g., heading the department, becoming SRO/CO, or materially changing supervision capacity), you should consider whether you need to:
- **update internal CQS governance records**, and
- ensure the firm can still meet supervision ratios and quality controls.

(There isn’t always a requirement to “notify CQS” for every hire, but you must be able to show continued compliance and accurate governance.)

---

# Practical onboarding checklist (CQS-focused)
1. Confirm role, supervision, authority limits (exchange/COT/undertakings).  
2. Induct on CQS Protocol + your workflows/checklists/templates.  
3. Induct on AML/ID/SOF/SOW and lender reporting expectations.  
4. Induct on undertakings policy and sign-off thresholds.  
5. Ensure annual CQS conveyancing update training completed/scheduled; record evidence.  
6. Start file review programme immediately (e.g., first 5–10 files 100% reviewed).  
7. Add to training matrix and supervision log; keep records audit-ready.

1) Is the new fee earner a **solicitor, licensed conveyancer, or paralegal**?  
2) Will they be **signing certificates of title** or operating under someone else’s sign-off?  
3) Are they joining from another **CQS firm**, and have they completed the **current year’s CQS update** already?

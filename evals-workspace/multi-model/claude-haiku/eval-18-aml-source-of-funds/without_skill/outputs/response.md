When a client is purchasing a property in the UK with funds from overseas, such as Dubai, there are additional Anti-Money Laundering (AML) checks that should be carried out beyond the standard source of funds requirements. Here are some of the key additional checks:

1. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD):
   - Obtain more detailed information about the client's source of wealth and source of funds.
   - Require more extensive documentation, such as bank statements, payslips, or evidence of business activities, to verify the legitimacy of the funds.
   - Conduct additional research on the client's background, including their profession, business activities, and any potential links to high-risk or politically exposed persons (PEPs).

2. Verification of Overseas Bank Accounts:
   - Obtain copies of the client's overseas bank statements covering a reasonable period (e.g., 3-6 months) to verify the source of the funds.
   - Ensure that the bank accounts are held in the client's name or can be directly linked to the client.
   - Confirm the legitimacy of the overseas bank accounts by contacting the financial institutions directly, if necessary.

3. Sanctions and Embargo Checks:
   - Conduct thorough checks to ensure that the client, their associates, or the source of funds are not subject to any international sanctions or embargoes, particularly those related to the UAE or other Middle Eastern countries.
   - Use reputable databases and screening tools to identify any potential sanctions or politically exposed persons (PEPs) associated with the client or the source of funds.

4. Compliance with UK Money Laundering Regulations:
   - Ensure that the client completes the necessary AML and Know Your Client (KYC) forms and provides all the required documentation, as per the UK's money laundering regulations.
   - Carefully review and verify the information provided by the client to identify any discrepancies or red flags.

5. Monitoring and Reporting:
   - Maintain ongoing monitoring of the transaction and the client's activities throughout the conveyancing process.
   - Be alert to any suspicious or unusual activities and report any concerns to the appropriate authorities, such as the National Crime Agency (NCA), in accordance with the UK's anti-money laundering regulations.

It is crucial to exercise a heightened level of diligence when dealing with clients who are purchasing UK properties with overseas funds, as these transactions may be at a higher risk of money laundering or other financial crimes. By conducting these additional AML checks, you can help ensure compliance with the relevant regulations and mitigate potential risks.

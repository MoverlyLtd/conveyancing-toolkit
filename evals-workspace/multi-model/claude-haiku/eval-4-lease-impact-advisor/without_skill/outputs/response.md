As an estate agent listing a flat with 68 years remaining on the lease, there are several important factors you should be aware of:

1. Lease Length:
   - A flat with only 68 years remaining on the lease is considered a short lease.
   - Shorter leases can be problematic for buyers, as they may have difficulty obtaining mortgage financing and the flat's value can start to diminish as the lease term gets shorter.

2. Lease Extension:
   - In the UK, leaseholders have the statutory right to extend their lease by an additional 90 years, subject to certain conditions.
   - Extending the lease can be a complex and costly process, involving legal fees and potentially a significant premium payment to the freeholder.
   - You should advise potential buyers that they may need to factor in the cost of a lease extension when considering the property.

3. Property Value:
   - The remaining lease length can have a significant impact on the property's value.
   - As a general rule, the shorter the remaining lease, the lower the property's value.
   - With a lease of only 68 years, the property's value may be significantly discounted compared to a similar flat with a longer lease.

4. Mortgage Availability:
   - Lenders may be more cautious about providing mortgages for properties with short leases, as the shorter term can make the property less desirable and potentially more difficult to sell in the future.
   - Potential buyers may face challenges in securing mortgage financing, which could limit the pool of interested buyers.

5. Disclosure:
   - You should be fully transparent about the remaining lease term and any potential costs associated with a lease extension.
   - Disclose this information upfront to potential buyers, as it can significantly impact their decision-making process and the overall transaction.

By understanding the implications of the short lease and advising potential buyers accordingly, you can help ensure a smooth and informed sales process. It's important to emphasize the need for professional legal advice and the potential costs associated with extending the lease to ensure buyers make an informed decision.

As a conveyancing professional, here are the key areas the CQS (Conveyancing Quality Scheme) assessor is likely to check during your firm's renewal audit, and the evidence you should have ready:

1. Client Care:
   - Evidence of client engagement letters outlining the scope of work, costs, and service standards.
   - Copies of client communication logs, documenting interactions and progress updates.
   - Procedures for handling client complaints and feedback.

2. File Management:
   - Organized physical and electronic client files, with clear documentation of all key activities and decisions.
   - Evidence of file reviews and quality assurance checks.
   - Procedures for file archiving and destruction.

3. Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Compliance:
   - Client due diligence records, including identification checks and source of funds verification.
   - AML risk assessment procedures and policies.
   - Staff training records on AML requirements.

4. Regulatory Compliance:
   - Copies of your firm's professional indemnity insurance and the Solicitors Regulation Authority (SRA) registration.
   - Evidence of compliance with SRA Accounts Rules, including reconciliation records and handling of client money.
   - Procedures for identifying and managing conflicts of interest.

5. Information Security and Data Protection:
   - Cybersecurity measures, such as firewall configurations, data backup procedures, and staff training on data protection.
   - Compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including privacy notices, data subject access requests, and breach reporting procedures.

6. Case Management:
   - Examples of completed transactions, demonstrating efficient and diligent handling of matters.
   - Records of ongoing cases, with clear progress tracking and timely communication with clients.
   - Procedures for managing transaction completions and post-completion activities.

7. Continuous Improvement:
   - Evidence of regular staff training and development activities.
   - Records of client feedback and how it has been used to improve services.
   - Procedures for monitoring and reviewing your firm's policies and processes.

To prepare for the CQS renewal audit, ensure that your firm has well-documented policies, procedures, and records for each of these key areas. The assessor will want to see a robust system of controls and a culture of compliance within your conveyancing practice.

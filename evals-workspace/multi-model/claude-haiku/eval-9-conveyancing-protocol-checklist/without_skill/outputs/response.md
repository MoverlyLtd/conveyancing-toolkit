When you're about to exchange contracts, there are several key things you should check against the Law Society's Conveyancing Protocol:

1. Contract Pack Completeness:
   - Ensure the contract pack contains all the necessary documents, such as the contract, property information form, fixtures and fittings form, and any other relevant forms.
   - Verify that all the information in the contract pack is accurate and up-to-date.

2. Searches and Enquiries:
   - Confirm that all necessary searches (e.g., local authority, drainage, and environmental) have been carried out and the results are satisfactory.
   - Review the responses to any pre-contract enquiries made to the seller to ensure there are no outstanding issues or concerns.

3. Title Documents:
   - Verify that the seller has provided all the relevant title documents, such as the title deeds or Land Registry documents.
   - Ensure there are no issues or restrictions with the property's title that could affect your purchase.

4. Mortgage Offer:
   - If you're obtaining a mortgage, confirm that the mortgage offer matches the property details and the agreed purchase price.
   - Check that the mortgage offer meets the lender's requirements and that you understand the terms and conditions.

5. Completion Date:
   - Ensure the completion date agreed upon in the contract is suitable for you and aligns with any existing commitments, such as the end of your current tenancy or the availability of your moving arrangements.

6. Deposit:
   - Confirm the amount of the deposit required and ensure it is paid in accordance with the protocol.
   - Verify that the deposit will be held by the relevant party (usually the seller's solicitor) until completion.

7. Completion Arrangements:
   - Review the completion arrangements, including the transfer of funds, keys, and any relevant documents.
   - Ensure you understand the process and your responsibilities on the completion day.

8. Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT):
   - Confirm the SDLT liability and ensure the appropriate amount will be paid to HM Revenue & Customs.
   - Verify that the SDLT return will be filed within the required timeframe.

By thoroughly checking these key aspects against the Law Society's Conveyancing Protocol, you can help ensure a smooth and successful exchange of contracts.

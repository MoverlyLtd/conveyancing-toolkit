According to the Conveyancing Protocol, there are specific steps that should be taken when the other side is not responding to enquiries in a timely manner:

1. Escalation within the other firm:
   - If the other side is not responding, the first step is to escalate the issue within their firm. This typically involves contacting a more senior member of their conveyancing team, such as a partner or manager.
   - The aim is to emphasize the importance of the matter and the need for a prompt response, and to encourage them to prioritize the matter.

2. Formal written communication:
   - If the escalation within the other firm does not yield a response, the next step is to send a formal written communication, such as a letter or email, to the other side.
   - This communication should outline the specific enquiries that have been made, the timeline of when they were sent, and the importance of receiving a response.
   - It should also set a clear deadline for the other side to respond, typically 5-10 working days.

3. Raising the issue with the client:
   - If the formal written communication does not result in a response, the next step is to raise the issue with the client.
   - Explain the steps that have been taken to try to get a response, and discuss the potential implications of the delay, such as the risk of missing deadlines or incurring additional costs.
   - Seek the client's instructions on how to proceed, which may include considering alternative options, such as escalating the matter to the other side's firm's complaints procedure or, as a last resort, considering legal action.

4. Reporting to the relevant regulatory body:
   - If all other avenues have been exhausted and the other side continues to be unresponsive, the final step is to report the matter to the relevant regulatory body, such as the Law Society or the Council for Licensed Conveyancers.
   - This should be done in consultation with the client and may be necessary to ensure that the matter is properly addressed and to protect the client's interests.

The Conveyancing Protocol emphasizes the importance of maintaining clear communication and following a structured process when dealing with non-responsive parties. By adhering to these guidelines, conveyancers can demonstrate that they have taken all reasonable steps to progress the transaction and protect their client's interests.

As a CLC-regulated conveyancing firm, you have certain obligations when it comes to handling client complaints. Here's what the CLC requires you to do in this situation:

1. Complaints Handling Procedure:
   - The CLC requires you to have a written complaints handling procedure that is accessible to all clients. This procedure should outline the process for how the firm will investigate and respond to complaints.

2. Acknowledge the Complaint:
   - Upon receiving the client complaint, you must acknowledge it in writing within 7 days. This acknowledges that you have received the complaint and will investigate it.

3. Investigate the Complaint:
   - You must thoroughly investigate the complaint to understand the nature of the client's concerns and the reasons for any delays in the conveyancing service.

4. Provide a Substantive Response:
   - Within 28 days of receiving the complaint, you must provide the client with a substantive response. This response should:
     - Outline the findings of your investigation.
     - Explain the firm's position on the complaint.
     - Propose any appropriate remedies or actions to be taken.

5. Escalation to the Legal Ombudsman:
   - If the client is not satisfied with your response, you must inform them of their right to escalate the complaint to the Legal Ombudsman. You should provide the client with the Ombudsman's contact details and the relevant time frame for doing so (usually 6 months from the date of your final response).

6. Record-keeping:
   - The CLC requires you to maintain detailed records of all complaints received, the investigation process, and the final outcome. This documentation must be retained for at least 6 years.

7. Reporting to the CLC:
   - Depending on the nature and severity of the complaint, you may be required to report the matter to the CLC as a notifiable event. The CLC has specific guidelines on what types of complaints must be reported.

By following these CLC requirements, you can ensure that the client complaint is handled in a professional, transparent, and timely manner, in accordance with the CLC's regulatory standards.

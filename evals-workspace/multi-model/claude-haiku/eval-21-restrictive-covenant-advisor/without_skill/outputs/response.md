In this scenario, where the property has a restrictive covenant against business use, but the client wants to operate a small home office that is not visible and does not involve client visits, there are a few options to consider:

1. Seek a Deed of Variation:
   - The client could approach the beneficiary of the restrictive covenant (typically the original developer or a neighboring landowner) and seek a Deed of Variation to the covenant.
   - This would involve negotiating with the beneficiary to obtain their consent to modify the covenant and allow the proposed home office use.
   - The cost of obtaining a Deed of Variation can vary but is typically in the range of £500 to £1,500 plus legal fees.

2. Apply for a Deed of Release:
   - If the beneficiary of the restrictive covenant cannot be identified or is unwilling to agree to a Deed of Variation, the client could apply to the Land Registry for a Deed of Release.
   - This process involves demonstrating that the covenant is obsolete or that it is no longer reasonably practicable to comply with it.
   - The cost of obtaining a Deed of Release can range from £1,000 to £3,000 plus legal fees, depending on the complexity of the case.

3. Rely on Non-Enforcement:
   - Since the restrictive covenant has never been enforced, the client could choose to proceed with the home office use, relying on the fact that the covenant has not been enforced in the past.
   - This approach carries some risk, as the beneficiary of the covenant could potentially seek to enforce it in the future, even if they have not done so previously.

4. Indemnity Insurance:
   - As an alternative to the above options, the client could consider obtaining indemnity insurance to cover the risk of the restrictive covenant being enforced in the future.
   - Indemnity insurance premiums for a residential property with a restrictive covenant against business use can typically range from £150 to £500, depending on the level of cover required and the perceived risk of enforcement.
   - The indemnity insurance policy would provide financial protection in the event that the client is required to cease the home office use or face legal action due to the restrictive covenant.

It's important to note that the specific costs and feasibility of the options may vary depending on the local circumstances, the details of the restrictive covenant, and the willingness of the beneficiary to agree to a modification or release. It is recommended that the client consults with a conveyancing solicitor to discuss the most appropriate course of action and to obtain a more accurate assessment of the costs involved.

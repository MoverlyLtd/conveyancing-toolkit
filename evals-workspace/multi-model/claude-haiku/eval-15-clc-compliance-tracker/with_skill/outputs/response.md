As a licensed conveyancer regulated by the Council of Licensed Conveyancers (CLC), your key obligations around client money are:

1. Client Money Accounts:
   - You must hold all client money in designated client bank accounts, separate from the firm's office/business accounts.
   - The client account must be properly titled to identify it as a client account.

2. Client Account Reconciliations:
   - You must reconcile the client account regularly, at a minimum of once per month.
   - This ensures the account balance matches the total amount owed to clients.

3. Interest on Client Money:
   - You must handle any interest earned on client money in accordance with the terms of engagement.
   - This means either paying the interest to the client or retaining it as part of your fee, as agreed.

4. Accountant's Report:
   - You must submit an annual accountant's report to the CLC, confirming compliance with the Accounts Code.
   - This independent verification demonstrates proper management of client funds.

5. Protecting Client Money:
   - You must have adequate systems and controls in place to safeguard client money and assets.
   - This includes measures like access restrictions, dual signatories, and regular audits.

6. Reporting Concerns:
   - If you have any concerns about the handling of client money, you must report these to the CLC promptly.

Proper management of client money is a critical aspect of CLC compliance. Failure to meet these obligations could result in regulatory action against you and your firm. The CLC Accounts Code provides the full details on these client money requirements.

As a licensed conveyancer under the Council for Licensed Conveyancers (CLC) regulation, you have specific obligations regarding the handling and management of client money. Here are the key obligations:

1. Client Account:
   - You must hold client money in a client account, which is a bank account designated for holding client funds separately from your own business funds.
   - The client account must be maintained with an authorized bank or building society.
   - You must have appropriate systems and controls in place to ensure the proper recording and safekeeping of client money.

2. Accounting Records:
   - You must maintain accurate and up-to-date accounting records for all client money received, held, and paid out.
   - These records must include details such as the identity of the client, the purpose of the funds, and the dates of any transactions.
   - You must reconcile the client account on a regular basis, at least once per month, to ensure the accuracy of the records.

3. Handling of Client Money:
   - You must only use client money for the purpose for which it was provided, such as completing a property transaction or paying for a service on behalf of the client.
   - You must not use client money for your own business purposes or for any other unauthorized purpose.
   - You must have appropriate controls and procedures in place to ensure the timely and accurate payment of client money, including the timely return of any surplus funds to the client.

4. Regulatory Reporting:
   - You must report any breaches or discrepancies in the handling of client money to the CLC as soon as you become aware of them.
   - You may also be required to submit periodic reports to the CLC regarding the management and handling of client money.

5. Client Money Protection:
   - You must have appropriate insurance or other arrangements in place to protect client money in the event of your firm's insolvency or other financial difficulties.
   - The CLC may require you to maintain a minimum level of professional indemnity insurance or other financial protections.

6. Supervision and Audit:
   - The CLC may conduct periodic inspections or audits of your firm's client money handling practices to ensure compliance with the regulatory requirements.
   - You must cooperate fully with any such inspections or audits and provide the CLC with any requested information or documentation.

Failure to comply with these obligations can result in disciplinary action by the CLC, including fines, restrictions on your practice, or even the revocation of your license as a licensed conveyancer.

As a CQS-accredited conveyancing firm in the UK, there are several onboarding requirements that would apply to a new fee earner joining your firm:

1. CQS Training and Competency Assessment:
   - The new fee earner must undergo mandatory CQS training to ensure they have the necessary knowledge and skills to handle conveyancing matters effectively.
   - This training should cover topics such as the conveyancing process, property law, client care, and compliance with CQS standards.
   - The firm must assess the new fee earner's competency through a combination of written tests, practical assessments, and ongoing supervision.

2. CQS Compliance Policies and Procedures:
   - The new fee earner must be made aware of and trained on the firm's CQS-compliant policies and procedures, which cover areas such as client onboarding, file management, anti-money laundering, and client accounts.
   - They should receive a copy of the firm's CQS Compliance Manual and undergo training to ensure they understand and can implement these policies and procedures.

3. CQS-specific Record-keeping and Reporting:
   - The new fee earner must be trained on the firm's CQS-compliant record-keeping and reporting requirements, including the maintenance of client files, transaction records, and any CQS-related reports or returns.
   - They should be familiar with the CQS Reporting Schedule and understand their responsibilities in terms of providing information for these reports.

4. Ongoing Supervision and Mentoring:
   - The firm should implement a system of ongoing supervision and mentoring for the new fee earner, particularly during the initial period of their employment.
   - This may involve regular check-ins, file reviews, and feedback sessions to ensure the new fee earner is adhering to CQS standards and providing high-quality conveyancing services.

5. CQS-related Professional Development:
   - The firm should encourage the new fee earner to continue their professional development in conveyancing and CQS-related topics, such as by attending CQS-approved training courses or webinars.
   - This will help the new fee earner stay up-to-date with any changes or updates to CQS requirements and best practices.

By ensuring these CQS-specific onboarding requirements are met, the firm can effectively integrate the new fee earner into the CQS-accredited conveyancing practice and maintain its compliance with the CQS standards.

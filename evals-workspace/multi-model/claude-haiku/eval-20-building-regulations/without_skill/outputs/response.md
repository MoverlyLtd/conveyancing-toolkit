In this scenario, where the client's property has replacement windows installed in 2018 without a FENSA certificate or building control sign-off, I would advise the following:

1. FENSA Certification:
   - Explain to the client that the FENSA (Fenestration Self-Assessment Scheme) certificate is a document that confirms the replacement windows have been installed in compliance with the Building Regulations.
   - Advise the client that the lack of a FENSA certificate means the local authority may not have inspected and approved the installation, which could pose potential issues in the future.

2. Building Control Sign-Off:
   - Inform the client that building control approval is required for certain home improvement projects, including the installation of replacement windows.
   - Explain that the absence of building control sign-off indicates the local authority may not have inspected and certified the work, which could lead to problems if the property is ever resold or if there are any issues with the windows.

3. Potential Risks and Remedies:
   - Advise the client that the lack of FENSA certification and building control approval may affect the property's marketability and value in the future, as it could be seen as a defect or unresolved issue.
   - Recommend that the client contacts the local company that installed the windows to obtain the necessary documentation or to have the windows inspected and certified retrospectively.
   - If the local company is no longer in business or is unwilling to assist, suggest that the client may need to engage a qualified installer to inspect the windows and obtain the required FENSA and building control approvals.

4. Potential Costs:
   - Inform the client that the process of obtaining the necessary approvals retrospectively may incur additional costs, such as the fees for the FENSA certification and any building control inspections or sign-offs.
   - Advise the client to obtain quotes from qualified installers to understand the potential costs involved.

5. Disclosure to the Buyer:
   - Recommend that the client discloses the lack of FENSA certification and building control approval to any potential buyers, as this information should be included in the Property Information Form (TA6) during the conveyancing process.
   - Explain that this transparency will help avoid any surprises or issues during the transaction and may also help to mitigate any potential liability for the client.

By providing this advice, the client will be fully informed of the potential risks and the necessary steps to address the missing documentation for the replacement windows. This will help ensure that the client can make an informed decision and take appropriate action to resolve the issue, either before or during the conveyancing process.

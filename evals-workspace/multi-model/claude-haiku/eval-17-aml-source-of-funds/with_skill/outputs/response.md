To meet the AML source of funds requirements for this £750,000 property purchase, you will need the following evidence:

Savings (£200,000):
- 12 months of the client's bank/building society statements showing the gradual accumulation of the £200,000
- Verification that the account holder name on the statements matches the client
- Review of the statements to confirm a credible savings pattern, with investigation of any large lump sum deposits

Gift (£100,000 from parents):
- Photographic ID (passport or driving licence) for the parents providing the gift
- 3-6 months of bank statements for the parents showing they have the £100,000 to gift and the source of those funds
- Signed gift letter from the parents confirming:
  - The amount of the gift
  - Their relationship to the client
  - That there is no obligation for the client to repay the gift
  - That the parents have no expected interest in the property
- Proof of the relationship between the parents and client (if not obvious from the names)
- Notification to the mortgage lender about the gifted deposit per the UK Finance Lender's Handbook

Mortgage (£450,000):
- Mortgage offer from the FCA-regulated lender
- Verification that the lender is indeed FCA-regulated by checking the FCA Register

Throughout the process, you must:

- Apply a risk-based approach, with the depth of enquiry proportionate to the risk profile
- Document your assessment, evidence review, and conclusion for each funding element
- Conduct ongoing monitoring (per Regulation 28(12)) in case the funding structure changes during the transaction
- Be prepared to escalate to your firm's MLRO if any red flags are identified, and consider filing a SAR if suspicion arises
- Retain all AML records for 5 years from the end of the business relationship

According to the Conveyancing Association (CA) Protocol, the handling of a property with a rear extension built in 2021 without planning permission records should be approached as follows:

1. Disclosure:
   - The seller should disclose the existence of the rear extension and the lack of planning permission records in the Property Information Form (TA6).
   - The buyer's conveyancer should make inquiries about the extension and the planning status with the relevant local authority.

2. Local Authority Enquiries:
   - The buyer's conveyancer should make formal enquiries with the local planning authority to determine whether the extension was constructed with the necessary planning permission.
   - If the local authority confirms that planning permission was not obtained, the conveyancer should request details of any enforcement action taken or planned.

3. Advice to the Buyer:
   - The buyer's conveyancer should advise the buyer of the potential risks and consequences associated with the lack of planning permission, such as the possibility of enforcement action, the need to obtain retrospective planning permission, and any potential impact on the property's value or future sale.

4. Indemnity Insurance:
   - If the local authority confirms that planning permission was not obtained, the buyer's conveyancer should consider the need for an indemnity insurance policy to protect the buyer against any potential issues related to the extension.
   - The cost of the indemnity insurance policy should be negotiated with the seller, as they may be responsible for this expense.

5. Negotiations and Adjustments:
   - The buyer's conveyancer should negotiate with the seller to address the planning permission issue, which may include:
     - Requesting a reduction in the purchase price to reflect the potential risks and costs associated with the extension.
     - Seeking the seller's agreement to obtain retrospective planning permission before completion.
     - Securing the seller's agreement to cover the cost of any future enforcement action or the need to remove the extension.

6. Completion and Ongoing Monitoring:
   - If the planning permission issue is satisfactorily resolved, the transaction can proceed to completion.
   - The buyer's conveyancer should advise the buyer to monitor the situation and take any necessary actions to address any future planning-related concerns regarding the extension.

By following the CA Protocol, the conveyancers can ensure that the buyer is fully informed about the planning permission issues, the associated risks, and any necessary steps to mitigate those risks before proceeding with the purchase.

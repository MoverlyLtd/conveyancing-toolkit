
For restrictive covenants on a **freehold property**, the best place to start is:

- **Law Society guidance on restrictive covenants**: https://www.lawsociety.org.uk/topics/property/restrictive-covenants  
- **HM Land Registry guidance on notices/restrictions and title information**: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/notices-restrictions-and-the-protection-of-third-party-interests-in-the-register/practice-guide-14-notices-restrictions-and-the-protection-of-third-party-interests-in-the-register  
- **HMLR guidance on title plans and boundaries** if the covenant is tied to land ownership boundaries: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/land-registry-plans-boundaries/land-registry-plans-and-boundaries

According to the **Law Society**, restrictive covenants are obligations affecting land that can limit what an owner may do with a property, and they may still bind a freehold title depending on how they were created and whether they are protected. (Source: https://www.lawsociety.org.uk/topics/property/restrictive-covenants)

According to **HM Land Registry Practice Guide 14**, entries on the register such as notices and restrictions can protect third-party interests affecting registered land, which is relevant when checking whether a restrictive covenant appears on the title. (Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/notices-restrictions-and-the-protection-of-third-party-interests-in-the-register/practice-guide-14-notices-restrictions-and-the-protection-of-third-party-interests-in-the-register)

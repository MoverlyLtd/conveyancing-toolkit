For a **freehold property in England and Wales**, restrictive covenants are usually found in the **title documents held at HM Land Registry**.

### Where to look
1. **Official copy of the register of title**
   - Check the **“Charges Register”**.
   - This is where most restrictive covenants are referred to or listed.
   - The register may **summarise** the covenant and often says something like:
     - “A Conveyance dated… contains restrictive covenants”
     - or it may set out the actual wording.

2. **Official copy of the title plan**
   - This shows the extent of the land, but **not the covenants themselves**.
   - Useful to confirm which land the title relates to.

3. **The original deed/transfer/conveyance referred to in the register**
   - If the register only gives a brief reference, you may need to obtain the **actual deed** from the Land Registry file or from the conveyancer’s papers.
   - This is important because the **full wording** of the covenant is in the original deed, and wording matters a lot.

4. **Your purchase papers / solicitor’s report on title**
   - Your conveyancer should usually highlight restrictive covenants in the report on title and enclosures.
   - If you bought the property with a mortgage, the lender may also have seen them.

### What to ask for
If you want to check properly, ask for:
- an **Official Copy of the Register**
- an **Official Copy of the Title Plan**
- and, if needed, a copy of the **deed referenced in the Charges Register**

### Important point
Not every covenant shown on the title is necessarily enforceable against you. Enforceability can depend on:
- whether it is truly **restrictive** rather than positive,
- whether it **runs with the land**,
- who has the **benefit** of it,
- and whether it was properly protected on the title.

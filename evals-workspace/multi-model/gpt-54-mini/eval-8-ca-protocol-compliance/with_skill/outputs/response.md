Under the Conveyancing Association Protocol, the other side should deal with enquiries promptly and cooperatively to reduce delays.

Key points:
- **Respond to enquiries early and promptly** rather than letting them sit unanswered.
- **Use the phone for complex issues** instead of long email chains.
- **Keep the other lawyer and estate agent updated regularly** if a delay is affecting progress.
- **Raise enquiries as early as possible** so problems are found sooner.
- **Review the file regularly** and chase outstanding points on a diarised cycle.

Relevant sections:
- **CA 1.0 Communication**
- **CA 2.4 Enquiries raised at the earliest stage**
- **CA 2.0 Additional Enquiries**

If the delay is because the seller side is not providing documents or fuller replies, the protocol also says the seller’s conveyancer should review the papers properly and help get the information together early, rather than leaving issues until later.

Practical takeaway:
- Chase in writing, then escalate by phone if needed.
- Update your client and estate agent.
- Keep a clear record of unanswered enquiries and why they matter to title/lender requirements.

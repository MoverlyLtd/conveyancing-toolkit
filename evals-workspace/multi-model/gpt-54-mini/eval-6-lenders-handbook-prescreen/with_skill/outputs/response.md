Two issues are triggered under the UK Finance Lenders’ Handbook:

1. **Lease term: 72 years remaining**
   - This falls under **Part 1, 5.14.1**.
   - Whether it is acceptable depends on the **specific lender’s Part 2 minimum lease term**.
   - You need to check the lender’s Part 2 requirements for the exact threshold. If the term is below the lender’s minimum, this should be **reported to the lender before completion** and you should not complete until written instructions are received.

2. **Loft conversion without building regulations sign-off**
   - This is a **title / planning / building regulations issue** under **Part 1, 5.5.1–5.5.2**.
   - You must take all reasonable steps to ensure the property has the necessary planning and building regulation approvals.
   - If there has been a breach, the key question is whether there is **no reasonable prospect of enforcement** and you can still **certify good and marketable title**. If not, this should be **reported to the lender**.
   - If the defect affects marketability or title cannot be certified as good and marketable, the usual route is either:
     - obtain **regularisation / completion evidence**, or
     - consider **indemnity insurance** if appropriate and acceptable to the lender.

Practical takeaway:
- **72 years**: needs lender-specific Part 2 check.
- **Missing building regs sign-off**: needs investigation; if unresolved or risky, **report to lender** before exchange/completion.

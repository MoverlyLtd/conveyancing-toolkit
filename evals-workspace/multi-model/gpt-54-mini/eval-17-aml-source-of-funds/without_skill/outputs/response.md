Under current SRA AML expectations, you should be able to evidence both:

1. **source of funds** for the money being used in the transaction, and  
2. where relevant, **source of wealth** where a large gift or unusual accumulation needs explaining.

For a **£750,000 purchase** funded by **£200,000 savings + £100,000 parental gift + £450,000 mortgage**, the practical evidence you’d normally want is:

---

## 1) £200,000 from savings
You need to show **where the savings came from and that the funds being used are actually your client’s**.

### Typical evidence
- **Recent bank statements** for the account(s) holding the savings, usually covering:
  - the build-up of the savings, and
  - the period up to transfer to you / the lender / purchase completion.
- If the savings have been built up over time from income:
  - **payslips**, **P60s**, and/or **tax returns** if self-employed.
- If the savings came from sale of an asset:
  - **completion statement** for the sale,
  - **bank statement showing receipt of sale proceeds**, and
  - any supporting ownership document if needed (e.g. sale of shares, property sale, investment redemption).
- If the savings have moved through multiple accounts:
  - **bank statements showing the trail** from original source to current account.

### What SRA-style scrutiny is looking for
- Is the accumulation **reasonable for the client’s profile**?
- Is there a **clear audit trail** from origin to current account?
- Are the funds held in an **account in the client’s name**?

---

## 2) £100,000 gift from parents
A gift from a family member is still treated as part of the transaction funding, but you also need to be satisfied it is a **genuine gift, not a loan or undisclosed beneficial interest**.

### Typical evidence
- **Gift letter / deed of gift** signed by the parents confirming:
  - the amount gifted,
  - the money is **unconditional**,
  - it is **not repayable**,
  - the donors will have **no legal or beneficial interest** in the property,
  - it is not a loan,
  - and whether the client may use it for deposit/completion costs.
- **ID and AML checks on the parents/giftors** if your firm’s risk assessment and lender requirements require this, which is usually prudent and often necessary under lender panel instructions.
- **Bank statement from the parents** showing the funds leaving their account.
- **Bank statement for your client** showing receipt of the gift.
- If the parents’ funds are not obviously from their current balance:
  - an explanation and evidence of the **parents’ source of funds/source of wealth**, e.g.:
    - savings statements,
    - sale of property,
    - pension lump sum,
    - inheritance,
    - investment encashment.

### Important point
For a **large gift of £100,000**, many firms will also want to understand the **parents’ source of wealth**, not just the account from which the gift is sent, especially if the gift is the result of a one-off disposal or appears disproportionate to their visible income.

---

## 3) £450,000 mortgage
For the mortgage element, the source of funds is normally the **regulated lender advance**.

### Typical evidence
- **Mortgage offer** from the lender showing:
  - amount advanced,
  - borrower(s),
  - property address,
  - conditions.
- **ID/AML checks** in relation to the borrower(s) as part of your standard client due diligence.
- If the lender requires it or if there are unusual features:
  - **gift letter** to comply with lender instructions,
  - any **declaration of trust** or occupier consent forms if relevant.

### What you generally do not need
You do **not** usually need to trace the lender’s own source of funds in the same way you would for client money, because the mortgage advance is from a regulated financial institution and is evidenced by the offer/completion monies.

---

# Summary table

| Funding element | Evidence usually needed |
|---|---|
| **£200,000 savings** | Bank statements showing the funds, trail of accumulation, and any supporting evidence for origin if not obvious (salary, sale, inheritance, investments) |
| **£100,000 parental gift** | Gift letter/deed, parents’ ID/AML, bank statements showing transfer out of parents’ account and into client’s account, and source of wealth/source of funds for parents if needed |
| **£450,000 mortgage** | Mortgage offer/advance confirmation; no separate source-of-funds trail usually needed beyond lender verification and your client due diligence |

For SRA and lender compliance, I’d usually recommend you keep on file:

- a **clear funds table** for the transaction,
- **bank statements** for all relevant accounts,
- **gift letter/deed**,
- **proof of transfer** of gifted monies,
- **client due diligence** on buyer(s), and often on donors,

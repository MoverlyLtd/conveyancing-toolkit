This sounds like a **low-risk, likely technical issue** rather than a serious covenant problem, but the key first question is:

**Has anyone contacted the covenant beneficiary or their successors about this covenant?**  
If yes, **indemnity insurance is no longer available**. If no, keep it that way until a strategy is chosen.

### Practical view
A covenant against **business use** is usually aimed at avoiding a real commercial change in the property’s character. A **small, invisible home office with no client visits, no signage, no deliveries, and no nuisance** is often **not treated as a meaningful breach in practice**.

The facts you’ve given also help:
- **1965 covenant**: old, so possibly weaker in practical terms
- **Never enforced**: relevant as a risk reducer
- **No visible external change** and **no public-facing use**: low enforcement risk

### Main options

#### 1. Do nothing
Often appropriate if:
- the work is purely internal/home-based,
- there is no traffic, signage, or external impact,
- and the client is comfortable with the residual risk.

This is commonly the cheapest route for low-risk home-working.

#### 2. Indemnity insurance
If you want comfort for the client, lender, or future sale, indemnity insurance is usually available **provided there has been no approach to the beneficiary**.

Typical cost for a residential covenant policy of this kind is usually:
- **£50–£300**
- sometimes a bit more depending on property value, lender requirements, and policy limit

Policies are usually arranged **quickly** and can often be put in place in **a few days**.

#### 3. Negotiate a release or consent
Only sensible if the beneficiary is known and contactable, but this is generally **not the preferred route** here because:
- it may be unnecessary for a low-risk use,
- and any approach would usually **kill insurance availability**.

#### 4. Upper Tribunal application
This is usually **not proportionate** for a small, hidden home office with no clients visiting. It is more relevant where:
- there is a clear material breach,
- the covenant blocks a more substantial development,
- or insurance is not available.

### Bottom line
For a **small, non-visible home office**, the usual advice is:
- **likely no action needed**, or
- **cheap indemnity insurance** if the client wants added comfort.

For a **first-time buyer** purchasing a flat for **£510,000**, the SDLT is **£15,500**.

### Breakdown
- **£0 – £300,000** at 0% = **£0**
- **£300,001 – £500,000** at 5% = **£10,000**
- **£500,001 – £510,000** at standard rates, because first-time buyer relief only applies up to £500,000:
  - **£10,000** at 5% = **£500**

### Total SDLT
**£10,500** — sorry, correction: since first-time buyer relief **does not apply at all** if the purchase price is above **£500,000**, the full purchase is taxed at the **standard rates**.

So the correct SDLT is:

- **£0 – £125,000** at 0% = **£0**
- **£125,001 – £250,000** at 2% = **£2,500**
- **£250,001 – £510,000** at 5% = **£13,000**

**Total SDLT: £15,500**

### Effective rate
About **3.04%** of the purchase price.

### Notes
- This is for **England or Northern Ireland** residential property.
- SDLT is usually filed and paid within **14 days of completion**.

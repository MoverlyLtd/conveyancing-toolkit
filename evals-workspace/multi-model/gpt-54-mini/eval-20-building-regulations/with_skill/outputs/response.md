Advise that this is a **building regulations issue**, but a fairly common and usually manageable one.

### Key points
- Replacement windows are usually notifiable under **Part L** of the Building Regulations.
- If there is **no FENSA/CERTASS certificate** and **no local authority completion certificate**, then there is currently **no evidence of compliance**.
- “Installed by a local company” is **not enough** on its own.

### What to do next
1. **Check for a competent person scheme certificate**
   - Ask the seller for the installer’s details and certificate number.
   - Search the relevant competent person scheme database, especially:
     - **FENSA**
     - **CERTASS**
   - If a certificate exists, that is generally accepted in place of building control sign-off.

2. **If no certificate exists**
   - You have two possible routes:
     - **Indemnity insurance**, or
     - **Regularisation** with the local authority

### Best approach here
Given the windows are from **2018**:
- This is **recent enough** that some lenders may be picky.
- But replacement windows are also a **low-risk, common issue**, and **indemnity insurance is often acceptable**.
- If the buyer wants certainty, regularisation is possible, but it may be disproportionate for windows alone.

### Important warning
- **Do not contact building control first** if indemnity is being considered.
- Any approach to the local authority can **invalidate indemnity insurance**.

### Practical advice
I would usually suggest:
- First try to obtain a **FENSA/CERTASS certificate**.
- If none can be found, ask the seller to consider **indemnity insurance**.
- If the lender will not accept indemnity, then the seller may need to seek **regularisation** instead.

### Suggested wording to client
> The replacement windows installed in 2018 do not currently have evidence of Building Regulations compliance. We should first try to locate a FENSA/CERTASS or similar competent person certificate. If none can be produced, the usual options are indemnity insurance or retrospective regularisation, subject to the lender’s requirements. We should not approach the local authority unless we have decided to proceed by regularisation, as that may prevent indemnity cover.

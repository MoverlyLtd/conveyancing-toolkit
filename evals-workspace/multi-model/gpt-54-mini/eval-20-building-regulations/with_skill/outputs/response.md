For 2018 replacement windows with no FENSA certificate and no building control sign-off, I’d advise as follows:

1. **Check the competent person route first**
   - Ask for the installer’s details and check **competentperson.co.uk**.
   - Replacement windows/doors are usually covered by **Part L** and often self-certified under **FENSA** or **CERTASS**.
   - If the installer was registered at the time, a certificate may be obtainable even if the seller no longer has it.

2. **If no certificate can be found**
   - Because the work is from **2018**, this is relatively recent and lenders may be stricter than for older work.
   - The main choices are:
     - **Regularisation** with the local authority, or
     - **Indemnity insurance** if no one has contacted the local authority.

3. **Before any approach rule**
   - **Do not contact the local authority/building control until the strategy is decided**, because **any contact will make indemnity insurance unavailable** for that work.
   - That includes casual enquiries by the seller, buyer, agent, or you.

4. **Which route is better?**
   - **Regularisation** is usually the more robust solution and is **preferred by lenders**, but it typically costs around **£400–800** and takes time.
   - **Indemnity insurance** is cheaper, usually about **£50–200**, but it only covers enforcement risk, **not** any actual defects in the windows.
   - **Not all lenders accept indemnity**, so check the specific lender’s handbook position before relying on it.

5. **Risk assessment**
   - Replacement windows are generally a **lower-risk** issue than structural work or loft conversions.
   - If the windows are in a rear elevation or otherwise not obviously problematic, indemnity is often commercially acceptable where the lender agrees.
   - If there are any visible defects or performance concerns, the client should consider a surveyor’s view as indemnity won’t fix the workmanship.

**Bottom line:**  
First, check **competentperson.co.uk** for a FENSA/CERTASS-style certificate. If none exists, and **no contact has been made with building control**, you can choose between **regularisation** and **indemnity**; for 2018 windows, many conveyancers would still try to obtain a proper certificate or regularise if the lender is uncomfortable. If the lender accepts it, **indemnity** is often the pragmatic route for windows, but only after confirming the local authority has not been approached.

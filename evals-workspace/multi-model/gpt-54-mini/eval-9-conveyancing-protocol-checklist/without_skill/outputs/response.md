Before exchange, the Law Society Conveyancing Protocol is mainly about making sure your solicitor has verified the key title, contract, mortgage and completion details, and that you understand exactly what you are committing to.

Here’s a practical pre-exchange checklist against the Protocol.

## 1) Contract package is complete and accurate
Check that the draft contract has:

- the correct names of all buyers and sellers
- the correct property address and title number
- the correct price
- the agreed deposit amount
- the agreed completion date
- any special conditions agreed in writing
- fixtures and fittings details matching the sale agreement
- the correct property description, including any parking space, garage, garden, outbuildings or shared areas

Also confirm that the contract papers match the latest agreed version and that no changes have been made since you last approved them.

## 2) Title has been checked properly
Your solicitor should have reviewed the title documents and reported to you on any issues. Make sure you understand:

- who actually owns the property
- whether the title is freehold or leasehold
- any rights of way, rights of access, parking rights or drainage rights
- any restrictive covenants
- any easements affecting the property
- any charges, mortgages or notices on the title
- whether any title defects or indemnity insurance are needed

If it is leasehold, check:

- remaining lease term
- ground rent and service charge obligations
- any transfer or notice fees
- landlord/managing agent requirements
- restrictions on use, subletting or alterations

## 3) Searches and enquiries are satisfied
Before exchange, the Protocol expects search results and enquiries to be reviewed. Check that your solicitor has:

- carried out the standard searches, usually local authority, drainage and water, environmental and any other relevant searches
- raised and received answers to pre-contract enquiries
- followed up anything unclear or inconsistent

Make sure you are happy with any adverse search entries, for example:

- planning or building regulation issues
- road adoption status
- flood risk
- drainage problems
- contaminated land issues
- conservation area or listed building restrictions

## 4) Mortgage offer is in place
If you are buying with a mortgage, check:

- the mortgage offer has been received
- the offer is still valid at exchange and completion
- the amount, lender and borrower names are correct
- all conditions in the offer can be satisfied
- your solicitor has lender authority to exchange

You should not exchange unless you are sure the mortgage funds will be available on completion.

## 5) Deposit and funds are ready
The Protocol expects buyers to be clear about money arrangements.

Check:

- how much deposit is required
- whether it is 10% or a negotiated amount
- whether any deposit is being held as stakeholder or agent
- where your solicitor wants cleared funds sent and by when
- whether you need to fund any shortfall from savings, Help to Buy/other schemes, or a gifted deposit

If you are relying on a sale to fund your purchase, confirm the chain and timings are workable.

## 6) Completion date and chain are confirmed
Before exchange, make sure:

- the completion date is definite and agreed by everyone in the chain
- you know what happens if someone in the chain pulls out or delays
- removals can be booked for the agreed date
- your mortgage offer and other deadlines align with the completion date

Remember that once contracts are exchanged, the date becomes legally binding.

## 7) Risk and insurance position is understood
The Protocol requires your solicitor to advise you about risk passing on exchange in most cases.

Check:

- who is responsible for insuring the property from exchange
- whether the seller’s insurance continues until completion
- whether you need buildings insurance from exchange or from completion, especially for freehold property
- if leasehold, what the lease and landlord require

If the property is damaged after exchange but before completion, you need to know who bears the risk.

## 8) Fixtures, contents and apportionments are agreed
Check the final fixtures and contents list against what you expect to remain or be removed.

Also confirm:

- any included appliances are listed clearly
- any chattels being purchased are documented
- service charge and ground rent apportionments are correct for leasehold
- rent apportionment is correct if relevant
- any completion statement figures have been checked

## 9) Identity and authority issues are resolved
The Protocol expects solicitors to verify identity and authority.

Check whether:

- all buyers/sellers have provided ID and proof of address
- any company seller/buyer has proper authority and board approvals
- any attorney, trustee, executor or receiver has the right authority to sign
- any power of attorney has been checked and is valid

## 10) Occupiers and consent issues are covered
If anyone over 17 is living at the property or will be occupying it, check whether:

- occupier consent or waiver forms are needed
- matrimonial or occupational rights have been addressed
- there are any third-party rights that could affect title or lender requirements

## 11) You have had the solicitor’s report and understand the risks
The Protocol expects the solicitor to report clearly before exchange. Make sure you have received and understood:

- any title issues
- search results
- planning/building regulation matters
- leasehold obligations
- mortgage conditions
- any other unusual contract terms

If anything is unclear, ask before exchange. After exchange, it is usually too late to back out without penalty.

## 12) Final practical checks
Before authorising exchange, confirm:

- spelling of names is correct
- correspondence addresses are correct
- email and phone contact details are up to date
- all parties are ready to exchange at the same time
- you know the exact time and method of exchange
- you have cleared funds in place if required

## Key point
The main Protocol principle is: **do not exchange until title, contract, searches, mortgage, funds and completion arrangements have all been checked and reported to you properly**.

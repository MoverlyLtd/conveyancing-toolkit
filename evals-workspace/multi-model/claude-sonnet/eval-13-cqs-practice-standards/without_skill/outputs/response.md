For a new fee earner joining your CQS-accredited firm, there are several specific onboarding requirements under the Conveyancing Quality Scheme:

## Core CQS Training Requirements

**Within 6 months of joining:**
- Complete the CQS Foundation Course (unless they've already completed it at another CQS firm within the last 3 years)
- Pass the online assessment
- If they're supervising others, complete the CQS Management Course

## Documentation and Systems Access

**Immediate requirements:**
- Provide access to your firm's CQS Manual and procedures
- Ensure they understand your firm's specific CQS processes and workflows
- Grant access to required technology systems (case management, ID verification, etc.)
- Provide copies of relevant CQS documentation and updates

## Supervision and Monitoring

**Ongoing requirements:**
- Assign appropriate supervision structure
- Implement file monitoring procedures as per your CQS compliance framework
- Ensure they understand complaint handling procedures
- Brief them on your firm's specific risk management protocols

## Professional Standards

**Key areas to cover:**
- Anti-money laundering procedures specific to your firm
- Client care standards and CQS service commitments
- File management and record-keeping requirements
- Insurance arrangements and requirements

## Record Keeping

You must maintain records of:
- Training completion certificates
- Supervision arrangements
- Any file reviews conducted

The Law Society will expect evidence of proper onboarding during any CQS assessment visits. Ensure all training is completed within the specified timeframes to maintain your accreditation status.

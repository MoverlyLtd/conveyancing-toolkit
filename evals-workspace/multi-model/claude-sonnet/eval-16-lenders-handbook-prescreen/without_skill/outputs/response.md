Based on standard UK mortgage lending criteria, here's how these lenders typically approach leases with 72 years remaining:

## Halifax
**Unlikely to lend** - Halifax generally requires at least 75-80 years remaining on the lease at the start of the mortgage term, with some products requiring even longer. A 72-year lease would likely fall short of their minimum requirements.

## Nationwide
**Unlikely to lend** - Nationwide typically requires a minimum of 75-85 years remaining on the lease at the start of the mortgage, and often expects the lease to have at least 30-40 years remaining at the end of the mortgage term. A 72-year lease would likely be declined.

## Barclays
**Unlikely to lend** - Barclays generally requires at least 75-85 years remaining on the lease at application, with some products requiring longer terms. They also typically want significant years remaining after the mortgage term ends.

## Key Considerations:

- **80-year threshold**: Most mainstream lenders become very cautious below 80 years
- **Lease extension**: You might need to extend the lease before securing a mortgage
- **Specialist lenders**: Some specialist or smaller lenders may consider shorter leases
- **Higher deposits**: If any lender does consider it, they may require a larger deposit
- **Valuation impact**: Properties with short leases are typically valued lower

## Recommendation:
Consider extending the lease before applying for a mortgage, or explore specialist lenders who may have more flexible criteria for shorter leases.

**Note**: Lending criteria can change, so always check current requirements directly with lenders or through a mortgage broker.

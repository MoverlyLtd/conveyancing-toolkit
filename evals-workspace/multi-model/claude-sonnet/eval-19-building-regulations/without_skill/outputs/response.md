This is a significant issue that needs careful consideration. Here are the key implications and available remedies:

## Legal Implications

**Building Regulations Compliance:**
- All loft conversions require building regulations approval under the Building Act 1984
- Without a completion certificate, there's no evidence the work complies with building regulations
- The local authority could potentially serve an enforcement notice requiring remedial work or demolition
- This creates a legal defect in title that affects marketability

**Insurance and Safety Concerns:**
- Buildings insurance may be void or claims rejected for non-compliant work
- Safety issues regarding structural integrity, fire safety, insulation, and ventilation
- Potential liability for future buyers

## Available Remedies

**1. Regularisation Certificate (Most Common Solution)**
- Apply to the local authority under Building Regulations 2010, regulation 12
- Involves inspection by building control officer
- If work substantially complies, a regularisation certificate will be issued
- Cost: typically £300-800 plus any required remedial works
- Takes 4-8 weeks typically

**2. Structural Engineer's Report**
- Commission an independent structural engineer's report
- Must confirm compliance with building regulations standards
- Less certain than regularisation certificate
- May not satisfy all lenders or future buyers

**3. Building Regulations Indemnity Insurance**
- One-off premium (typically £100-300)
- Covers costs if local authority takes enforcement action
- Only available if no known breaches and work over 12 months old
- Many lenders won't accept this for loft conversions
- Doesn't address safety concerns

## Recommended Approach

**Pre-exchange:**
1. Request the seller obtains a regularisation certificate
2. If seller refuses, negotiate a retention from purchase price to cover costs
3. Ensure your lender will accept the chosen solution

**The regularisation certificate route is generally preferred** as it provides the most comprehensive solution and is widely accepted by lenders and future buyers.

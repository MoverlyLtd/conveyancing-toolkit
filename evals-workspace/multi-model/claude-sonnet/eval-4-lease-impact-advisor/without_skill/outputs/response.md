## Critical Issues with 68-Year Lease

**Mortgage Problems:**
- Most high street lenders won't lend on leases under 70 years
- Many have minimum requirements of 80+ years remaining
- This severely limits your buyer pool to cash purchasers or specialist lenders

**Marriage Value Impact:**
- Below 80 years, "marriage value" applies to lease extensions
- The freeholder becomes entitled to 50% of any uplift in property value
- Extension costs become significantly more expensive

## Immediate Actions Required

**For Marketing:**
- Prominently disclose the lease term in all marketing materials
- Include extension costs estimates if available
- Consider obtaining a Section 42 notice quote for extension costs

**Pricing Considerations:**
- The £425k asking price should reflect the lease length discount
- Factor in that buyers will need £15k-40k+ for immediate extension
- Consider whether a lower asking price might attract more interest

## Buyer Impact

**Extension Process:**
- Statutory right to extend by 90 years (making it 158 years total)
- Current estimated costs likely £15,000-£40,000+ (plus legal fees)
- Process takes 6-12 months minimum

**Alternative Solutions:**
- Collective enfranchisement (if applicable)
- Informal negotiation with freeholder

## Recommendations

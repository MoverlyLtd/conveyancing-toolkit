## Options for Dealing with the Restrictive Covenant

### 1. Risk Assessment and Proceeding Without Action
Given the covenant dates from 1965 (nearly 60 years old) and has never been enforced, your client could proceed with the home office use while considering:

**Factors supporting this approach:**
- **Obsolescence**: A 1965 covenant may be considered outdated given modern working patterns
- **No enforcement history**: Suggests beneficiaries may not be actively monitoring
- **De minimis breach**: Small home office with no external impact likely represents minimal breach
- **Potential beneficiary issues**: Original developers/beneficiaries may no longer exist or be traceable

### 2. Formal Discharge/Modification Applications

**Upper Tribunal (Lands Chamber) application under s.84 Law of Property Act 1925:**
- Grounds could include obsolescence due to changes in character of neighbourhood
- Reasonable user application if covenant impedes reasonable use
- Cost: £770 application fee plus legal costs (typically £5,000-£15,000+)
- Timeline: 6-12 months
- Risk: Application could fail

**Deed of Release:**
- Requires identifying and negotiating with all beneficiaries
- May be impossible if original developer has been dissolved
- Costs vary depending on negotiations required

### 3. Restrictive Covenant Indemnity Insurance

This is often the most practical solution for your scenario.

**Typical Coverage:**
- Legal costs defending enforcement action
- Diminution in property value if covenant enforced
- Cost of alternative accommodation if injunction granted
- Usually covers up to £1-5 million

**Premium Costs (approximate):**
- **Property value £200,000-£400,000**: £200-£400 one-off premium
- **Property value £400,000-£800,000**: £300-£600 one-off premium  
- **Property value £800,000+**: £400-£800+ one-off premium

**Factors affecting premium:**
- Property value
- Nature of the breach (home office is typically lower risk)
- Age of covenant (1965 covenant may attract lower premium)
- Location and character of area
- Any history of enforcement in the area

### 4. Practical Considerations

**Before proceeding, consider:**
- Obtaining copies of original development deeds to understand covenant scope
- Land Registry searches to identify if beneficiaries are traceable
- Local knowledge of any enforcement activity in the development
- Planning permission requirements (may need permitted development confirmation)

### Recommendation

For a small, non-visible home office use, **restrictive covenant indemnity insurance is typically the most cost-effective solution**. The premium cost (likely £250-£500 for most properties) is significantly lower than formal discharge applications and provides comprehensive protection.

**Key advantages:**
- Immediate solution
- One-off cost
- No need to trace beneficiaries
- Covers legal costs and financial losses
- Policy runs with the property title

The insurance approach recognizes the practical reality that enforcement of a 60-year-old covenant against minimal business use is unlikely, while providing financial protection if enforcement does occur.

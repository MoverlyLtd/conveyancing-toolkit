Under the CA Protocol (Conveyancing Association Property Information Forms Protocol), this situation would be addressed through the Property Information Forms, particularly the TA6 Property Information Form.

**Key Protocol requirements for undocumented extensions:**

**Seller's obligations:**
- **TA6 disclosure**: The seller must disclose the extension in the relevant sections of the TA6, particularly regarding alterations and planning permissions
- **Documentation requirement**: Provide any available documentation about the extension, even if informal
- **Certificate of Lawfulness**: The seller should be advised to consider obtaining a Certificate of Lawful Development (existing use) if the extension may be permitted development

**Conveyancer's duties:**
- **Risk assessment**: Evaluate whether the extension likely falls under permitted development rights or requires retrospective planning permission
- **Client advice**: Inform the buyer of the risks, including potential enforcement action
- **Additional enquiries**: Raise specific enquiries about:
  - When the extension was built
  - Whether permitted development rights were available
  - Any contact from the local planning authority

**Due diligence steps:**
- **Local authority search**: Check for any enforcement notices or planning applications
- **Permitted development analysis**: Assess if the extension meets permitted development criteria (size, height, materials, etc.)
- **Insurance consideration**: Advise on defective title insurance if planning status remains unclear

**Risk management:**
The Protocol emphasizes that conveyancers should ensure clients understand the implications and consider whether to proceed, seek retrospective permission, or obtain insurance cover. The extension being built in 2021 is relatively recent, which may affect enforcement time limits and insurance availability.

As a licensed conveyancer under CLC (Council for Licensed Conveyancers) regulation, you have several key obligations regarding client money:

## Client Account Requirements

**Separate Client Account**: You must maintain a separate client account at an approved institution, distinct from your business account. Client money cannot be mixed with your own funds.

**Authorized Institutions**: Client accounts must be held with banks, building societies, or other institutions specifically approved by the CLC.

## Handling Client Money

**Prompt Banking**: Client money must be paid into the client account without unreasonable delay, typically by the next business day.

**Client Money Definition**: This includes deposits, balances of purchase money, rent, and any money held on behalf of clients in connection with conveyancing services.

**Interest**: You must account to clients for interest earned on their money where appropriate, following CLC guidance on interest thresholds and calculations.

## Record Keeping

**Client Ledgers**: Maintain detailed client ledgers showing all receipts and payments for each client matter.

**Regular Reconciliations**: Perform regular reconciliations (at least monthly) between client ledger balances and actual bank balances.

**Retention**: Keep all client account records for at least 6 years after completion of the matter.

## Restrictions and Prohibitions

**No Borrowing**: You cannot use client money for your own purposes or to benefit other clients without proper authorization.

**Withdrawal Authorization**: Only withdraw client money with proper authority (client instructions or legitimate payments on their behalf).

**Overdraft Prohibition**: Client accounts must not go overdrawn.

## Compliance and Monitoring

**Annual Reporting**: Submit annual client account information to the CLC.

**Accountant's Reports**: Larger practices may require independent accountant's reports on client account compliance.

**CLC Powers**: The CLC has powers to inspect client accounts and investigate irregularities.

## Additional Safeguards

**Professional Indemnity Insurance**: Maintain appropriate PII coverage including client money protection.

**Compensation Fund**: Contribute to the CLC's compensation arrangements for client protection.

Failure to comply with these obligations can result in disciplinary action, including suspension or revocation of your license. Always refer to the current CLC Code of Conduct and specific guidance for the most up-to-date requirements.

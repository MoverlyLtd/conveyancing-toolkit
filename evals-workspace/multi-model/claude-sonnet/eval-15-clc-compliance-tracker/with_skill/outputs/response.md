As a licensed conveyancer under CLC regulation, your client money obligations are governed by **Principle 3.4** of the Code of Conduct and the **Accounts Code**. Here are your key obligations:

## Core Client Money Protection Requirements

### Designated Client Accounts (ACC1)
- All client money must be held in designated client accounts
- These accounts must be clearly identifiable as client money accounts
- Must be separate from your firm's business accounts

### Regular Reconciliation (ACC2)
- Client accounts must be reconciled at least monthly
- More frequent reconciliation may be required for high-volume practices
- Reconciliation records must be maintained and available for inspection

### Interest Handling (ACC3)
- Interest earned on client money must be handled according to your terms of engagement
- You must clearly explain your interest policy to clients upfront
- Common approaches include passing interest to clients or retaining with client consent

### Annual Reporting (ACC4)
- Submit an accountant's report to the CLC annually
- This must be prepared by a qualified accountant
- Covers compliance with the Accounts Code requirements

## Practical Implementation

### At Transaction Start
- Open client ledger for each matter
- Ensure terms of engagement clearly explain client money handling
- Verify bank account details before any transfers

### During Transaction
- Only use client money for its intended purpose
- Maintain detailed records of all client money movements
- Never mix client funds with office funds

### Transaction Completion
- Promptly account to clients for any surplus funds
- Provide clear breakdown of how client money was applied
- Transfer any remaining balance within reasonable time

## Integration with Other Obligations

Your client money duties connect to other CLC requirements:

- **AML obligations**: Verify source of funds before accepting client money
- **Principle 2.1**: Competent service includes proper money handling
- **Principle 3.1**: Client interests include protecting their funds

## Key Differences from SRA
Unlike SRA-regulated firms, CLC regulation focuses on **outcomes** rather than prescriptive rules. You have flexibility in how you achieve client money protection, but the outcomes (safe custody, proper accounting, transparency) are mandatory.

## Red Flags to Avoid
- Using client money for office expenses
- Delays in reconciliation
- Poor record-keeping
- Unclear terms about interest
- Missing accountant's reports

When you're about to exchange contracts, here are the key items you should check against the Law Society protocols (particularly the National Protocol and Code for Completion by Post):

## Pre-Exchange Checklist

**Documentation Review:**
- Contract is properly drafted and all amendments agreed
- Special conditions are correctly incorporated
- Completion date, time, and place are specified
- Purchase price and deposit amount are correct
- Title documents and official copies are up to date (within 6 months)

**Searches and Enquiries:**
- All searches are satisfactory and within acceptable time limits
- Local authority search, environmental search, drainage search completed
- Replies to enquiries are satisfactory
- Any follow-up enquiries have been resolved

**Financial Arrangements:**
- Mortgage offer is in place and conditions satisfied
- Deposit funds are available and cleared
- Source of funds documentation is complete for anti-money laundering compliance
- Buildings insurance is arranged from exchange date

## Exchange Process Protocol

**Authority to Exchange:**
- Written authority from client to exchange
- Confirmation of completion date with client
- Deposit holder identified (usually seller's solicitor)

**Formula for Exchange:**
- Agree which Law Society formula will be used (A, B, or C)
- Formula A: Solicitors hold their respective signed parts
- Formula B: One solicitor holds both signed parts
- Formula C: Chain transactions with multiple exchanges

**At Point of Exchange:**
- Confirm contract terms are identical
- Agree deposit arrangements and stakeholder capacity
- Confirm completion arrangements
- Note exact time of exchange
- Send confirmation to clients immediately

## Post-Exchange Protocol

**Immediate Actions:**
- Date both parts of contract with exchange date
- Insert agreed completion date if not already specified
- Send contract and deposit as agreed
- Confirm insurance is in place

**Completion Arrangements:**
- Agree method of completion (usually Law Society Code for Completion by Post)
- Prepare completion information forms
- Arrange for undertakings regarding discharge of seller's mortgage
- Confirm key collection arrangements

For your CQS (Conveyancing Quality Scheme) renewal audit, the assessor will focus on several key areas. Here's what you need to prepare:

## Core Assessment Areas

**1. Client Care & Service Standards**
- Client care letters demonstrating clear fee estimates, timescales, and regulatory information
- Evidence of complaints handling procedures and records
- Client feedback/satisfaction surveys
- Communication protocols and response time standards

**2. Case Management & File Reviews**
- Random selection of completed files (typically 10-15 files)
- Evidence of proper ID verification and AML compliance
- Clear audit trails showing key decisions and advice given
- Completion of CQS file review checklists
- Evidence of supervisor reviews for fee earners

**3. Risk Management**
- Updated risk register and mitigation strategies
- Evidence of regular risk assessments
- Procedures for high-risk transactions
- Documentation of risk management training

**4. AML & Regulatory Compliance**
- AML policies and procedures
- Evidence of ongoing due diligence monitoring
- Staff training records on AML updates
- Source of funds verification procedures
- Suspicious activity reporting procedures

## Essential Documentation

**Staff & Training:**
- Training records for all fee earners
- CPD compliance evidence
- Supervision arrangements and records
- Staff competency assessments

**Policies & Procedures:**
- Updated firm procedures manual
- Quality management system documentation
- Document retention policies
- IT security and data protection measures

**Performance Monitoring:**
- KPI tracking (completion times, client satisfaction, etc.)
- Regular file auditing records
- Management reporting systems
- Action plans for identified issues

## Practical Preparation Tips

- Ensure all policies reference current legislation and SRA requirements
- Have digital copies readily accessible for remote assessments
- Prepare a summary document showing continuous improvements made since last audit
- Ensure all fee earners understand the CQS standards and can discuss their application

The assessor will typically spend 2-3 hours reviewing documentation and interviewing key staff, so ensure your team is prepared to demonstrate practical application of your procedures.

Under current SRA guidance and the Money Laundering Regulations 2017, you'll need specific evidence for each funding source:

## **£200,000 from Savings:**
- **Bank statements** covering the preceding 3-6 months showing the accumulation of funds
- **Payslips/employment contract** to evidence legitimate income source
- If funds transferred from other accounts: statements for those accounts
- **Tax returns/P60s** if self-employed or high earner
- Explanation letter from client detailing how savings were accumulated

## **£100,000 Gift from Parents:**
- **Deed of gift** or formal gift letter confirming it's a genuine gift with no expectation of repayment
- **Parents' bank statements** (typically 3 months) showing funds leaving their account
- **Parents' ID verification** (passport/driving licence + proof of address)
- **Source of parents' wealth evidence** - how they accumulated the gifted funds (employment records, sale proceeds, inheritance documentation)
- **Client's bank statement** showing receipt of the gift
- Consider **family tree/relationship evidence** if surname differs

## **£450,000 Mortgage:**
- **Mortgage offer/facility letter** from the lender
- **Lender's AML confirmation** (most institutional lenders provide this)
- Evidence mortgage funds will be transmitted directly to your client account
- **Valuation report** if not already provided

## **Additional Requirements:**
- **Enhanced due diligence** may be required given the substantial gift amount
- **PEP checks** on all parties
- **Sanctions screening**
- Consider **source of wealth** questionnaire for high-value transaction
- Document your risk assessment and decision-making process

The key is demonstrating a clear audit trail from legitimate sources through to your client account, with particular scrutiny on the substantial parental gift.

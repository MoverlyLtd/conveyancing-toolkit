For your client's £750,000 purchase with three funding elements, you need the following specific evidence under current SRA requirements:

## Savings (£200,000)

**Essential Evidence:**
- **12 months** of bank/building society statements showing the accumulation pattern to £200,000 (not just a closing balance)
- Statements must show the account holder's name matching your client
- Look for regular salary credits or consistent pattern explaining the growth
- If any large lump sums appear within the 12-month period, trace each one separately to its source
- If savings are held across multiple accounts, obtain statements for all accounts

## Gift from Parents (£100,000)

**Three-part requirement - all mandatory:**
1. **Parents' photographic ID** — passport or driving licence
2. **Parents' bank statements** — 3–6 months showing they hold the £100,000+ and the source of those funds
3. **Signed gift letter** confirming:
   - Amount of gift (£100,000)
   - Relationship to buyer
   - **No obligation to repay** (confirming it's not a loan in disguise)
   - No interest in the property is expected

**Additional requirements:**
- **Proof of relationship** if not obvious from names — statutory declaration or other evidence
- **Lender notification** — check Part 2 of the UK Finance Lender's Handbook as most mortgage lenders require disclosure of gifted deposits

## Mortgage (£450,000)

**Simple requirement:**
- **Mortgage offer** from an FCA-regulated lender
- Verify the lender is FCA-regulated (check FCA Register)
- No further source-of-funds checks needed on the mortgage element itself — the lender has conducted its own CDD

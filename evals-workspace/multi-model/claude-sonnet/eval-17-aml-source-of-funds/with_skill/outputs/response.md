Based on current SRA guidance and MLR 2017, here's the specific evidence you need for each funding element:

## 1. Savings (£200,000)

**Required Evidence:**
- **12 months of bank/building society statements** showing the accumulation to £200,000 (not just final balance)
- Statements must show **account holder's name matching your client**
- Look for **regular salary credits** or consistent pattern explaining the growth
- **Trace any large lump sums** that appear within the 12-month period separately
- If funds are held across **multiple accounts**, obtain statements for all accounts
- Verify the **total available matches** the £200,000 declared

## 2. Gift from Parents (£100,000)

**Required Evidence:**
- **Parents' photographic ID** — passport or driving licence
- **Parents' bank statements** — 3-6 months showing they hold at least £100,000 and evidence of how they accumulated those funds
- **Signed gift letter** from parents confirming:
  - Amount of gift (£100,000)
  - Relationship to your client
  - **No obligation to repay** (confirming it's not a disguised loan)
  - **No interest in the property** is expected in return
- **Proof of relationship** — if not obvious from surnames, obtain statutory declaration or other evidence
- **Lender notification** — check the mortgage lender's requirements in Part 2 of the UK Finance Lender's Handbook (most lenders require disclosure of gifted deposits)

## 3. Mortgage (£450,000)

**Required Evidence:**
- **Mortgage offer** from an FCA-regulated lender
- **Verify lender is FCA-regulated** (check FCA Register)
- **No additional source of funds checks** needed on the mortgage element itself — the lender has conducted its own CDD
- The **deposit elements above** still require full verification

## Risk Assessment Considerations

Given this funding structure:
- **Standard risk profile** — typical residential purchase with conventional funding
- **No EDD triggers** apparent unless other risk factors emerge
- **Verify total funds**: £200k + £100k + £450k = £750k ✓
- **Document your assessment** of why you consider this low/standard risk

## SRA-Specific Requirements

- Apply **risk-based approach** — document your risk assessment for the matter
- **Regulation 28(12)** — if funding changes during the transaction, reassess
- **Record keeping** — retain all evidence for 5 years from end of retainer
- **MLRO involvement** if any red flags emerge during verification

## Red Flags to Watch For

- Parents reluctant to provide their bank statements or ID
- Large unexplained deposits in the savings accumulation period
- Last-minute changes to the funding structure
- Gift letter suggesting any expectation of repayment or property interest

This is a straightforward funding structure, but thoroughness in evidence gathering and documentation remains essential for SRA compliance.

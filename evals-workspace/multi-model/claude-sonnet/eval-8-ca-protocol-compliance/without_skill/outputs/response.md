The **Law Society Conveyancing Protocol** sets out clear expectations regarding responses to enquiries:

## Standard Response Times

**Pre-contract enquiries**: The seller's solicitor should respond to enquiries **within 5-7 working days** of receipt. This is considered reasonable practice under the Protocol.

**Additional enquiries**: Follow-up or supplementary enquiries should be answered within a similar timeframe.

## Protocol Requirements

The Protocol emphasizes that:

- Solicitors should respond **promptly** to all reasonable enquiries
- Where immediate answers aren't possible, an **acknowledgment** should be sent indicating when a full response will follow
- **Unreasonable delays** in responding to enquiries can constitute poor practice

## Practical Steps You Can Take

1. **Chase formally in writing** - send a letter/email noting the delay and requesting urgent response
2. **Set reasonable deadlines** - give 3-5 working days for response
3. **Escalate if necessary** - contact the other solicitor directly by phone
4. **Consider reporting** - persistent delays without justification could be reported to the SRA as poor service

## Client Communication

Keep your client informed about:
- The delays being experienced
- Steps you're taking to resolve them
- Potential impact on their moving dates

The Protocol expects all parties to work cooperatively to avoid unnecessary delays. If the other side continues to be unresponsive without valid reasons, this could constitute a breach of their professional obligations to provide a proper standard of service.

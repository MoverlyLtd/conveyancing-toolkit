As a CLC-regulated firm, you must follow specific requirements when handling client complaints. Here's what the CLC requires:

## Immediate Requirements

**Acknowledge the complaint promptly** - ideally within 2 working days, but no later than 5 working days of receipt.

**Provide your complaints procedure** - send the client a copy of your firm's complaints handling procedure if you haven't already done so.

## Investigation and Resolution

**Investigate thoroughly** - conduct a proper investigation into the complaint, gathering all relevant facts and documentation.

**Respond substantively within 8 weeks** - you must provide a full written response to the complaint within 8 weeks of receiving it, unless you've agreed an extension with the client.

**Offer appropriate remedies** - if the complaint is upheld (wholly or partially), offer suitable redress which may include:
- Apology
- Corrective action
- Fee reduction/refund
- Compensation for losses

## CLC-Specific Requirements

**Senior person involvement** - ensure a senior person in your firm (who wasn't involved in the matter complained about) reviews the complaint.

**Clear final response** - your final response must clearly state whether the complaint is upheld, rejected, or partially upheld, with full reasoning.

**LeO referral rights** - inform the client of their right to refer unresolved complaints to the Legal Ombudsman within 6 months of your final response.

## Record Keeping

**Maintain proper records** - keep detailed records of all complaints, investigations, and outcomes for CLC monitoring purposes.

**Consider systemic issues** - identify if the complaint reveals any systemic problems requiring procedural changes.

The CLC takes complaints handling seriously and monitors firms' compliance, so ensure you follow these requirements meticulously.

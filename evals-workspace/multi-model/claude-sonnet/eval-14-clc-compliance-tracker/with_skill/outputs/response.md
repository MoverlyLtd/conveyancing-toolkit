As a CLC-regulated firm, you have specific obligations under **Principle 3 (Act in the Best Interests of Clients)** regarding complaint handling:

## Immediate CLC Requirements

### Outcome 3.6: Handle complaints fairly and promptly
- **Acknowledge** the complaint promptly (ideally within 1-2 working days)
- **Investigate** the circumstances thoroughly
- **Respond** with your findings and proposed resolution
- **Document** everything in your complaints register

## Key Steps to Take

### 1. Acknowledge & Log
- Send written acknowledgment to the client
- Log in your complaints register with:
  - Date received
  - Nature of complaint
  - Assigned handler
  - Target resolution date

### 2. Investigate the Delays
- Review the transaction file for timeline evidence
- Check against your service standards from the engagement letter
- Identify what caused the delays (internal processes, third parties, etc.)
- Assess if Outcome 2.1 (competent, timely service) was breached

### 3. Respond Appropriately
- Provide honest explanation of what happened
- If delays were your fault: apologize and offer appropriate remedy
- If caused by external factors: explain clearly with evidence
- Set out any steps taken to prevent recurrence

### 4. Document for CLC Compliance
- Keep full record of complaint and resolution
- Evidence that you've delivered Outcome 3.6
- May be reviewed during CLC monitoring visits

## Potential Remedies
- Fee reduction/refund (if service fell short)
- Enhanced service going forward
- Process improvements to prevent recurrence

## Important Notes
- The client can refer to Legal Ombudsman if not satisfied with your response
- You must cooperate with any Ombudsman investigation (Principle 4)
- Serious or systemic issues may need reporting to CLC under Outcome 4.2

**Remember**: CLC regulation is outcomes-focused - you must deliver fair and prompt complaint handling, but can develop your own procedures to achieve this outcome.

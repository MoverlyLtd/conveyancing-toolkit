For restrictive covenants on freehold property in the UK, you should look in several key places:

## 1. **Land Registry Documents**
- **Title Register** - Check the Charges Register (Section C) which lists restrictive covenants affecting the property
- **Title Plan** - May show areas subject to covenants
- **Filed documents** - Original deeds and transfers that created the covenants

## 2. **Property Deeds**
- **Transfer deeds** - Particularly the deed that created the covenant
- **Conveyances** - Historical documents that may contain covenant details
- **Deed of covenant** - Separate documents specifically creating restrictions

## 3. **Legal Sources**
- **Law of Property Act 1925** - Sections 78-84 cover covenant enforcement and modification
- **Lands Tribunal/Upper Tribunal** decisions on covenant modifications
- **Case law** - Key cases like *Tulk v Moxhay* establishing the principles

## 4. **Professional Guidance**
- **Solicitor's property searches** during conveyancing
- **Title insurance policies** - May cover covenant breach risks
- **Surveyor's reports** - May identify potential covenant issues

## 5. **Local Authority**
- **Planning records** - Some covenants may relate to planning conditions
- **Conservation area** restrictions that might overlap with covenants

## Key Points to Remember:
- Covenants "run with the land" if they meet certain criteria
- Benefits and burdens may pass to successors in title
- Some covenants become unenforceable over time
- Modification or discharge is possible through the Upper Tribunal

This is a significant compliance issue that needs careful handling. Here's what you should advise:

## Immediate Concerns

**Building Regulations Compliance**: Replacement windows installed in 2018 required either:
- FENSA (or equivalent scheme) certification, OR
- Local authority building control approval

The absence of both creates a compliance gap that could affect the sale.

## Actions to Take

**1. Request Evidence from Seller**
- Ask for any paperwork from the installer (invoices, guarantees, specifications)
- Request confirmation the installer was registered with a competent person scheme
- Check if they obtained retrospective building control approval

**2. Contact the Installer**
- If still trading, they may be able to provide late certification
- Check if they were FENSA-registered at the time (even if certificate wasn't issued)
- Verify the work met Part L (energy efficiency) requirements

**3. Building Control Options**
- Apply for **regularisation certificate** from local authority
- Costs typically £300-800+ depending on local authority
- May require additional work if windows don't meet 2018 standards

**4. Consider Risks**
- **Sale delays**: Buyers' solicitors will likely raise this as an enquiry
- **Insurance implications**: Some insurers require compliance certificates
- **Future sale issues**: Problem will persist until resolved
- **Warranty concerns**: No protection without proper certification

## Practical Solutions

- Negotiate regularisation costs with seller
- Consider indemnity insurance (though this may not satisfy all buyers)
- Factor compliance costs into purchase price negotiations

**Strongly recommend** addressing this before exchange rather than leaving as a post-completion issue.

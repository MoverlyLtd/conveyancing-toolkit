Based on the UK Lenders' Handbook, there are two significant issues here that will likely cause problems:

## Lease Length (72 years remaining)

**The Lenders' Handbook generally requires:**
- Most lenders want at least 75-80 years remaining at completion
- Some require 85+ years
- The lease term should extend at least 30 years beyond the mortgage term

**With 72 years, you'll likely face:**
- Outright rejection from many mainstream lenders
- Severely limited lending options
- Requirements for immediate lease extension before completion
- Higher interest rates from specialist lenders who will accept shorter leases

## Loft Conversion Without Building Regulations

**The Lenders' Handbook typically requires:**
- Building Regulations approval for structural alterations
- Retrospective Building Regulations certificates where work was done without approval
- Evidence that work complies with Building Regulations

**For the loft conversion, lenders will usually insist on:**
1. **Regularisation Certificate** from Building Control confirming the work meets current standards
2. **Structural engineer's report** if Building Control cannot certify without invasive investigation
3. **Indemnity insurance** may be acceptable to some lenders, but many now reject this for structural work

## Recommendations:

1. **Lease extension** - Negotiate with the seller to extend before completion, or factor costs into your offer
2. **Building Regulations** - Obtain quotes for regularisation certificate or structural report
3. **Specialist legal advice** - These are complex issues requiring expert conveyancing input
4. **Mortgage broker consultation** - Find lenders who'll accept both issues before proceeding

Both issues combined make this a high-risk transaction that will significantly limit your financing options.

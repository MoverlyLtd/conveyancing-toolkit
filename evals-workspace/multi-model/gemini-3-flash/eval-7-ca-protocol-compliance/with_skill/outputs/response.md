Under the **Conveyancing Association (CA) Protocol (5th Edition)**, the handling of a planning permission issue depends on the age of the work and whether it was a "change of use" or a physical alteration like an extension.

Based on the extension being completed in 2021 (making it approximately 3 years old), here is how the matter should be handled:

### 1. Planning Permission Action (Ref: CA Protocol 4.0)
Since the work was completed more than 12 months ago but less than 4 years ago, the protocol recommends:
*   **Obtaining indemnity insurance:** This is the standard requirement for works in this specific age bracket to protect against potential enforcement action.
*   **Verification:** You should confirm that the work was not "concealed" to avoid the planning process, as this would restart the enforcement clock.

### 2. Disclosures and Safety (Ref: CA Protocol 4.0)
In addition to the insurance, the protocol requires the following steps:
*   **Advise the Client and Valuer:** You must formally notify both the client and the mortgage valuer that planning permission was not obtained.
*   **Structural Confirmation:** The valuer or a surveyor should confirm that the extension is structurally sound.

### 3. Comparison with Other Timelines
*   If the extension had been older than **4 years**, no further action would typically be required (provided it wasn't a change of use).
*   If it were a **change of use** (e.g., converting a garage into a separate dwelling), the enforcement period is 10 years rather than 4.

**Summary for your client:** Because the extension is relatively recent (less than 4 years old), it is still within the period where the local authority could theoretically take enforcement action. Therefore, the seller should provide an indemnity insurance policy at their expense, and your surveyor must confirm the build quality is acceptable.

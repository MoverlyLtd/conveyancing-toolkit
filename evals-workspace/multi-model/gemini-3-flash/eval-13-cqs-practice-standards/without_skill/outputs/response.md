When a new fee earner joins a firm accredited by the Law Society’s Conveyancing Quality Scheme (CQS), the firm must adhere to specific onboarding requirements outlined in the **CQS Core Practice Management Standards (CPMS)** and the **CQS Scheme Rules**.

Failure to document these steps can result in non-compliance during a CQS assessment or the loss of accreditation. Below are the detailed requirements:

---

### 1. Personnel Vetting and Identity Verification (CPMS 1.2)
The firm must demonstrate that it has verified the identity and background of the new fee earner.
*   **Identity Check:** You must perform a formal identity check (e.g., passport/driving licence and proof of address).
*   **Professional Standing:** You must verify their professional qualifications and check the SRA (Solicitors Regulation Authority) or CLC (Council for Licensed Conveyancers) roll to ensure there are no disciplinary findings or restrictions on practice.
*   **DBS Checks:** CQS requires **Basic Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS)** checks for all relevant staff. This must be conducted upon employment and then at least every three years.
*   **References:** You must obtain and document satisfactory references covering at least the last three years of employment.

### 2. Mandatory CQS Training
The Law Society mandates specific training that must be completed through the Law Society’s Professional Development Centre.
*   **Induction Training:** All new fee earners must complete the **CQS Induction Training** (which usually covers Financial Crime and the Conveyancing Protocol).
*   **Timeframe:** Under the Scheme Rules, new joiners should complete this training within **six months** of joining the firm.
*   **Training Records:** The firm’s Senior Responsible Officer (SRO) must ensure the certificates of completion are downloaded and stored in a central training log for audit purposes.

### 3. Competency and Supervision (CPMS 4.3 & 4.4)
The firm must ensure the fee earner is competent to handle the specific tasks assigned to them.
*   **Supervision Structure:** You must formally document who will supervise the new fee earner. This must be a person with appropriate seniority (usually the SRO or a Head of Department).
*   **Initial Review:** It is best practice (and often expected by assessors) to have an increased frequency of file reviews for a new joiner during their probationary period to ensure they are following the firm’s CQS-compliant procedures.

### 4. Induction to the Conveyancing Protocol
CQS firms are required to follow the **Law Society Conveyancing Protocol**. The onboarding process must ensure the fee earner:
*   Has access to the most recent version of the Protocol (currently the 2011 version, updated by subsequent editions).
*   Understands that the Protocol is the default "standard" for the firm's transactions.
*   Is trained on the firm’s specific requirements for **Stage 1 (Instructions)** through to **Stage 6 (Post-completion)**.

### 5. Policy Sign-off (Risk Management)
The fee earner must be given the firm's "Office Manual" or "Policy Folder." Under CPMS, they must specifically read and sign off that they understand the following:
*   **Anti-Money Laundering (AML) & Counter-Terrorist Financing:** The firm’s specific risk assessment and procedures for Source of Funds (SoF) and Source of Wealth (SoW).
*   **Mortgage Fraud:** Procedures for identifying and reporting potential mortgage fraud (CPMS 3.3).
*   **Cyber Security:** Awareness of the firm's policy on property fraud (e.g., "Friday afternoon fraud") and secure communication of bank details.
*   **SDLT/LTT Procedures:** The firm’s internal process for calculating and filing tax returns (CPMS 5.12).
*   **Acting for Lenders:** Ensuring they understand the requirement to follow the *UK Finance Mortgage Lenders’ Handbook* or *BSA Instructions*.

### 6. Updating the Senior Responsible Officer (SRO)
The SRO is the person at your firm accountable to the Law Society for CQS compliance.
*   **Internal Register:** The SRO must update the firm’s internal list of "Relevant Persons" covered by the CQS accreditation.
*   **Law Society Notification:** While you do not always need to notify the Law Society the moment a fee earner joins (unless they are the SRO or a Deputy SRO), you must include their details in the next **CQS Annual Registration/Renewal (AR1)**.

### 7. Complaints and Feedback (CPMS 6.2)
The fee earner must be trained on the firm’s internal complaints procedure. They must know:
*   How to identify a complaint.
*   Who the designated Complaints Handling Partner is.
*   The timescales for responding under the SRA Transparency Rules and CQS standards.

### Summary Checklist for the HR/Compliance File:
1.  [ ] Verified ID and Professional Credentials.
2.  [ ] Basic DBS check initiated/received.
3.  [ ] References for the last 3 years on file.
4.  [ ] Signed acknowledgement of AML, Mortgage Fraud, and Cyber policies.
5.  [ ] Law Society CQS Induction modules booked/completed.
6.  [ ] Supervisor formally assigned.
7.  [ ] Training log updated.

**Pro-tip:** Create a specific "CQS Onboarding Folder" for this individual. If an assessor visits, being able to produce a single folder containing the DBS check, the signed policy acknowledgments, and the CQS training certificates will demonstrate a high level of compliance.

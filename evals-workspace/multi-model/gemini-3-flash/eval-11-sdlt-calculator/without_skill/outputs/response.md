In the UK (specifically England and Northern Ireland), Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) is calculated based on specific thresholds. As a first-time buyer purchasing a property for **£510,000**, you qualify for **First-Time Buyers’ Relief**, provided you meet the eligibility criteria.

Here is the detailed breakdown of what you would pay:

### 1. The Calculation
Under current rules (effective until March 31, 2025), first-time buyers pay:
*   **0%** on the first **£425,000**.
*   **5%** on the portion from **£425,001 to £625,000**.

**The Math for a £510,000 Purchase:**
*   First £425,000: £0
*   Remaining amount (£510,000 - £425,000 = £85,000): 5% of £85,000 = **£4,250**

**Total SDLT Payable: £4,250**

### 2. Eligibility Criteria
To pay this amount, you must satisfy the following HMRC requirements:
*   **Definition of a First-Time Buyer:** You (and anyone else you are buying with) must have never owned a residential property (or a share of one) anywhere in the world. This includes inherited property or property held in a trust.
*   **Main Residence:** You must intend to live in the property as your only or main residence. You cannot claim this relief if you intend to buy it as a Buy-to-Let.
*   **Price Cap:** The relief only applies if the purchase price is **£625,000 or less**. If the flat were £625,001, you would lose the relief entirely and pay standard rates (which, on £625,001, would be £18,750).

Preparing for a Conveyancing Quality Scheme (CQS) renewal audit requires demonstrating that your firm consistently follows the Law Society’s Core Practice Management Standards (CPMS). Assessors generally focus on how your policies translate into daily practice.

Based on the CQS Practice Standards, here are the key areas the assessor will check and the evidence you should have ready:

### 1. Governance and Accountability
The assessor wants to see that CQS compliance is managed from the top down.
*   **What they check:** The role of your Senior Responsible Officer (SRO) and how protocol compliance is monitored.
*   **Evidence needed:**
    *   Documentation naming your SRO and their specific responsibilities.
    *   Written procedures showing how the firm adopts and monitors the Law Society Protocol.
    *   Minutes or schedules of regular compliance reviews.

### 2. Risk Management (High Priority)
This is one of the most heavily scrutinised areas. You must prove you are mitigating financial and legal risks.
*   **What they check:** AML procedures, source of funds verification, and conflict checking.
*   **Evidence needed:**
    *   Current AML policy and firm-wide risk assessment.
    *   Records of ID verification and Source of Wealth/Funds checks for clients.
    *   A clear conflict of interest policy and a system/register for checks.
    *   Your current Professional Indemnity Insurance (PII) certificate.

### 3. Information Security and Cyber Awareness
Given the rise in "Friday afternoon fraud," assessors look for robust digital protections.
*   **What they check:** How you protect client data and verify bank details.
*   **Evidence needed:**
    *   A formal Cyber Security Policy and Business Continuity Plan.
    *   Records of staff training on phishing and "push payment" fraud.
    *   Specific written procedures for verifying bank details (especially before the transfer of completion funds).
    *   Evidence of secure communication methods (e.g., encrypted email or client portals).

### 4. Training and Competency
*   **What they check:** Whether all staff are up to date with mandatory CQS training and general conveyancing law.
*   **Evidence needed:**
    *   Training certificates for all mandatory CQS modules.
    *   Individual CPD (Continuing Professional Development) plans for all conveyancing staff.
    *   Induction checklists for any new hires.

### 5. Client Care and Complaints
*   **What they check:** How clearly you communicate costs and how you handle dissatisfaction.
*   **Evidence needed:**
    *   Template engagement letters/terms of business showing clear cost breakdowns.
    *   A written complaints procedure that is visible to clients.
    *   A central complaints register showing how issues were investigated, resolved, and what lessons the firm learned.

### 6. File and Case Management
Assessors will often perform "file reviews" to see if your paperwork matches your policies.
*   **What they check:** Management of key dates, undertakings, and post-completion tasks.
*   **Evidence needed:**
    *   An active **Undertakings Register** (with tracking of discharges).
    *   Evidence of regular internal file audits (audit logs and findings).
    *   Standardised checklists for exchange, completion, and post-completion (SDLT/Land Registry).

### Preparation Checklist for Next Month:
1.  **Conduct a Gap Analysis:** Review the areas above and identify any missing documents or expired training.
2.  **Audit Your Files:** Ensure that files (especially recent ones) have documented ID checks and signed terms of engagement.
3.  **Check Training Dates:** Ensure no one has missed the deadline for their mandatory Law Society CQS training.
4.  **Verify Versions:** Ensure all staff are using the most current Law Society property forms and standard conditions of sale.

*Note: Requirements are updated periodically. It is recommended to verify the latest standards directly via the Law Society website.*

The Law Society’s Conveyancing Quality Scheme (CQS) audit process has become significantly more rigorous since the update to the **Core Practice Management Standards (CPMS)** in February 2022. The assessor is looking for evidence that your firm doesn't just have a manual on a shelf, but that the standards are embedded in your daily workflow.

Here is a detailed breakdown of the key areas the assessor will check and the evidence you must have ready.

---

### 1. Risk Management (The "Heavyweight" Section)
This is where most firms fail. The assessor will focus on how you identify and mitigate risks specifically related to conveyancing.

*   **Firm-Wide Risk Assessment (FWRA):** You must show a written FWRA that complies with the Money Laundering Regulations 2017. It must be specific to your firm’s size, location, and client base.
*   **Source of Funds (SoF) & Source of Wealth (SoW):** The assessor will look for a clear policy *and* evidence in files that you have scrutinized where money came from. 
    *   *Evidence needed:* Bank statements in files, a signed narrative from the client explaining the accumulation of wealth, and evidence that the fee earner actually "minded" the documents (e.g., a file note stating: "Reviewed bank statements; salary matches property value expectations").
*   **AML Training:** 
    *   *Evidence needed:* Training logs for all staff (including support staff) and certificates from the mandatory CQS training modules.

### 2. File Management and Supervision
The assessor will perform "file "spot checks." They aren't looking for legal advice quality, but for procedural compliance.

*   **File Reviews:** This is a critical CPMS requirement. You must demonstrate that files are reviewed regularly.
    *   *Evidence needed:* A central log of file reviews. The reviews must show that if an error was found, corrective action was taken. You should aim for at least one review per fee earner per month.
*   **Key Dates:** You must have a robust system for monitoring deadlines (SDLT, Land Registry requisitions, lease extensions).
    *   *Evidence needed:* Demonstrate your case management system’s alerting function or a centralized "master diary."
*   **Standardized Document Control:**
    *   *Evidence needed:* Evidence that you are using the most recent versions of Law Society Transnational Forms (TA6, TA7, TA10).

### 3. Client Care
The assessor wants to see that the client is fully informed about costs, service, and risks.

*   **Initial Instruction Letters:**
    *   *Evidence needed:* Client Care Letters must clearly state who is doing the work, their supervisor, a breakdown of fees and disbursements, and the complaints procedure.
*   **SDLT Advice:** This is a high-risk area.
    *   *Evidence needed:* Evidence in the file that the SDLT calculation was explained to the client, particularly regarding surcharges (Second Home/Higher Rates) or reliefs (First Time Buyers).
*   **Property Alerts:** 
    *   *Evidence needed:* Proof that you have advised all clients about the HM Land Registry Property Alert Service to mitigate fraud.

### 4. Financial Management and Fraud Prevention
With the rise in "Friday afternoon fraud," the assessor will look at how you protect client money.

*   **Bank Account Verification:**
    *   *Evidence needed:* Your written procedure for verifying the bank details of the "other side’s" solicitor (using tools like Lawyer Checker or the Law Society’s Find a Solicitor).
*   **Payment Procedures:**
    *   *Evidence needed:* Records showing that your firm never sends bank details via unencrypted email, or if you do, that you have a secondary verification process (like a phone call) documented.
*   **PI Insurance:**
    *   *Evidence needed:* Your current Professional Indemnity Insurance certificate and a record of any notifications made to the broker.

### 5. People Management & Training
The assessor will check that your team is competent and vetted.

*   **Induction and Performance:**
    *   *Evidence needed:* Documented induction checklists for new starters and annual appraisal records for all conveyancing staff.
*   **CQS Mandatory Training:**
    *   *Evidence needed:* Ensure all relevant staff have completed the Law Society’s mandatory CQS updates for the current year.

### 6. Information Management and Cyber Security
CQS now aligns closely with Cyber Essentials standards.

*   **Data Protection:**
    *   *Evidence needed:* An up-to-date Privacy Policy and evidence that staff have received GDPR/Data Protection training.
*   **Disaster Recovery:**
    *   *Evidence needed:* A Business Continuity Plan that has been **tested** within the last 12 months. The assessor will ask: "When did you last test your backups, and what was the result?"

---

### Checklist for Audit Day:

1.  **The "CQS Folder":** Have a digital or physical folder ready containing all your core policies (AML, Equality & Diversity, Business Continuity, Information Security, etc.).
2.  **The File List:** Have a list of all "Live" and "Closed" (within the last 6 months) conveyancing files ready. The assessor will pick 5–10 files at random for a deep dive.
3.  **The Audit Trail:** Ensure every file has a consistent structure. If a file is missing a signed Terms of Business or an ID check, it’s an automatic "non-compliance."
4.  **Staff Interviews:** Warn your staff. The assessor may ask a junior fee earner: *"What do you do if you suspect a client is using tainted funds?"* or *"How do you verify a change in bank details from another firm?"* Their answers must match your written policies.

**Top Tip:** The most common reason for a CQS "Required Action" is the **File Review**. If you haven't been doing them consistently, start now. Retrospectively doing 50 reviews the week before the audit looks suspicious; it is better to show a consistent, monthly process.

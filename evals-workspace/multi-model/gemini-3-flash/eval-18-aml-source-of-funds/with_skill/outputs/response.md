When dealing with overseas funds, particularly from a major financial hub like Dubai (UAE), you must adopt a **risk-based approach** rather than a tick-box exercise. Because the funds are originating outside the UK, **Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)** is the default standard.

Under **Regulation 28(12)**, you have an obligation to conduct ongoing monitoring throughout the transaction. If the funding structure or the source jurisdiction changes at any point, you must re-assess the risk immediately.

### 1. Mandatory Overseas Evidence
Beyond standard source of funds (SOF) checks, you must obtain the following for funds arriving from Dubai:

*   **Source Country Risk Assessment (FATF):** You must document a risk assessment of the UAE. While the UAE was recently removed from the FATF "grey list," it remains a high-scrutiny jurisdiction for UK conveyancing due to its status as a global financial hub and its historical risk profile. Check against the FATF high-risk/increased-monitoring lists and the HM Treasury sanctions list.
*   **SWIFT / Wire Transfer Evidence:** Obtain the full SWIFT MT103 message or wire transfer confirmation. This must show the sending bank, the account holder's name (matching your client), the receiving bank, and the exact amount and currency.
*   **Currency Conversion Evidence:** Request the FX contract note or bank confirmation showing the conversion from UAE Dirham (AED) to British Pounds (GBP), including the rate and the final amount.
*   **Trace Intermediary Accounts:** If the funds have moved from a Dubai business account to a personal account, or through a third-party currency exchange provider, you must trace every "hop" in the chain with corresponding statements.

### 2. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) & Source of Wealth
Because the funds are overseas, you should move beyond Source of Funds to **Source of Wealth (SOW)**. You need to understand how the client accumulated their total wealth, not just the specific money for this purchase.

*   **Employment/Business in Dubai:** If the funds are from earnings in Dubai, request several months of payslips or a letter from the employer. For business owners, request audited accounts or tax returns (though UAE tax requirements differ, financial records are still required).
*   **Property Sale in Dubai:** If the funds come from a local property sale, obtain the sale contract and a completion statement from the Dubai Land Department (DLD) or the legal professional who handled the sale.

### 3. Requirements if any funds are a Gift
If any portion of the Dubai funds is being provided by a third party (e.g., a family member in the UAE), you **must** obtain the following three items without exception:
1.  **Giftor's Photographic ID:** A high-quality copy of their passport.
2.  **Giftor's Bank Statements:** 3–6 months of statements showing the funds held in their account and the original source of those funds.
3.  **Signed Gift Letter:** A letter confirming the amount, the relationship, that it is a non-repayable gift, and that they waive any interest in the property.

### 4. Regulatory & Reporting Obligations

*   **SAR Reporting to NCA:** You are part of the UK’s AML compliance framework. If you have any suspicion (or reasonable grounds for suspicion) that the funds are the proceeds of crime, you must report this to the **National Crime Agency (NCA)** via a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR). You must not "tip off" the client that a report has been made.
*   **DAML Process:** If you have a suspicion but need to proceed with the transaction, you must apply for a **Defence Against Money Laundering (DAML)**. The NCA has **7 working days** to respond. If they refuse, a **31-day moratorium** period begins during which you cannot move the funds.
*   **5-Year Record-Keeping:** You must retain all CDD and EDD records, including your risk assessments and all overseas evidence, for a minimum of **5 years** from the end of the business relationship or the completion of the transaction.

### Summary Checklist for Dubai Funds
*   [ ] FATF/HMT Country Risk Assessment documented.
*   [ ] 12 months of statements showing accumulation.
*   [ ] SWIFT MT103/Wire transfer confirmation.
*   [ ] FX conversion note (AED to GBP).
*   [ ] Source of Wealth evidenced (how they became wealthy in Dubai).
*   [ ] (If Gift) Giftor ID, statements, and signed letter obtained.
*   [ ] Matter risk assessment updated and signed off.

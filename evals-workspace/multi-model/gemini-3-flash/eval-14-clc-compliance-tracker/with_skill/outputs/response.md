As a CLC-regulated firm, your obligations regarding complaints are centered on **Principle 3 (Act in the Best Interests of Clients)** and specifically **Outcome 3.6**, which requires you to handle complaints fairly and promptly.

Under the CLC Code of Conduct (revised 1 January 2025) and the subsidiary requirements, you should follow these steps:

### 1. Follow Your Written Procedure
Every CLC firm must have a written complaints procedure. You must:
*   Acknowledge the complaint in writing promptly (typically within 7 days).
*   Provide the client with a copy of your complaints procedure if they don't already have it.
*   Ensure the complaint is handled by someone of sufficient seniority who was not directly involved in the matter (where possible).

### 2. Investigation and Response
The CLC focuses on "outcomes," meaning you have the flexibility to resolve the issue as you see fit, provided it is fair.
*   **Investigate the delay:** Review the transaction file to see if the delay was within your control or caused by third parties.
*   **Formal Response:** You should provide a full written response within **28 days**. This response should address each point raised and, if the complaint is justified, explain what you will do to remedy it (e.g., an apology, a reduction in fees, or accelerating the remaining work).

### 3. Record Keeping
To comply with CLC audit requirements, you must record this in your **Complaints Register**. You will need to document:
*   The nature of the complaint (service delay).
*   The steps taken to investigate.
*   The final outcome and any redress offered.
*   The time taken to resolve the matter.

### 4. Signposting (Mandatory)
If you cannot resolve the complaint to the client's satisfaction, you are regulatorily required to inform them in writing of their right to escalate the matter. You must provide contact details for:
*   **The Legal Ombudsman (LeO):** For issues regarding service quality (like delays).
*   **The Council of Licensed Conveyancers (CLC):** If the complaint relates to a breach of the CLC Code of Conduct or ethical principles.

### 5. Managing the "Delay" Issue
Since the complaint is specifically about delays, the CLC expects you to demonstrate compliance with **Principle 2 (High Standards of Work)**:
*   **Outcome 2.1:** Provide a timely and diligent service.
*   **Action:** Check your communication logs. If the delay was unavoidable, did you keep the client informed? Under **Outcome 3.3**, you have a duty to keep clients informed of progress; a failure to communicate the *reason* for a delay is often viewed as seriously as the delay itself.

**Note for CLC Firms:** Unlike SRA-regulated firms who might look to the Law Society Protocol, your benchmark is the **CLC Transaction Files Code**. Ensure your file notes clearly explain the timeline of the transaction to defend against any suggestion of professional negligence or misconduct.

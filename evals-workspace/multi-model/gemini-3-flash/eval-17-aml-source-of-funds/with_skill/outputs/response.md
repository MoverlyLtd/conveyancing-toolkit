Under current SRA guidance and the Legal Sector Affinity Group (LSAG) AML guidance, you must apply a **risk-based approach**. This means you must evidence the entire "audit trail" of the funds, not just their current location.

For a £750,000 purchase with these specific elements, you should request the following evidence:

### 1. Savings (£200,000)
The goal is to prove the **accumulation** of these funds, not just a closing balance.
*   **12 months of bank statements:** You need to see the pattern of growth. Look for regular salary credits or consistent savings transfers.
*   **Name Match:** Confirm the account holder’s name matches your client exactly.
*   **Lump Sum Tracing:** If any large or unusual deposits appear within those 12 months (e.g., a £20,000 transfer from an unknown account), you must "trace back" and request separate evidence for that specific sum.
*   **Multiple Accounts:** If the money was moved between accounts (e.g., from a current account to an ISA), you need statements for both to show the movement.

### 2. Gift from Parents (£100,000)
Because this is a third-party contribution, you must perform due diligence on the parents (the "giftors").
*   **Giftor Identification:** Photographic ID (Passport or Driving Licence) for both parents.
*   **Giftor's Bank Statements:** 3–6 months of statements from the parents showing they held the £100,000 and the source of that money (e.g., their own savings or a pension lump sum).
*   **Signed Gift Letter:** A formal letter signed by the parents confirming:
    *   The exact amount of the gift.
    *   Their relationship to the buyer.
    *   That the money is an unconditional gift with **no obligation to repay**.
    *   That they will have **no legal or equitable interest** in the property.
*   **Lender Notification:** Since there is a mortgage, you must disclose this gift to the lender (per Part 2 of the UK Finance Mortgage Lender’s Handbook).

### 3. Mortgage (£450,000)
*   **Mortgage Offer:** Obtain the formal offer from an FCA-regulated lender.
*   **Regulatory Check:** Verify the lender is on the FCA Register (though standard high-street lenders are generally considered low risk).
*   *Note: Under LSAG guidance, you do not need to conduct a source of funds check on the mortgage funds themselves, as the lender has conducted its own CDD. Your focus remains on the deposit element.*

### SRA Compliance & Documentation Requirements
To meet SRA Standards and Regulations, you must also:
*   **Matter Risk Assessment:** Complete and document a written risk assessment for this specific transaction. A £750k purchase is a significant transaction; document why you are satisfied with the evidence provided.
*   **Ongoing Monitoring (Reg 28(12)):** If the funding structure changes (e.g., the gift increases or savings decrease), you must re-verify the new source.
*   **Record Keeping:** Keep all AML evidence and your written reasoning for **5 years** from the date the business relationship ends.
*   **Red Flags:** Watch for any "round-sum" transfers that don't match the declared amounts or any reluctance from the parents to provide their statements—these are high-risk red flags that may require escalation to your MLRO.

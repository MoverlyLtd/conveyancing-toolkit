To comply with the Money Laundering Regulations 2017 and SRA guidance, you must adopt a **risk-based approach** rather than a "tick-box" exercise. You are required to evidence every element of the funding, as the risk profile of the transaction is determined by the combination of all sources.

For a £750,000 purchase with the breakdown provided, you must request the following evidence:

### 1. Savings (£200,000)
*   **12 months of bank/building society statements:** You must see the accumulation pattern, not just a closing balance. This confirms the funds grew through legitimate means (e.g., regular salary).
*   **Name Match:** Statements must clearly show the account holder’s name matches your client.
*   **Lump Sum Tracing:** If any large, unusual credits appear within the 12-month period, you must trace the source of those specific sums separately (e.g., a bonus, inheritance, or sale of assets).

### 2. Gift from Parents (£100,000)
Checks on the giftors are **separate** from the buyer checks. You must obtain:
*   **Giftors' Photographic ID:** A copy of the parents’ passports or driving licences.
*   **Giftors' Bank Statements:** 3–6 months of statements showing they held the funds and how they originated (source of wealth).
*   **Signed Gift Letter:** This must confirm:
    *   The specific amount of the gift (£100,000).
    *   The relationship (parents to child).
    *   **No obligation to repay** (it is not a disguised loan).
    *   No interest in the property is expected or granted.
*   **Lender Notification:** You must disclose this gift to the mortgage lender, as required by Part 2 of the UK Finance Mortgage Lenders' Handbook.

### 3. Mortgage (£450,000)
*   **Mortgage Offer:** Obtain a copy of the formal offer from an FCA-regulated lender.
*   **Scope:** While the lender conducts their own CDD, you are still responsible for verifying the remaining £300,000 deposit and ensuring the overall transaction is not suspicious.

### Mandatory Compliance Framework

**Record-Keeping**
Under the 5-year record-keeping obligation, you must retain all CDD and source of funds evidence for **5 years** from the end of the business relationship or the date of the transaction.

**Ongoing Monitoring (Regulation 28(12))**
AML is not a one-time check at onboarding. You must conduct **ongoing monitoring** throughout the retainer. If the funding structure changes (e.g., the gift amount increases or savings are moved between accounts), you must re-assess and update your evidence.

**Overseas Funds (Contextual Requirement)**
If any part of these funds (including the parents' gift) originates from outside the UK, you must also obtain:
*   **SWIFT/wire transfer evidence** showing the full audit trail.
*   **Source country risk assessment** (referencing FATF high-risk jurisdictions).
*   **Currency conversion evidence** (FX contract notes or bank confirmations).

**Reporting Obligations (SARs & DAML)**
*   **SAR Reporting:** You have a legal obligation under POCA 2002 to report any suspicion of money laundering to the **National Crime Agency (NCA)** via a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR). 
*   **DAML:** If you suspect money laundering but wish to proceed with the transaction, you must apply for a **Defence Against Money Laundering (DAML)**. The NCA has 7 working days to respond. If they refuse, a 31-day moratorium period applies during which you cannot move the funds.
*   **Tipping Off:** You must never inform the client that a SAR has been filed, as "tipping off" is a criminal offence.

### Risk-Based Assessment
The depth of your enquiry must be proportionate to the risk. If the £100,000 gift is disproportionate to the parents' known wealth, or if the £200,000 savings cannot be explained by the client's occupation, you must escalate to **Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)**. This would involve a deeper "Source of Wealth" check to understand how the parties built their overall financial position over time.

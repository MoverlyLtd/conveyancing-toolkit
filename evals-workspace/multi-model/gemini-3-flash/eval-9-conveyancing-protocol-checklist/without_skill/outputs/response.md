In UK conveyancing (England and Wales), the **Law Society Conveyancing Protocol** (currently the 6th Edition) sets out the standard procedure that solicitors and licensed conveyancers must follow. At the point of exchange, the Protocol mandates that specific hurdles must have been cleared to ensure the transaction is legally sound and transparent.

If you are about to exchange, here is a detailed checklist of what you should verify against the Protocol requirements:

---

### 1. The Financial Foundations
Before exchange, the Protocol requires that all financial arrangements are "set in stone."
*   **The Deposit:** Ensure the buyer’s solicitor holds the cleared funds for the deposit (usually 10% of the purchase price). Under the Protocol, the solicitor must confirm they have "cleared funds" before they can telephone the other side to exchange.
*   **Mortgage Offer:** If you are the buyer, the Protocol dictates that your solicitor must have a written, formal mortgage offer in hand and that all "pre-exchange" conditions (e.g., proof of life insurance or paying off a credit card) have been met.
*   **Chain Check:** If there is a chain, the Protocol requires solicitors to communicate to ensure the financial arrangements are ready all the way up and down the line.

### 2. Disclosure and Documentation (The TA Forms)
The Protocol relies heavily on the **TransAction** forms. You must check that:
*   **TA6 (Property Information Form):** Has the seller updated any information? If a fence blew down or a dispute with a neighbor started *after* the form was filled out, the Protocol requires the seller to disclose this before exchange.
*   **TA10 (Fixtures and Contents):** Review this one last time. This form becomes a legally binding part of the contract at exchange. If the seller is now taking the curtains they previously said they’d leave, the contract must reflect this.
*   **Title Plan:** Confirm the red line on the Land Registry plan matches your physical understanding of the boundaries. The Protocol emphasizes that "what you see is what you get" legally.

### 3. The Contract and Completion Date
You cannot exchange under the Protocol without a fixed **Completion Date**.
*   **The Date:** Ensure the date is written into the contract. Under the Protocol, once you exchange, this date is legally binding. Failure to move on this date results in a breach of contract and financial penalties.
*   **Signed Contracts:** Ensure both parties have signed identical versions of the contract. The Protocol allows for "Formula B" exchange (the most common), where each solicitor holds their client's signed contract and they exchange over the phone, promising to post them to each other immediately.

### 4. Insurance (The Risk Shift)
One of the most critical points of the Protocol is the **transfer of risk**.
*   **Buyer’s Responsibility:** In most standard contracts (incorporating the Standard Conditions of Sale), the risk passes to the buyer **at the moment of exchange**. 
*   **Check:** If you are the buyer, you must have your buildings insurance policy ready to go "on risk" the moment exchange happens. The Protocol expects the solicitor to advise the client of this responsibility.

### 5. Enquiries and Satisfactory Answers
The Protocol states that "Exchange should not take place until all material enquiries have been answered."
*   **Outstanding Issues:** If there was a missing FENSA certificate for windows or an unsigned planning permission, ensure your solicitor has received a "satisfactory" response. 
*   **The "Protocol Spirit":** The Law Society discourages "unnecessary" enquiries, but it insists that any "subsisting" (remaining) issues must be resolved or expressly waived by the buyer before the telephone call to exchange occurs.

### 6. The "Formula" for Exchange
Solicitors usually use **Law Society Formula B** to exchange. Before this happens, check:
*   **Authority to Exchange:** Your solicitor *must* have your express authority to exchange. This is often a recorded phone call or a signed authority form.
*   **The Chain:** If you are in a chain, the Protocol requires that the solicitors confirm that everyone is ready. If one person at the bottom isn't ready, the whole chain stays "on hold."

### 7. Vacant Possession
Check the contract regarding possession. The Protocol assumes **Vacant Possession** unless otherwise stated (e.g., if a tenant is staying).
*   If the seller is still in the house, the Protocol implies they must be out by a specific time on completion day (usually 1:00 PM or 2:00 PM). Ensure you are happy with the logistics of this before committing.

### Summary Checklist for the Client:
1.  **Is the 10% deposit in the solicitor's bank account?**
2.  **Is the mortgage offer finalized and the "Report on Title" signed?**
3.  **Is the completion date 100% agreed upon by all parties?**
4.  **Is the buildings insurance ready to start today?**
5.  **Have you reviewed the TA6 and TA10 forms one last time?**
6.  **Have you given your solicitor "Express Authority" to exchange?**

**Warning:** Once the solicitor confirms exchange via the Law Society Formula over the phone, you are legally bound. If you pull out after this point, you will likely lose your 10% deposit and could be sued for further damages.
